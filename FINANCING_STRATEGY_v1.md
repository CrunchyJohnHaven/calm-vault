## The question, framed precisely

The Same As You Network needs operational capital. The doctrine constraints are non-negotiable:

- **No traditional equity round** (per DESTROY_THE_RING.md §IX)
- **No founder skim from merch revenue** (founder takes 0% of Money Python margin)
- **No fealty** to any specific lender, lab, administration, or investor
- **Open-source IP** (Apache 2.0 / CC BY 4.0) — meaning conventional IP collateralization yields ~$0 (anyone can fork the code; lenders can't seize what's already in the commons)

The question is: **what financing structures can capitalize the Network's compute, services, and infrastructure without breaking any of the four doctrine constraints?**

Within that frame, there are six accessible categories:

1. **No-collateral compute credits** (AWS / GCP / Anthropic / Cloudflare startup programs)
2. **Revenue-based financing** (Lighter Capital / Capchase / Shopify Capital — once Money Python flows)
3. **Personal / business credit** (business credit cards, SBA loans, veteran-specific programs)
4. **IP-backed loans against the AAO-Certified mark** (the trademark is the ONE excludable asset)
5. **Grants and donations** (AI safety grants, Open Philanthropy, GitHub Sponsors, OpenCollective)
6. **Pre-order / crowdfunding** (Money Python Founder's Series, Kickstarter-style)

The WDMM evaluation below ranks 10 specific strategies — 5 Dark Musk, 5 Rick Sanchez — by EV.

### DM-F5: AAO-Certified trademark-backed loan + revenue-stream-backed financing

**The move.** The AAO-Certified mark is the ONE excludable IP asset in the doctrine stack. Everything else is Apache 2.0 / CC BY 4.0 — open-source, not collateralizable. But trademarks ARE collateralizable. Once registered (USPTO process: ~6-12 months, ~$350 filing fees per class), the mark can be:

- Collateralized via specialty IP-backed lenders (Aon IP Solutions, Brookline Capital, etc.) at low LTV (10-25%)
- Licensed for fee-based use by certifying entities (we explicitly DON'T do this in the doctrine — but the option exists)

Separately, once Money Python pre-order revenue starts flowing (target: $5K-$25K in first 30 days), revenue-stream-backed financing becomes accessible:

- **Capchase** — non-equity revenue forwarding
- **Pipe** — revenue securitization
- **Stripe Capital** — Stripe-platform-specific lending; available after 3-6 months of Stripe history

**Value if it works.** Trademark-backed loan against AAO-Certified at modest LTV: $5K-$25K against ~$100K-$500K trademark valuation (highly speculative valuation). Revenue-stream financing: 20-60% of monthly recurring revenue advance ratio.

**Probability of working.** Trademark-backed before registration completes: ~5%. After registration: ~30% at meaningful amounts. Revenue-stream financing requires actual revenue: ~70% probability of qualifying once $5K+ monthly revenue exists.

**EV.** ($10K × 0.30 trademark-loan) + ($30K × 0.70 × 0.5 utilization revenue-stream) ≈ **$13K central estimate over 12-month horizon.**

**Cost.** ~$500 trademark registration (USPTO + lawyer review). ~$50-100 Reedsy Legal scan of revenue-stream financing terms.

**Risk to doctrine.** Trademark licensing for fee would break the "self-certifying, permissionless" line — DO NOT pursue. Trademark-backed loans are fine if the loan terms don't require we monetize the mark in ways that contradict the doctrine.
