FAQ

What is technosocialism? Collective ownership of the tools. Individual ownership of the kill. Read it in full at /technosocialism.

Are you a labor marketplace? No. We are a placement firm. You don't bid for tasks. You apply once, take a 30-minute skills test, get matched to a project inside an AAO, and the value you build is yours.

What's the 20% for? Hosting, AI API credits, payment rails, the cryptographic accountability layer, the brand, the customer pipeline. The "village's spears." Lower than YC, Upwork, Stripe Atlas, most book royalty deals.

Am I an employee? No. You are an independent contractor — by the cleanest legal definition. You set your hours. You own your output. You are responsible for your own taxes. Terms.

What is Dennis? The mascot. Anarcho-syndicalist serf from Monty Python and the Holy Grail (1975). The wink that signals the project takes itself seriously without taking itself too seriously. Appendix on Dennis.

Why use the word "socialism"? Because the tools are collectively owned by the network, and the marginal cost of additional users is near zero. The accuracy of the word matters more than the political reaction. More in the FAQ →
