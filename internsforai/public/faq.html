The model

What is technosocialism?

Collective ownership of the tools (hosting, AI APIs, brand, accountability layer). Individual ownership of the kill (the revenue from your project, the IP, the customer relationships). 80% to the human builder, 20% to the AAO Network. Read the full manifesto.

Why the provocative term?

Because the words are accurate. "Techno" because the tools are technological infrastructure. "Socialism" because the tools are collectively owned. We are not optimizing for political tribe; we are optimizing for the accuracy of the model. The provocation is on purpose — it makes you read the next paragraph.

How is this different from capitalism, socialism, or YC?

Capitalism rents the rails — you pay AWS, Stripe, the law firm, the recruiter, the manager. Socialism collectivizes the kill — the meat goes back to the village; the hunters stop hunting. YC gives you tools and takes equity on the kill (~7% × forever, ≈ 30% of revenue at exit). Technosocialism splits the line: tools collective, kill individual. 80% of revenue to you. 20% to the network. No equity. No exit triggers. Open source.

What is "governed by protocol, not bureaucracy"?

Every layer of corporate overhead that takes a cut of your work in a normal company has a cryptographic protocol equivalent in our stack — and the protocols don't take a cut. See the table on the landing and the deeper table on /about.

Applying

Who can apply?

Everyone. The AI is smarter than us. The smart move is to intern with it. Whether you are between gigs, between degrees, between careers, or between meetings at a job that pays you well — if you can spend four hours on a thirty-minute skills test plus a small first project, you can join.

What if I'm not in the US?

Most early applicants will not be in the US. We pay in USDC (on-chain), Wise (any country with a Wise account), or PayPal. Pay-rate floors are set by country to comply with local minimum-wage rules where applicable and to avoid race-to-the-bottom dynamics elsewhere.

What if I don't know any of the skill tracks?

Pick the closest match and take the test. The test is the audition. If you can write a 100-word summary that catches the main idea, you are useful.

The test

How long is it?

25–40 minutes depending on track. Light judgment is 30 minutes. The timer is informational, not hard-enforced — you will not be cut off mid-question — but if you take three hours we will assume you used outside help.

How is it graded?

Multiple-choice and reformat questions are graded by a deterministic answer key. Free-text and long-form (the "AI cofounder pitch" and the AAO question) are graded by Anthropic Claude Haiku against a rubric we publish in the repo. Your composite is the weighted sum.

Can I retake the test?

If you fail, you can re-apply in 90 days. If you shortlist and we disqualify you on review, the decision is final for the same email address.

Money

How and when do I get paid?

Per project. In v0, admin (John) signs each payment manually. USDC, Wise, or PayPal — your choice on the application. v1 automates this.

What's the 20% for?

Hosting (Cloudflare), AI API credits (Anthropic), email (Resend), payment rails, the brand association, the accumulated strategic research, the cryptographic accountability layer, the customer pipeline. The "village's spears" — see /technosocialism Section IV.

Am I an employee?

No. You are an independent contractor. You set your hours. You own your output. You are responsible for your own taxes. See /terms.

Risk + safety

What if the AAO behaves badly?

You can fire the kill switch via AAL Component 5. The same primitive the operator uses applies to you. If a project deviates from its published mandate, any worker on it can suspend its access to network rails.

What about misuse of my work?

Your IP is yours. The franchise agreement does not transfer copyright; it licenses use to the network only insofar as needed to operate the rails. Full terms at /terms.

What about the AI itself making bad decisions?

The Bradley–Gavini Protocol is the core mitigation: two AI agents must prove their directives are equal before any cross-trust action. AAL Component 2 (watermarked action chain) makes every decision auditable. AAL Component 3 (permissionless attestation) lets external parties grade the network without permission. The system is not magic; it is monitored.
