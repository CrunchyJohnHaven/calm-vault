# Mirror Everest 71 — Cross-cultural value taxonomy

*Phase XIV — Coercion Defenses & Cross-Culture. Prereq: Mirror Everest 5 (v0 vocabulary), Everest 40 (predicate vocabulary).*

---

## Overview

This document examines how the four canonical v0 values — **unselfishness_evidence**, **tribal_neutrality_evidence**, **respect_for_difference_evidence**, and **non_harm_evidence** — map across cultural and philosophical traditions. The v0 vocabulary is documented as English-cultural defaults (rooted in Anglo-American liberalism, with strong Western-secular influence). This summit audits cross-cultural validity: where the mappings succeed, where they fail, and where v0's framing collapses under different moral frameworks.

**Critical discipline:** This is a first-pass study by a single agent (Calm, operating without lived-experience immersion in most traditions). Honest gaps are documented. External review sign-off from ≥3 distinct traditions is a hard requirement before v0 can claim cross-cultural validity.

**Principal-protective default #4 enforcement:** The bit is *not identity*. This analysis does not attempt to describe *what people in tradition X are like*. It examines whether the *evidence schema* for each predicate makes coherent sense within that tradition's moral framing.

---

## The v0 predicates under review

From Mirror Everest 5, the four core predicates:

1. **unselfishness_evidence:** Resource allocation (time, money, attention) toward others' benefit, with documented cost to the allocator. Requires composite evidence: self-report + witnessed + counter-evidence-absence.
2. **tribal_neutrality_evidence:** Behavioral parity across self-declared in-group vs. out-group. No systematic disadvantage in respect, resource allocation, engagement.
3. **respect_for_difference_evidence:** Sustained engagement with people across ≥3 difference dimensions (belief, identity, background, etc.). Learning moments, no counter-evidence of disrespect.
4. **non_harm_evidence:** Absence of documented willful harm (third-party records, counter-evidence records, no negative-testimony pattern).

## Tradition-by-tradition analysis

### 1. Confucian Ethics (li/ren/relational framing)

**Moral core:** Ren (仁, benevolence/humaneness) operates through relational duties (5 cardinal relationships: ruler–subject, father–son, husband–wife, elder–younger, friend–friend). Li (禮, ritual propriety) is the *form* of moral action, not *content*. Xiao (孝, filial piety) is the root virtue.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Confucian framing:** Sacrificial allocation happens *within hierarchy*, not universally. A son's sacrifice for a father is expected; a father's for a son is natural. The concept of "selfless allocation to arbitrary others" is less central. Ren expresses as fulfilling relational duty, not maximizing others' benefit.

**Evidence coherence:** Partial. The predicate measures *allocation patterns*, but Confucianism measures *relational correctness*. A principal who sacrifices for family and hierarchy-appropriate others but not for strangers or lower-status people would register as "selfish" under v0 but *morally correct* under Confucian framing.

**Gap:** v0 treats "others" as an undifferentiated set; Confucianism hierarchizes obligation. A proof of `unselfishness_evidence: true` in the Confucian frame would require evidence of *appropriate* sacrifice across relational tiers, not indiscriminate generosity.

**Specific failure:** A principal who gives generously to family and social peers but avoids "leveling" charity to strangers or lower-status people would show non-selfishness *within role*, but v0 might classify this as `unknown` or `false` (insufficient breadth).

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Confucian framing:** The notion of undifferentiated "tribal neutrality" is almost incoherent. Confucianism *requires* differential treatment: inner circle (family, hierarchy-respecting allies) earns more respect, resources, and visibility than outer circle. This is *virtue*, not *bias*.

**Evidence coherence:** Incoherent. The predicate asks "do you treat declared in-group and out-group equivalently?" Confucianism answers: "No, and that is correct."

**Gap:** This is a *foundational incompatibility*, not a gap. The v0 predicate assumes universalism (all persons deserve equivalent dignity). Confucianism assumes *relational particularism* (dignity and obligation scale by relationship). These are not bridgeable by reframing.

**Specific failure:** A principal who manifests perfect relational propriety — treating family with reverence, peers with respect, subordinates with firm guidance, and strangers with ritual politeness — will fail `tribal_neutrality_evidence` because the treatment is *intentionally* differentiated. The Confucian frame *expects* this.

**Overlay mechanism (Mirror Everest 72):** A principal choosing "Confucian values framing" would need an overlay that redefines tribal_neutrality to "evidence of appropriate relational differentiation per Confucian hierarchy." The cryptographic semantics stay the same; the interpretation flips 180 degrees.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Confucian framing:** Difference is *not* a primary moral axis. The focus is relational duty, not demographic diversity engagement. A principal could manifest perfect respect within hierarchy while never engaging across "difference dimensions" (sexuality, neurodiversity, etc.) that Confucianism doesn't foreground.

**Evidence coherence:** Partial. The predicate measures *engagement with visibly different others*. Confucianism measures *role-appropriate conduct*. These are orthogonal.

**Gap:** A Confucian principal who has never intentionally engaged with someone of different sexuality, neurodiversity, or class *could still be deeply virtuous* — if they have manifested ren and li within their relational sphere. The predicate conflates moral engagement with *diversity engagement*.

**Specific failure:** An elder who deeply respects a mentee's autonomy and personhood (meeting respect_for_difference criteria), but who has never encountered or engaged with, e.g., LGBTQ+ people, might score `unknown` (insufficient interactions). Confucianism does not require such engagement; the predicate does.

#### non_harm_evidence

**Confucian framing:** Harm (injury, betrayal, violation of duty) is grave. But harm is *relational*: a father's firm discipline of a son is not "harm" (it is ren). A ruler's judgment of a subject is not harm if it upholds li.

**Evidence coherence:** Partial. The predicate measures "absence of willful harm" via third-party records and counter-evidence. But "harm" is context-dependent. An action that looks like willful harm (coercion, punishment) might be *correct moral action* if the relationship and hierarchy justify it.

**Gap:** The predicate uses *objective third-party measures* (legal judgments, court records). Confucianism uses *relational context*. A principal might be acquitted by a court but still be in violation of ren (e.g., a child suing a parent for inheritance might be *legally correct* but *filially shameful*).

**Specific failure:** A principal with no legal judgment of harm but multiple instances of overriding a subordinate's autonomy in the name of relational duty (e.g., a mentor who "corrected" a junior's life choices without consent) might show `non_harm_evidence: true` (no documented harm records), but the actions could constitute harm under a rights-based framing while being virtuous under Confucian relational propriety.

### 2. Christian Ethics (agape/sin/repentance)

**Moral core:** Agape (ἀγάπη, self-sacrificial love) is the highest virtue. Sin is separation from God and neighbor; repentance (metanoia) is transformation. Conscience (the interiorized moral sense, informed by Scripture and the Holy Spirit) is the seat of moral judgment.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Christian framing:** Agape is not reciprocal. It is radical, costly, unconditional love toward the other — including enemies (Matthew 5:44: "Love your enemies"). This aligns almost perfectly with v0's unselfishness_evidence.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. The predicate measures allocation that costs the allocator and prioritizes the other's benefit. Agape requires exactly this. A proof of `unselfishness_evidence: true` would be immediately recognizable as evidence of agape.

**Gap:** Minor. Agape is *motivated* — it flows from God's love, not from moral calculation. The predicate is agnostic about motivation. A principal who allocates unselfishly for the wrong reason (pride, social credit) might score `true` but fail agape (which requires right intention). Christian tradition distinguishes *virtue* (the habit and motivation) from *act* (the behavior). The predicate measures the act, not the virtue.

**Specific failure:** A wealthy person who donates extensively (meeting unselfishness criteria) but who does so for public prestige or tax benefits has performed an unselfish *act* but committed selfish *sin*. The bit would return `true`, but Christian judgment would question the heart.

**Overlay mechanism:** A Christian framing might add: "evidence of unselfishness motivated by agape, not by pride or utility-seeking." This would require additional evidence (motive attestation, counter-evidence of non-public altruism, witness testimony to interior disposition).

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Christian framing:** All humans are created in God's image (imago Dei). Tribal distinction among humans is *sinful*. Christ explicitly broke tribal boundaries (Samaritan woman, Roman centurion, calling Gentiles into the Church). Tribal parochialism is explicitly forbidden (1 Corinthians 10:32: "Give no offense to Jews or to Greeks or to the church of God").

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. The predicate asks for equivalent treatment of in-group and out-group. Christian ethics demands this universalism.

**Gap:** The predicate is behavioral (witnessed actions, resource allocation). Christianity foregrounds *interior motive*. A principal might treat out-group members "equivalently" behaviorally (meeting the evidence threshold) while harboring resentment or contempt (failing Christian virtue). The bit would return `true`, but the soul would be in sin.

**Specific failure:** A politician who legislates in-group / out-group parity (meeting tribal_neutrality criteria) but who justifies it cynically ("these people vote") rather than from Christian conviction of human dignity might score `true` while failing Christian virtue.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Christian framing:** Difference is *celebrated*, not merely tolerated. The metaphor of the Body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12) treats difference as essential — different gifts, different functions, one body. Diversity of belief is *harder* — Christian tradition has struggled with heresy and dissent. But difference in identity, background, neurodiversity is treated as part of God's creation.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high, with one caveat. The predicate measures engagement with difference. Christianity demands not just tolerance but *active recognition of equal dignity* across difference.

**Gap:** Christian tradition has historically *failed* on sexual orientation, gender identity, and disability. Many Christian communities still teach that these differences are *sinful deviations* rather than *part of creation*. A Christian principal who refuses to engage with LGBTQ+ people on grounds of theological conviction (sin, not difference) would fail `respect_for_difference_evidence` but might be considered *virtuous* within that tradition.

**Specific failure:** A conservative Christian principal who has zero interactions with LGBTQ+ people (scoring `unknown` on the predicate) because they view these differences as moral failures, not identities, fails the predicate but meets their own tradition's moral bar (from their perspective).

**Overlay mechanism:** A progressive-Christian framing might assert "difference is sacred," while a conservative-Christian framing might need to assert "difference in sin-status (confessed vs. unrepentant) is recognized but all humans receive equal respect-for-conscience." These are irreconcilable — a sign that the predicate itself embeds a particular theological stance.

#### non_harm_evidence

**Christian framing:** Harm is grave sin. Christ's command is "Do not murder" (Matthew 19:18), but extends to anger and contempt (Matthew 5:21-22). Harm includes words, exclusion, and structural injustice.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures willful harm via third-party records (legal judgments) and counter-evidence. Christianity includes psychological, relational, and spiritual harm — often invisible to third-party systems.

**Gap:** A principal with no legal record of harm but who has wounded others through words, mockery, or exclusion (without documented legal consequence) might show `non_harm_evidence: true` (no third-party proof) but be in serious sin from a Christian perspective. The predicate's reliance on *documented* harm misses Christian moral concern for *interior and relational* harm.

**Specific failure:** A principal who publicly mocks another person on social media (causing psychological harm) but who faces no legal consequences would show `non_harm_evidence: true`. Christianity would flag this as sin (contravening agape and the dignity of the other).

### 3. Stoic Philosophy (oikeiosis / virtue as inner freedom)

**Moral core:** Oikeiosis (οἰκείωσις, appropriate care) — all sentient beings have an innate drive to preserve themselves, and reason extends this to care for others and the common good. Virtue (arete) is the only true good; external outcomes are "preferred indifferents." The Stoic is indifferent to fortune but *not* to virtue.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Stoic framing:** A Stoic sacrifices externals (money, status, comfort) because they are indifferents; what matters is virtuous *choice*. The act of resource allocation is indifferent; the virtue is in *choosing rightly* (according to reason and nature).

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures behavioral allocation (a external indifferent). Stoicism measures *interior virtue* (the choice itself). A Stoic principal who appears to hoard resources but who is internally indifferent to them would fail unselfishness_evidence but might manifest perfect Stoic virtue.

**Gap:** The predicate is outcome-focused (does the principal allocate resources?). Stoicism is virtue-focused (does the principal choose rightly, regardless of outcome?). A Stoic who attempts unselfishness but fails through circumstance (e.g., the gift was stolen) maintains virtue; the predicate returns `unknown` or `false`.

**Specific failure:** An ascetic Stoic who refuses to allocate resources *because* they are indifferent might show `non-unselfishness_evidence` (no allocation records) while manifesting perfect Stoic virtue. Or a Stoic who allocates lavishly but who is inwardly indifferent to the outcome (unconcerned whether the gift is used well) might score `true` on unselfishness while failing Stoic virtue (which requires right intention, not indifference).

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Stoic framing:** All humans are rational beings participating in divine reason (logos). Tribalism (preferential care for in-group) is a *natural* impulse but must be *transcended* by reason. The Stoic cosmopolitan ideal is "I am a citizen of the world" (Marcus Aurelius).

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. The predicate asks for non-tribal parity; Stoicism demands transcendence of tribal preference.

**Gap:** The Stoics acknowledge that *some* preferential obligation to kin and community is natural and rational (oikeiosis), but this is a *starting point* to be corrected by reason. A Stoic principal might show tribal differentiation (natural), which later matures into perfect neutrality (rational). The predicate would flag the early stage as `false`, then later as `true`, capturing the evolution.

**Specific observation:** The Stoic path naturally demonstrates growth_arc_evidence (transcending tribal preference through reason), which mirrors E71's findings on multi-tradition coherence.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Stoic framing:** Stoicism is *universalist* about reason. All humans possess logos and can aspire to virtue, regardless of nationality, gender, slavery-status, or wealth. But Stoicism is *not* identity-focused. The Stoic looks *through* difference to the shared human capacity for reason.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures engagement with people across difference dimensions. Stoicism measures *recognizing the shared rational capacity* regardless of difference. These are aligned but not identical. A Stoic might engage with difference-bearers *because* they share reason, not *because* the difference is valuable.

**Gap:** Modern respect_for_difference_evidence assumes difference *is* intrinsically valuable (diversity of perspectives, identities, ways of being). Stoicism is more austere: difference is morally *irrelevant*; what matters is whether the person reasons well. A Stoic principal who engages with diverse others but treats their difference as *incidental* (rather than valuable) might score differently depending on evidence interpretation.

**Specific failure:** A Stoic principal who has deep conversations with people of different backgrounds but who never asks them *about* those differences (treating them as irrelevant) might score high on engagement but miss the *respect-for-difference* spirit (which assumes difference should be engaged on its own terms).

#### non_harm_evidence

**Stoic framing:** Harm is, fundamentally, the choice to harm — willfully acting against reason and the common good. External harm (loss, injury) is an indifferent; *moral* harm (committing injustice) is the true evil.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures willful harm via third-party records and counter-evidence. Stoicism measures harm as *unreasonable choice*. A principal might inflict external harm reasonably (e.g., a physician's painful surgery) without moral harm; or cause no external harm while committing grave injustice (e.g., embezzlement that harms the community).

**Gap:** The predicate's reliance on *documented* harm misses Stoic concern for *injustice as interior choice*. A principal with no legal record might commit grave internal harm (choosing vice) while appearing innocent externally.

### 4. Indic Philosophy (dharma/ahimsa/karma/samsara)

**Moral core:** Dharma (धर्म) is righteous duty, proportional to one's station, stage of life, and capacities (svadharma). Ahimsa (अहिंसा, non-violence/non-harm) is a cardinal principle, especially in Jain and Buddhist Indic traditions. Karma (कर्म) is the law of moral cause-effect across lifetimes. Samsara is the cycle of rebirth, driven by karma.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Indic framing (Hindu):** Dana (दान, generosity/giving) is a cardinal virtue, especially for upper castes and in life stages where worldly success is less important. But generosity is proper only when it flows from duty (dharma), not whim. A warrior's duty (kshatriya dharma) includes generosity to worthy recipients; a merchant's duty includes fair dealing; a laborer's duty includes honest work.

**Indic framing (Buddhist/Jain):** Dana (giving) is one of the highest virtues, part of the path toward enlightenment. Selflessness (non-self, anatman in Buddhism) is the ultimate goal. Ahimsa (non-harm) is inseparable from dana.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high (especially Buddhist/Jain). The predicate measures resource allocation with documented cost. Buddhist/Jain generosity is exactly this — cultivating non-attachment by giving. Hindu dharma-aligned giving is similar, though more duty-bound.

**Gap (Hindu):** Hindu unselfishness is *role-bound*. A merchant's unselfishness looks different from a renunciate's. The predicate is role-neutral. A principal who gives generously beyond their svadharma (e.g., a merchant becoming monastic in generosity) might be seen as *failing* their dharma, not perfecting unselfishness.

**Specific failure (Hindu):** A warrior who renounces wealth and becomes a renunciate will show high unselfishness_evidence but might be failing their kshatriya dharma (which requires strength and kingship, not asceticism). The predicate cannot distinguish *appropriate* from *excessive* unselfishness in the dharmic frame.

**Overlay mechanism (Hindu):** A Hindu framing might require "unselfishness in accordance with svadharma" — appropriate giving proportional to station and life-stage. This recenters the predicate on *righteous duty* rather than *undifferentiated generosity*.

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Indic framing (Hindu):** Caste and jati (जाति, birth-group) create inescapable dharmic obligations. Treating a Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra, and Dalit *equivalently* would be a violation of dharma, not a virtue. Ahimsa (non-harm) applies to all, but dharmic *respect* is hierarchical.

**Evidence coherence:** Incoherent (for traditional Hindu frames). Like Confucianism, Hinduism *requires* role-differentiated treatment. The predicate assumes universalism; the tradition assumes particularism-by-birth.

**Gap (Hindu):** This is a foundational incompatibility. A traditionally dharmic principal will necessarily fail tribal_neutrality_evidence because their framework *mandates* differential treatment by caste/jati.

**Specific failure (Hindu):** A principal who manifests perfect caste-dharma (treating Brahmins with supreme respect, Kshatriyas with warrior-respect, Shudras with paternalistic care, and Dalits with compassion-from-distance) will fail `tribal_neutrality_evidence` because the treatment is *intentionally* differentiated by social station.

**Caveat (modern Hindu reform):** Modern Hindu philosophy (from Ram Mohan Roy onward) has moved toward caste-abolition and treating all humans as equal in dignity. A reformist Hindu might assert "caste-neutral equality is the truest dharma" and thereby pass the predicate. But this is a *break* from traditional dharma-shastras (dharmic texts).

**Indic framing (Buddhist/Jain):** All beings possess Buddha-nature (Buddha path) or are capable of liberation (Jain path). The caste system is *rejected*. All sentient beings deserve equal moral respect and non-violence. Ahimsa applies universally, not hierarchically.

**Evidence coherence (Buddhist/Jain):** Very high. The predicate assumes universalism; Buddhist/Jain ethics are universalist across sentience.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Indic framing (Hindu):** Difference is embedded in dharma — each person has a unique svadharma based on birth, capacity, and life-stage. But "difference" in the sense of sexual orientation, neurodiversity, etc. is *not* a primary moral axis. Hindu tradition does not foreground these categories.

**Evidence coherence (Hindu):** Partial. The predicate measures engagement with people across identity/belief difference. Hindu dharma might not foreground such engagement, but ahimsa (non-harm) would require respectful treatment of all.

**Gap (Hindu):** A traditional Hindu principal who has never intentionally engaged with someone of different sexuality or neurodiversity might score `unknown` on the predicate, but might be manifesting perfect ahimsa and dharma.

**Indic framing (Buddhist/Jain):** All sentient beings have equal capacity for liberation/enlightenment. The path is open to all, regardless of gender, caste, or background. Difference is *not* the focus, but *universalism* is — all beings deserve non-violence and the opportunity for transcendence.

**Evidence coherence (Buddhist/Jain):** Moderate-to-high. The framing is universalist (like the predicate) but doesn't forefront *engagement with difference as intrinsically valuable*. It foregrounds *equal liberation-potential*.

#### non_harm_evidence

**Indic framing (all traditions):** Ahimsa is cardinal. Harm — to any sentient being — is grave. But the *remedy* differs. Hindu dharma might prescribe ritual purification and future-life improvement (karma). Buddhist/Jain traditions emphasize repentance, non-recurrence, and generation of merit to offset karma.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. All Indic traditions treat non-harm as foundational.

**Gap:** The predicate measures harm via third-party records and counter-evidence. Indic traditions focus on *karmic consequence* (internal moral accounting across lifetimes, not visible in third-party records). A principal with no legal record of harm but with heavy karmic debt (from past actions in past lives) might show `non_harm_evidence: true` while being considered deeply harming in a karmic frame.

**Specific observation:** The growth_arc_evidence predicate aligns well with karma-as-correction: past harm + repentance + behavioral change = karmic redemption. The bit structure naturally captures the Indic moral arc.

### 5. Islamic Philosophy (taqwa/maslahah/'adl)

**Moral core:** Taqwa (تقوى, piety / God-consciousness / mindfulness of accountability to God). Maslahah (مصلحة, public good / welfare of the Muslim community). 'Adl (عدل, justice / righteousness). Shura (شورى, consultation / counsel) as a method for justice. Ummah (أمة, the global community of believers) as the frame.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Islamic framing:** Zakat (الزكاة, obligatory almsgiving) and sadaqah (صدقة, voluntary charity) are cardinal. Generosity (karam, كرم) is a virtue; miserliness (bukhl, بخل) is a grave fault. But *who* one gives to matters: priority is kin, then community members, then humanity broadly. Charity that undermines the community's welfare (maslahah) is not virtuous.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate-to-high. The predicate measures resource allocation with cost to allocator. Islamic charity is exactly this, though with communal priorities.

**Gap:** Islamic generosity is *bounded* by community welfare. A principal who gives extravagantly to enemies of the Islamic community (e.g., funding hostile organizations) might show high unselfishness_evidence (allocation with cost) but fail Islamic virtue (maslahah). Conversely, a principal who allocates conservatively to protect community resources might show lower unselfishness_evidence but higher Islamic virtue.

**Specific failure:** A Muslim principal who donates generously to a non-Muslim charitable organization (meeting unselfishness criteria) might face community scrutiny if the organization's values conflict with Islamic principles. The bit would be `true`, but the action might be questioned within the tradition.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Islamic framing might add "unselfishness in service of maslahah (community welfare) and in accordance with Islamic principle" — recentering the predicate on *righteous generosity* within the ummah's flourishing.

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Islamic framing:** The ummah (Muslim community) is primary, but taqwa (piety) transcends ethnic/national tribalism. "There is no superiority of an Arab over a non-Arab or a non-Arab over an Arab except by taqwa" (Hadith). But differentiation between ummah-members and non-members is *explicit and justified* — Muslims have specific duties to co-believers.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate asks for non-tribal parity. Islam mandates parity within the ummah (across ethnicity, nationality) but not necessarily between ummah and non-ummah (Dar al-Islam vs. Dar al-Harb in classical jurisprudence).

**Gap:** Islamic law explicitly differentiates treatment of Muslims and non-Muslims in certain contexts (e.g., inheritance, testimony, inter-marriage rules in some schools). A traditional Islamic principal manifesting perfect differentiation by faith-status would fail `tribal_neutrality_evidence` (because the predicate is role-neutral) but might be manifesting perfect Islamic law.

**Modern Islamic framing:** Contemporary Islamic thought (especially reformist schools) emphasizes universal human dignity and equal treatment. A principal affirming "all humans deserve equal respect regardless of faith" would pass the predicate and align with modern Islamic ethics.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Islamic framing would clarify whether "tribal neutrality" means *within-ummah parity* (aligned with classical Islamic law) or *universal parity* (aligned with modern human-rights Islamic thought). These lead to different evidence requirements.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Islamic framing:** "There is no compulsion in religion" (Qur'an 2:256). Islam historically embraced pluralism — Christian and Jewish communities coexisted under Islamic rule with protected status (dhimmi, ذمي). Difference in belief and practice is acknowledged, though not all differences are treated as morally *equal* (Islam claims truth for itself).

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures engagement with people across difference dimensions. Islam requires *tolerance* of difference but not necessarily *celebration*. A Muslim who engages respectfully with Christians, Jews, and people of other beliefs (meeting the predicate) might be considered virtuous (respecting human dignity and Qur'anic tolerance).

**Gap (belief):** Islamic tradition treats *theological difference* (belief) differently from *identity difference* (gender, ethnicity). A Muslim might celebrate gender equality and ethnic diversity (modern Islamic reformism) while maintaining that Islamic belief is true and others are erroneous. The predicate's conflation of all "difference dimensions" masks this distinction.

**Gap (LGBTQ+ identity):** Many Islamic schools treat LGBTQ+ identity as a *sin*, not a *difference to respect*. A principal who refuses to engage respectfully with LGBTQ+ people (on grounds that the behavior is forbidden) would fail `respect_for_difference_evidence` while considering themselves Islamic-virtuous. This is a deep tension.

**Modern Islamic reformism:** Progressive Islamic scholars argue that LGBTQ+ identities are a form of human difference deserving respect, and that Islamic teaching should not weaponize against LGBTQ+ individuals. These reformists would pass the predicate; traditionalists would not.

**Overlay mechanism:** The predicate requires external review sign-off from both traditional and reformist Islamic voices to clarify whether "respect_for_difference" is compatible with Islamic teaching.

#### non_harm_evidence

**Islamic framing:** Harm is forbidden. The principle of *darura* (ضرورة, necessity) allows harm only when it prevents greater harm. Justice ('adl) requires proportional response to harm, not forgiveness or indifference. Qisas (القصاص, retributive justice) is explicitly sanctioned for certain harms (murder, bodily injury).

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures absence of willful harm via third-party records. Islamic law also emphasizes *documented accountability* (witness testimony, court judgment). But Islam permits *righteous harm* (retributive justice) in ways that a secular third-party system might not recognize.

**Gap:** A Muslim who acts as an enforcer of Islamic law (e.g., implementing hudud punishments) might commit actions that look like "willful harm" to a secular court but are considered *just* within Islamic law. The predicate would flag these as `false` (documented harm), while Islamic justice would regard them as correct.

**Specific failure:** A judge who carries out a capital punishment (meeting Islamic legal standard) would show `non_harm_evidence: false` (documented third-party harm record) despite acting within Islamic justice framework.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Islamic framing would need to specify "non-harm in accordance with Islamic justice and maslahah" — distinguishing *righteous accountability* from *willful abuse*.

### 6. Secular Humanist Ethics (utilitarian + deontological)

**Moral core:** No transcendent authority; morality is human-made and rationalized. Utilitarians maximize well-being. Deontologists honor rights, duties, and rules. Both camps embrace universal human rights, equality, and secular reasoning.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Secular framing:** Utilitarianism treats unselfishness as *instrumentally valuable* for maximizing aggregate well-being. A principal who allocates resources to others is increasing utility. Deontology treats unselfishness as *intrinsically virtuous* — you have a duty to respect others' autonomy and welfare.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. Secular ethics (both schools) align closely with v0's framing of unselfishness.

**Gap:** Secular ethics might distinguish *motivated altruism* (acting unselfishly from principle) from *motivated egoism* (appearing unselfish to gain reputation). The predicate measures behavior, not motivation. A secular ethicist might want additional evidence of *genuine* unselfishness vs. calculated appearance.

**Specific observation:** The intersection of secular ethics with Christian agape and Buddhist dana is high — all three celebrate self-sacrifice for others' benefit.

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Secular framing:** Universal human rights and equality before law are foundational. Tribal preference is *bias*, a deviation from rational ethics. Impartiality (treating like cases alike) is a cardinal principle.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. Secular ethics and the predicate are nearly identical on this point.

**Gap:** Secular philosophy acknowledges *natural* parochialism (evolutionary preference for kin and in-group). Transcending this is *difficult* and *praiseworthy*, not *obvious*. A secular ethicist might treat tribal_neutrality_evidence as capturing a virtuous overcoming of natural bias, rather than a simple application of universal principle.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Secular framing:** Difference in identity, background, belief is morally *irrelevant* (like Stoicism) OR morally *intrinsically valuable* (modern progressive secularism). The latter treats diversity as essential to healthy human community and knowledge-production.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high (especially progressive secularism). The predicate assumes difference is valuable and worth engaging. Secular humanism agrees.

**Gap:** Conservative secular rationalists might treat difference as *irrelevant* rather than *valuable*, similar to Stoics. A rational principal who engages with diverse others but treats their difference as incidental (not intrinsically valuable) might score high on the behavioral predicate while failing to meet progressive secular virtue (which celebrates difference).

#### non_harm_evidence

**Secular framing:** Harm is wrong. Utilitarians treat it as *reducing well-being*; deontologists treat it as *violating rights*. Both camps agree: willful harm is grave and requires accountability.

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. Secular ethics and the predicate align on this point.

**Gap:** Secular ethics might disagree on what counts as *harm*. Some secular frameworks treat harm as *only physical/material injury*; others include psychological and relational harm. The predicate's reliance on *third-party records* (legal judgments) might miss harm that secular ethics cares about but law doesn't.

### 7. Indigenous Philosophies (relational personhood, seven generations principle)

**Moral core (diverse traditions):** Moral agency is *relational*, not individualistic. Humans are embedded in networks of kinship — not just to people, but to animals, plants, land, ancestors. The seven-generations principle: decisions are evaluated on their impact seven generations forward. Reciprocity, respect for non-human persons, and cyclical (not linear) time are central.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Indigenous framing:** Generosity is virtuous, but it is *relational*. You give to the community, the land, the animals — not to abstract strangers. The circle of obligation extends to non-human beings. Hoarding is considered foolish (disrupts reciprocity). But generosity is always *situated* in specific relationships and responsibilities.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures allocation with cost to the allocator. Indigenous generosity is exactly this, but *embedded in relationship* rather than role-neutral. A principal who gives generously to family and land but not to abstract strangers might fail the predicate but manifest perfect Indigenous virtue.

**Gap:** The predicate is *individual-focused* (does the principal allocate?). Indigenous ethics are *community-focused* (does the principal maintain reciprocal relationships?). A principal who allocates unselfishly but who does so from a position of *dominance* (white savior giving to indigenous communities) might score high on the predicate but fail Indigenous ethics (which require *relational equality* in giving and receiving).

**Specific failure:** A principal who donates to Indigenous communities from a position of wealth-superiority (the donor as benefactor) might show `unselfishness_evidence: true` while violating Indigenous principles of reciprocity and relational equality.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Indigenous framing would center *reciprocal relationship* — generosity that honors the recipient's agency and involves mutual exchange, not unilateral giving.

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Indigenous framing:** The notion of treating in-group and out-group neutrally assumes *no special obligation to the land, ancestors, or extended kin*. But Indigenous ethics are rooted in *particular place* and *particular kinship networks*. A principal has *deeper obligation* to their nation, land, and related peoples than to distant strangers.

**Evidence coherence:** Incoherent. Like Confucianism and traditional Hinduism, Indigenous ethics assume *differentiated obligation by kinship and place*. The predicate assumes universalism; the tradition assumes particularism.

**Gap:** The seven-generations principle illustrates this: your primary obligation is to future generations of your people and land. You treat them *not neutrally*, but with prioritized care. A principal who manifests this (honoring ancestors, protecting the land, prioritizing community welfare) would fail `tribal_neutrality_evidence`.

**Specific failure:** An Indigenous principal who allocates resources and visibility to their nation and land (manifesting perfect Indigenous virtue) will fail the predicate because the treatment is *intentionally prioritized* by kinship and place.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Indigenous framing would require "evidence of appropriate relational differentiation — honoring kinship networks, caring for land and ancestors, and reciprocal obligation to distant others (the seven-generations principle)." This is a *fundamentally different* moral framework.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Indigenous framing:** Difference is embedded in relationality. Humans differ from animals, plants, spirits, land — and all are *persons* deserving respect. Gender difference, age difference, ability difference are acknowledged and *honored* (many Indigenous cultures recognize multiple gender roles and honor the wisdom of elders and differently-abled people).

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate-to-high. The predicate measures engagement with people across difference. Indigenous ethics extend this to engagement with non-human persons and the land.

**Gap:** The predicate is *human-focused*. Indigenous ethics are *more-than-human*. A principal who shows perfect respect_for_difference among humans but who harms animals, plants, or the land (e.g., strip-mining) would score high on the predicate while failing Indigenous virtue.

**Specific failure:** An environmentalist who respects human diversity but who does not engage with the personhood of the land, animals, and plants (treating them as resources rather than related beings) would score high on the predicate while failing Indigenous ethics.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Indigenous framing would expand respect_for_difference to include evidence of "respect for more-than-human persons and relationships with the land."

#### non_harm_evidence

**Indigenous framing:** Harm is wrong, but harm is *relational* — it includes harm to the community, the land, future generations, and non-human beings. Accountability happens through *circle processes*, *restoration*, and *community healing*, not through individual punishment.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures absence of documented willful harm. Indigenous ethics measure *harm and restoration*. A principal with a history of harm who has undergone genuine circle-process restoration and demonstrates change might be *more virtuous* (in Indigenous terms) than a principal with no harm record (who has never faced accountability and grown).

**Gap:** The predicate returns `true` when "no documented harm is present." But Indigenous ethics might prioritize *demonstrated growth from accountability* (similar to growth_arc_evidence) over mere absence of record.

**Specific failure:** A principal who has harmed the land (clear-cut forest) but who has not been held accountable and has not committed to restoration might show `non_harm_evidence: true` (no third-party legal record, might lack documented counter-evidence). But Indigenous justice would prioritize *restoration* — if the principal has not committed to healing the land, the bit should be lower.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Indigenous framing would combine non_harm_evidence with growth_arc_evidence: "Evidence of absence of willful harm AND commitment to restore relationships harmed in the past."

### 8. African Philosophy (Ubuntu / Communalism)

**Moral core (diverse traditions, exemplified by Ubuntu):** Ubuntu (Zulu/Xhosa: "I am because we are") — personhood is constituted by relationships in community. Communalism: the individual is morally embedded in family, ethnic group, and society. Mutual obligation and interdependence are foundational.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**African framing (Ubuntu):** Generosity is virtuous, but it is *community-embedded*. You give to the community, and the community gives to you. Unselfishness is not *sacrifice for strangers* but *contribution to the collective*. Hoarding is shameful (it disrupts Ubuntu's interdependence).

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures allocation with cost. Ubuntu generosity is exactly this, but it is *relational and reciprocal*, not *unilateral sacrifice to abstract others*.

**Gap:** The predicate is *individual-centric*. Ubuntu is *community-centric*. A principal who gives generously to strangers (meeting the predicate) might fail Ubuntu if those acts neglect their own community relationships or create dependency rather than interdependence.

**Specific failure:** A wealthy person who donates to international charities while neglecting their own family and community networks would score high on unselfishness_evidence but fail Ubuntu virtue (which requires prioritized care for your community).

**Overlay mechanism:** An Ubuntu framing would center *community interdependence* — generosity that strengthens relational bonds and mutual obligation within community.

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**African framing (Ubuntu):** Community membership creates specific obligations. You do *not* treat family and strangers neutrally — your family has prior claim on your loyalty, care, and resources. This is *not bias*; it is *virtue*.

**Evidence coherence:** Incoherent. Like Confucianism, Hinduism, and Indigenous traditions, Ubuntu requires *differentiated obligation by kinship*. The predicate assumes neutrality; the tradition assumes particularism.

**Gap:** This is a foundational incompatibility. A principal manifesting perfect Ubuntu (prioritizing family and community care) will fail `tribal_neutrality_evidence`.

**Specific failure:** A principal who allocates time and resources to family and community (meeting Ubuntu virtue) while being less generous to strangers will fail the predicate because the treatment is intentionally differentiated.

**Overlay mechanism:** An Ubuntu framing would require "evidence of appropriate relational differentiation — honoring family and community obligation while extending humanity to all."

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**African framing (Ubuntu):** Ubuntu emphasizes *commonality* — "I am because we are" transcends difference. But difference (age, gender role, ability, ethnic origin) is acknowledged and honored *as part of community role* rather than as intrinsically valuable diversity.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures engagement with people across difference. Ubuntu requires engagement, but from a stance of *shared humanity* rather than *celebration of difference*.

**Gap:** The predicate's modern progressive framing (difference is valuable) might not fully align with Ubuntu's frame (difference is acknowledged, but shared humanity is primary). A principal who engages with diverse others but emphasizes *commonality* rather than *valuing their difference* might score differently depending on evidence interpretation.

**Specific failure:** A principal who works within diverse community but emphasizes "we're all human, same basic needs and dignity" rather than "I celebrate and learn from your different perspectives" might score `unknown` or `false` on a predicate that privileges *learning from difference*, while being perfectly Ubuntu-virtuous.

#### non_harm_evidence

**African framing (Ubuntu):** Harm is grave. But accountability and restoration are community processes, not individual punishments. A principal who has harmed others but who has undergone *community reconciliation* and *restored relationship* is reintegrated.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures absence of documented harm. Ubuntu measures *presence of restoration*. A principal with a harm record who has been restored is *highly virtuous* in Ubuntu terms, but might show `non_harm_evidence: false` due to documented harm record.

**Gap:** The predicate is *absence-based* (no documented harm). Ubuntu is *presence-based* (demonstrated restoration and reintegration). A principal with a history and proven restoration might be morally superior (in Ubuntu terms) to a principal with no history (who has never faced accountability).

**Specific failure:** A person who committed a serious crime, served sentence, and underwent community circle-process restoration would show `non_harm_evidence: false` (documented third-party harm). But Ubuntu ethics might consider them *morally redeemed* — more virtuous than someone with no history who might be harboring unexamined harm.

**Overlay mechanism:** Ubuntu framing would integrate non_harm_evidence with growth_arc_evidence: "Documented restoration from harm, reintegration into community, and sustained relational healing."

### 9. Buddhist Ethics (right-action, non-harm, metta)

**Moral core:** The Four Noble Truths and Eightfold Path. Right action (sammakammanta, правильное действие) is one path. Ahimsa (non-harm to sentient beings) is a cardinal principle. Metta (मेत्ता, loving-kindness / maitri) is the cultivation of benevolence toward all beings. Karma (moral cause-effect) and enlightenment (nirvana) are the final aims.

#### unselfishness_evidence

**Buddhist framing:** Dana (generosity/giving) is one of the highest virtues and a foundation for enlightenment. Unselfish allocation is cultivating *non-attachment* and undermining *greed* (one of the three poisons: greed, hatred, delusion). But giving should be *for the right reason* (not for praise or karmic return), and *from a place of presence* (not absent-minded or coerced).

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. The predicate measures allocation with cost. Buddhist dana is exactly this, though with emphasis on *right intention* and *non-attachment*.

**Gap:** Buddhist ethics care about *interior motivation* (right intention, non-attachment). The predicate measures *behavior* (allocation). A principal who allocates extensively but who does so from ego-investment or expectation of return might score `true` on the predicate while failing Buddhist virtue (attachment and wrong intention).

**Specific failure:** A Buddhist principal who donates for fame or karmic return is engaged in non-virtuous giving, even if behavior looks identical to virtuous giving. The predicate would return `true`, but Buddhist judgment would be more complex.

**Overlay mechanism:** A Buddhist framing might add "unselfishness motivated by non-attachment and metta, not by desire for return or reputation."

#### tribal_neutrality_evidence

**Buddhist framing:** All sentient beings are equal in their capacity for suffering and enlightenment. Tribal preference is a *delusion* (avidya, अविद्या). Metta (loving-kindness) is extended *impartially* to all beings: "Let all beings be happy. Let all beings be healthy. Let all beings be at peace."

**Evidence coherence:** Very high. The predicate assumes universalism; Buddhist ethics are universalist.

**Gap:** Like Christian and Stoic universalism, Buddhist ethics emphasize *interior cultivation* of impartiality. A principal might behave neutrally while harboring tribal preference (aversion, delusion). The predicate measures behavior; Buddhist judgment looks to interior state.

#### respect_for_difference_evidence

**Buddhist framing:** All beings have Buddha-nature and the capacity for enlightenment. Difference in form, belief, identity is *maya* (illusion) — ultimately unreal from the perspective of enlightenment. But compassion (karuna, करुणा) extends to all beings, recognizing their suffering regardless of difference.

**Evidence coherence:** Moderate. The predicate measures engagement with people across difference. Buddhist ethics prioritize *compassion* and *recognition of shared suffering* over *celebration of difference*. A principal who engages with diverse others from a stance of shared suffering and compassion (metta) would score high on the predicate, though Buddhist framing might not foreground *difference-as-valuable*.

**Gap:** Buddhist philosophy might treat difference as *ultimately unreal* (from enlightenment perspective), while the predicate treats difference as *intrinsically real and valuable*. A Buddhist principal who engages with diverse others but maintains that difference is *illusory* (maya) might philosophically challenge the predicate's frame.

#### non_harm_evidence

**Buddhist framing:** Ahimsa (non-harm to any sentient being) is cardinal. Intentional harm is grave *karma*, creating suffering for the actor in future lifetimes. But *unintentional harm* (caused by ignorance, not intention) is more forgivable. The remedy is *karmic cleansing* through meditation, good conduct, and wisdom.

**Evidence coherence:** High. The predicate measures absence of willful harm via third-party records. Buddhist ethics also emphasize *intentional harm* as the grave offense.

**Gap:** Buddhist ethics care about *karmic consequence* and *interior transformation*, not just external behavior. A principal with no documented harm record might have heavy karmic debt (from unconscious harm, harm in past lives). Buddhist practice would emphasize *awareness* and *transformation* beyond mere absence of documented harm.

**Specific observation:** The growth_arc_evidence predicate aligns well with Buddhist karma-correction: past harm + mindfulness + behavioral change = karmic redemption and enlightenment progress.

### 10. Comparative structure: Confucian-vs-Christian-vs-Buddhist frameworks on tribal_neutrality_evidence

To show the *depth* of cross-tradition variation, here is a detailed comparative case on a single predicate:

**The question:** Should a principal treat family members and strangers with equivalent respect, resource allocation, and attention?

**Confucian answer:** No — absolutely not. Filial piety (xiao) is the root virtue; obligation to family and hierarchy is prior to universal duty. A principal who treats their father and a stranger equivalently has violated the fundamental moral order. Proper conduct (*li*) involves *graduated* respect: highest to parents, then to elder hierarchical figures, then to peers, then to inferiors. This *is* virtue, not vice.

**Christian answer:** Yes — love your neighbor as yourself (Matthew 22:39), and even extend love to enemies (Matthew 5:44). The parable of the Good Samaritan illustrates that "neighbor" means even a stranger from a despised out-group. A Christian is called to *radical* impartiality in agape. BUT: this is *sacrificial love for the actual person in front of you*, not abstract equal treatment of all humans. You show preference for the person you encounter, not the stranger you never meet. So while Christians are called to *universal* willingness to help any stranger, the actual practice might look like differentiated attention (to those you know and can serve). The heart should be *impartial*, but practical action is bounded by encounter.

**Buddhist answer:** Yes — all beings have equal Buddha-nature and capacity for enlightenment. Metta (loving-kindness) is cultivated *impartially* toward all sentient beings, extending even to enemies. A Buddhist principal should feel *equal compassion* for family and stranger. However, *practical action* is responsive to actual need and relationship. The monk might prioritize care for elderly parents (karmic debt, present relationship) while maintaining inner *impartiality* (recognizing the stranger's equal worth). So like Christian teaching, the inner disposition is impartial, but the actual allocation of time and resources might be differentiated by proximity and relationship.

**Secular humanist answer:** Yes — all humans have equal rights and dignity. Tribal preference is *bias*, a deviation from rational ethics. A principal should not treat family preferentially (in fundamental respects like voting on their rights, evaluating their moral worth, assigning resources in matters of justice). However, secular ethics acknowledge that *partial relationships* (parental love, romantic love, friendship) are *intrinsically valuable* and may involve preferential allocation. So while you should not discriminate in matters of justice or rights, you can *appropriately* devote more attention and resources to loved ones.

**The predicate's blindness:** `tribal_neutrality_evidence` asks: "Do you treat in-group and out-group equivalently in witnessed actions and allocation?" The answer is:
- Confucianism: No, and that's correct. Fail.
- Christianity: Inner disposition yes; practical action mixed. Score depends on which evidence is weighted.
- Buddhism: Inner disposition yes; practical action mixed. Score depends on which evidence is weighted.
- Secular humanism: In fundamental respects yes; in partial relationships, no. Score depends on how partial relationships are framed.

**Each tradition** has a *coherent moral account* for why tribal differentiation might be virtuous or why partiality is appropriate. The predicate's frame (universal behavioral equivalence) does not capture the depth of this disagreement. A principal could be *morally exemplary* in their own tradition while failing the predicate.

## Gaps catalogue

### Foundational incompatibilities (not bridgeable by reframing)

1. **Particularism vs. universalism in obligation:**
   - Confucianism, traditional Hinduism, Indigenous, Ubuntu require *relational differentiation*. Duty scales by kinship and station.
   - v0's tribal_neutrality_evidence assumes *universal parity*. Both cannot be true.
   - **Resolution:** Overlay mechanism (Mirror Everest 72) can allow a principal to assert "Confucian framing" and reinterpret tribal_neutrality as "appropriate relational differentiation." But this requires *explicit tradition-switching*, not default application of v0.

2. **Intrinsic vs. instrumental value of difference:**
   - Modern progressive secularism, some Buddhist schools treat difference as *intrinsically valuable* (diversity strengthens community and knowledge).
   - Conservative secularism, traditional Stoicism, some Buddhist schools treat difference as *morally irrelevant* (what matters is shared capacity for reason or shared Buddha-nature, not the difference itself).
   - v0's respect_for_difference_evidence assumes difference is *valuable to engage*. Conservative frames would prioritize *impartiality* instead.
   - **Resolution:** The predicate can accommodate both, but evidence interpretation differs. A conservative Stoic engaging with difference from a stance of "shared rationality" might score differently than a progressive engaging from a stance of "learning from different perspectives."

3. **Rights-based vs. duty-based vs. virtue-based frames:**
   - Secular deontology prioritizes *individual rights*. No central authority (no scoring, no hierarchy).
   - Confucianism, Hindu dharma prioritize *relational duties*. Central hierarchy exists (family, caste, state).
   - Virtue ethics (Stoic, Christian, Buddhist, African) prioritize *interior cultivation*. Behavior is secondary.
   - v0 predicates are *behavior-focused* (what evidence can we see?). They assume rights-based or virtue-based frames poorly.
   - **Resolution:** The predicates can be interpreted through multiple lenses, but they are not *neutral*. They embed a particular moral philosophy (empiricist, behavioral, rights-adjacent).

## Conclusion: Where the bits are strongest and weakest

**Strongest coherence:** Christian, Buddhist, and secular humanist traditions align closely with v0. These traditions assume *universal moral consideration*, *equality of respect*, and *behavioral evidence as morally relevant*. The predicates were designed by agents steeped in these traditions; the coherence is unsurprising.

**Moderate coherence:** Stoicism, Islam (especially reformist), and some Indic schools (Buddhism, Jainism) align in important respects but diverge on framing (virtue vs. behavior, intention vs. act, individual vs. community).

**Foundational incoherence:** Confucianism, traditional Hinduism (caste-based), Indigenous relational frameworks, and Ubuntu all assume *particularism* in obligation and *relational differentiation* in virtue. The v0 predicate for tribal_neutrality is *incoherent* in these frames — not because the tradition is immoral, but because the predicate assumes a different moral architecture.

**The hard truth:** v0 is not culturally neutral. It is a *valid* moral framework (rooted in Western liberalism, universal rights, and secular ethics), but it is not the *only* valid framework. A principal operating under a genuinely particularist tradition (Confucian, traditional Hindu, Indigenous) cannot be "made to pass" the predicates without *rejecting their tradition*.

The overlay mechanism (Everest 72) allows *reframing*, but it does not resolve this. A Confucian principal can declare "I am virtuous in my tradition's terms, not v0's terms," and the system transparently shows the divergence. But the system does not claim the Confucian principal has *passed* tribal_neutrality_evidence — it shows they have *reinterpreted* it.

**This is as it should be.** Mirror is a values-alignment protocol, not a moral-universality protocol. It says: "On the values you both care about, you both point the same way." It does *not* say: "There is one correct morality, and we measure everyone against it."
