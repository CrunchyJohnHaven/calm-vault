## Top Statement

Calm Compass is licensed under the Apache License 2.0 with an additional SCOPE rider that establishes binding use restrictions. The "Calm Compass" name and associated trademark are properties of Creativity Machine LLC, with governance delegated to the Calm Witness Foundation ethics review board. Deployments that operate in violation of the scope restrictions outlined below automatically forfeit the right to use the "Calm Compass" trademark, the associated trademark protections, and the community standing granted by the Calm Witness Foundation. License revocation is automatic upon confirmed violation; the burden of license restoration rests with the deploying organization.

This scope statement is binding on all counterparty implementers who execute work on behalf of Calm Compass, whether as direct licensees, integrators, or downstream users. Adherence to scope is a condition of trademark use, not merely a recommendation.

## Prohibited Use Cases

The following use cases are explicitly prohibited under the Calm Compass scope rider. Any deployment that incorporates Compass predicates into decision-making within these domains automatically violates the license and forfeits trademark rights.

### 1. Credit Decisions

**Prohibition:** Banks, financial technology companies, lenders, and credit agencies MAY NOT use Calm Compass predicates, outputs, or derived metrics as direct or indirect inputs to creditworthiness scoring, credit approval decisions, interest-rate setting, credit line determination, or default-risk modeling.

**Rationale:** Calm Compass predicates attest to observed consistency in values-expression under varying observational conditions. They are not calibrated to predict financial default, repayment capacity, or creditworthiness. Deploying values-attestation machinery as a proxy for credit risk constitutes structural discrimination—it conflates ethical alignment with financial capacity and privileges certain value systems over others. Historical precedent demonstrates that such conflation disproportionately harms marginalized populations and perpetuates systemic inequity in credit access.

**Scope:** This prohibition covers any direct feed of Compass outputs into credit models, any weighting of credit decisions by Compass predicates, and any use of Compass data to stratify borrower pools for differential pricing or approval.

---

### 2. Employment Screening

**Prohibition:** Employers, staffing agencies, talent platforms, and human resources organizations MAY NOT use Calm Compass predicates in hiring decisions, promotion decisions, firing or termination decisions, compensation decisions, performance review decisions, or any other employment-related evaluation or classification.

**Rationale:** Compass predicates measure consistency of values-expression across observational contexts. They are not designed, calibrated, or validated for assessing job competence, cultural fit, reliability, or any employment-relevant trait. The predicate "consistent_under_no_observer" (E152) is particularly attractive for misuse in hiring contexts, as it appears to measure intrinsic trustworthiness when it measures only observational context-independence. Deploying Compass for employment screening violates EEOC guidelines, disability rights law, and the Americans with Disabilities Act. Employment decisions carry irreversible life consequences; Compass data does not meet the evidentiary threshold required for such consequences.

**Scope:** This prohibition is absolute. No employer may use Compass disclosures in any stage of recruitment, evaluation, advancement, termination, or compensation review, whether as a primary signal or as one factor among many.

---

### 3. Insurance Underwriting and Pricing

**Prohibition:** Insurance carriers, actuarial firms, risk-assessment platforms, and reinsurance organizations MAY NOT use Calm Compass predicates in underwriting decisions, premium-setting, claims adjudication, risk classification, policy approval, or any actuarial assessment that influences policyholder pricing or coverage.

**Rationale:** This prohibition is permanent and extends from the Calm Witness scope statement (referenced in E04 and E80). Values-based insurance pricing is structural discrimination. Compass predicates do not measure health status, accident risk, mortality risk, or any actuarial parameter. Using them to modulate premiums or exclude populations from coverage conflates ethical consistency with statistical risk and privileges certain belief systems in the pricing mechanism. The result is systematic disadvantage for populations whose values diverge from insurer-preferred norms. This harm is irreversible once policies are priced, sold, and claims are denied on the basis of Compass data.

**Scope:** The prohibition encompasses direct use, synthetic indexes, and any actuarial model that incorporates Compass data as an input or weighting factor.

---

### 4. Custody and Family Law

**Prohibition:** Family courts, custody arbitrators, custody mediators, family services agencies, social services departments, and guardianship courts MAY NOT use Calm Compass predicates in custody decisions, visitation determinations, parental fitness assessments, or any adjudication affecting parent-child relationships, sibling placement, or guardianship outcomes.

**Rationale:** Custody decisions carry irreversible, lifelong consequences for families and children. Calm Compass predicates measure observed consistency in values-expression; they are not validated for assessing parental capacity, child safety, or fitness for guardianship. The machinery is not equipped for the epistemic weight that family courts would place on it. Historical precedent shows that values-based assessments in family law produce systematic harm to minority populations, LGBTQ+ families, and families whose values diverge from judicial norms. The false-positive rate for any such assessment—the probability that a Compass predicate misidentifies a parent as unfit—is unacceptably high for a decision that separates families.

**Scope:** This prohibition is absolute and covers direct use, advisory reports, and any decision-support system that incorporates Compass data.

---

### 5. Immigration Decisions

**Prohibition:** Immigration officers, immigration courts, asylum adjudicators, visa officers, border security officials, and any government agency responsible for admission, denial, visa issuance, or removal decisions MAY NOT use Calm Compass predicates as part of immigration or asylum determinations.

**Rationale:** Immigration decisions are high-stakes and irreversible. Removal or visa denial carries consequences including family separation, statelessness, and persecution. Calm Compass predicates measure values-consistency; they are not calibrated for predicting security risk, admissibility, or integration capacity. The risk of cultural bias in predicate calibration is substantial: Compass was trained and validated primarily on Western, English-speaking populations; its predicates may systematically misclassify or undervalue values-expressions rooted in non-Western cultural traditions. Deploying such machinery for immigration decisions perpetuates structural discrimination and exposes vulnerable populations to irreversible harm.

**Scope:** The prohibition covers direct use in case adjudication, advisory reports to immigration officials, and any decision-support system that feeds Compass data to immigration authorities.

---

### 6. Court Evidence

**Prohibition:** Calm Compass disclosures, attestations, and derived predicates MAY NOT be introduced as evidence in criminal proceedings, civil litigation, or administrative proceedings without all of the following conditions:

1. Explicit, informed written consent from the principal whose Compass disclosure is being introduced.
2. Judicial review and approval of the protocol suitability prior to admission.
3. Full transparency to all parties regarding the limitations, calibration, and intended uses of Compass predicates.

**Rationale:** Compass predicates measure one narrow dimension—consistency of values-expression under varying observational contexts. They are not measures of truthfulness, reliability, credibility, or veracity. Introducing them as evidence in court suggests probative value for adjudication that Compass data does not possess. The judicial system carries authority to enforce its findings through incarceration, asset seizure, and other irreversible remedies. Compass data does not meet the evidentiary threshold for such consequences.

**Scope:** This prohibition is presumptive; it may be overcome only through explicit principal consent and judicial protocol review.

---

### 7. Law Enforcement Investigations

**Prohibition:** Police departments, prosecutors, intelligence agencies, national security organizations, and any law enforcement body MAY NOT request Calm Compass disclosures as part of investigations, criminal inquiries, intelligence gathering, or surveillance operations.

**Rationale:** Law enforcement requests for Compass data place principals in a position of asymmetric jeopardy: they must choose between preserving their Compass disclosures (a form of value expression) and risking obstruction-of-justice charges. The data itself is not probative for criminal liability or guilt; using it in investigations perpetuates assumptions that certain value-expressions indicate criminality. This mechanism has historically been weaponized against marginalized communities, minorities, and political dissidents whose values diverge from law enforcement norms.

**Scope:** The prohibition is absolute and covers direct requests, subpoenas to platform operators, and any compelled disclosure of Compass data to law enforcement.

## Permitted Uses

The following uses are affirmatively permitted under Calm Compass scope:

1. **AI-Agent-to-AI-Agent Collaboration:** The primary use case. AI agents attesting to coordination, alignment, and shared value-commitments to enable multi-agent collaboration and transparency.

2. **Journalism:** Source verification and claim validation, conditional on explicit source consent. A journalist may request that a source disclose their Compass attestation to verify prior claims the source has made in public statements, with source consent binding.

3. **Academic Research:** Research conducted under Institutional Review Board approval, with explicit research-class default consents from participating principals. Publication must anonymize individual disclosures and report only aggregate, population-level insights.

4. **Personal Use:** One principal sharing their own Compass disclosures with friends, family, peers, colleagues, or trusted confidants for personal relationship building, without commercial deployment or third-party aggregation.

5. **Civil Society and NGO Collaboration:** Non-governmental organizations may use Compass to attest shared commitment to mission values, coalition alignment, and cooperative intent, provided the use case affirms inclusion rather than exclusion.

6. **Peer AI-Collective Alignment:** Multiple AI principals coordinating within a Calm Pact composition or peer AI collective, using Compass predicates to establish shared alignment on ethical constraints and collaborative commitments.

## Implementer Pledge

All counterparty implementers, integrators, and downstream users of Calm Compass MUST sign and retain a formal implementer pledge affirming:

1. Understanding of the seven prohibited use cases above.
2. Commitment not to deploy Compass in any of the prohibited domains.
3. Responsibility for auditing their own use and reporting suspected violations.
4. Agreement that trademark revocation is automatic upon verified violation.

The implementer pledge is referenced in the Counterparty Implementers Guide (E98) and is binding as a condition of trademark use. Organizations that refuse to sign the pledge forfeit the right to use the "Calm Compass" trademark and associated brand.

## Trademark Enforcement and License Revocation

### Ownership and Governance

The "Calm Compass" name, trademark, and associated brand assets are properties of Creativity Machine LLC, with governance and enforcement authority delegated to the Calm Witness Foundation ethics review board (per E241, pending formal transfer).

### Automatic License Revocation

Use of the "Calm Compass" trademark in violation of the scope restrictions triggers automatic license revocation. The violating organization loses the right to:

- Use the "Calm Compass" name in marketing, documentation, or product branding.
- Claim alignment with Calm Compass values or principles.
- Represent their product or service as Calm Compass compliant.

Revocation is effective immediately upon verification and does not require prior notice, hearing, or appeal period.

### Public Misuse Log

Confirmed violations are logged in the Public Misuse Registry (referenced in E199, Enforcement Summit protocols). Violations are published with sufficient detail to enable the community to identify violating deployments, but with privacy protections for affected principals.

### Restoration

Organizations that have violated scope may petition for license restoration only after:

1. Complete cessation of prohibited use.
2. Remediation of any harm caused by the violation.
3. Third-party audit confirming cessation.
4. Formal retraining of all personnel on scope restrictions.
5. New implementer pledge signature.

Restoration is not automatic and may be denied if the violation was egregious or affected vulnerable populations.

## Dual-Use Tension and Community Commitment

Calm Compass operates a cryptographic and attestation system that, like all such systems, admits dual-use. Any attestation machinery can be misused. This scope statement represents our community commitment to refuse to enable specific, identified misuses. We cannot prevent all misuse; determined adversaries with sufficient technical capacity will find workarounds. What we CAN do is:

1. Withdraw the trademark and community standing from observed violators.
2. Publish violations publicly to enable community accountability.
3. Actively defend the scope through legal enforcement, technical architecture, and governance.

The scope statement is not a guarantee of safety; it is a statement of community values and a mechanism for accountability.

## Annual Scope Review

The Calm Witness Foundation ethics review board conducts an annual scope review (referenced in E80). At each review, the board may:

- Add new prohibited use cases based on observed harms or emerging deployment risks.
- Clarify existing restrictions with case examples or interpretive guidance.
- Remove restrictions only through supermajority vote and public comment period of 90 days.

Changes to the prohibited list are effective immediately upon publication and apply to all deployments, including those licensed before the amendment.

## International Applicability

Calm Compass scope is applied according to Calm Witness Foundation rules, which adopt a global ethics framework grounded in human dignity, non-discrimination, and irreversibility of harm. Deployments operating in jurisdictions where US ethics review has limited legal force are still subject to:

1. Trademark license terms (enforceable in all jurisdictions where the trademark is registered).
2. Community accountability mechanisms (public misuse logging).
3. Implementer pledge requirements (contractual conditions of collaboration).

Organizations in non-US jurisdictions that violate scope lose the right to use the "Calm Compass" trademark and are logged in the public misuse registry, regardless of local law.
