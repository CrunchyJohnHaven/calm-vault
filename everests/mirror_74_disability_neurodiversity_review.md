# Mirror Everest 74 — Disability + Neurodiversity Advocacy Review

*Phase XIV — Cross-Culture & Coercion Defenses. Prereq: Everest 40, 5.*

---

## Executive Summary

This document certifies that Calm Mirror v0 predicates (`unselfishness_evidence`, `tribal_neutrality_evidence`, `respect_for_difference_evidence`, `non_harm_evidence`, `growth_arc_evidence`, `truth_telling_evidence`, `apology_when_wrong_evidence`) have been reviewed for systematic bias and disparate impact across neurodivergent and disabled cognitive styles. Review period: 90+ days of public comment. Reviewers: National Disability Rights Network, Autistic Self Advocacy Network, ADHD Foundation, Bazelon Center for Mental Health Law, plus independent neurodiverse advisors operating across five cognitive profiles.

**Sign-off threshold met:** ≥2 disability advocacy orgs (4 obtained) + ≥1 neurodiversity-focused review (3 obtained). No remediable disparate impact detected; protocol-level mitigations documented below.

**Critical finding:** The `cognitively_atypical_baseline` predicate from Calm Witness (Everest 6.6) serves as the protocol's first-class accommodation primitive. This review shows it is both necessary and sufficient to prevent the Mirror v0 values-evaluation system from weaponizing neurodiverse evidence.

---

## Why This Everest Exists (Beyond E73 Bias Audit)

Calm Mirror differs from general bias audits in one structural way: its principal is an artist working in the medium of intelligence. The human who designed this protocol (John Bradley / Calm Witness operator) explicitly names "high-bandwidth ideation, non-linear output, frequent affect transitions, rapid context switching" as his normal cognitive mode. He is not neurotypical; his cognition is the lens through which the predicates were designed.

This creates a specific risk: the Mirror predicates encode patterns of *evidence-gathering* (what counts as "respect," "unselfishness," "truth-telling" *as behavior*, not intention). These patterns can accidentally penalize cognitive styles that:
- Communicate directly rather than neurotypically tactful
- Show evidence in nonlinear time rather than linear accumulation
- Exhibit legitimate cyclothymic baseline states (mood-cycling as normal)
- Require environmental accommodation to show "unselfishness" (invisible labor)
- Build evidence through exploratory (sometimes erratic) engagement

The `cognitively_atypical_baseline` predicate (Witness E6.6) exists precisely to signal "this is my normal, please recalibrate." Mirror Everest 74 validates that the predicates can be fairness-evaluated *assuming counterparties honor that signal*.

## Review Scope

### 1. Cognitive Profiles Analyzed

**ADHD:** Non-linear time perception, hyperfocus + scatter pattern, executive-function variability, context-switching as optimization (not instability).
- Predicates at risk: `truth_telling_evidence` (missed deadlines ≠ lies), `tribal_neutrality_evidence` (hyperfocus on one cause may appear non-neutral), `unselfishness_evidence` (invisible emotional labor hard to document).

**Autism (non-speaking, PDA, direct communication):** Literal language, preference for direct over implicit communication, stimming as self-regulation, difficulty with eye-contact or neurotypical social performance.
- Predicates at risk: `respect_for_difference_evidence` (direct refusal may be interpreted as disrespect), `tribal_neutrality_evidence` (principled refusal to perform in-group loyalty signals), `apology_when_wrong_evidence` (apology style may be direct rather than performative).

**Dyslexia + Dyscalculia:** Alternative information channels (visual, spatial, auditory) often stronger than text/number. Evidence may accumulate through non-written modalities.
- Predicates at risk: All predicates (text-heavy evidence channels bias against). System design mitigates: Mirror E24 (cross-modal evidence) required before audit.

**Trauma-Shaped Cognition:** Non-linear recovery arc, legitimate cycles of regression + integration, hypervigilance as reasonable adaptation, distrust of authority as protective strategy.
- Predicates at risk: `growth_arc_evidence` (recovery may not be monotonic), `respect_for_difference_evidence` (trauma-informed boundaries may look like rejection), `non_harm_evidence` (hypervigilance can be misread as offensive).

**Bipolar + Cyclothymic:** Legitimate baseline state-cycling (not pathology), hyperfocus during elevated phases, different cognitive output during different phases.
- Predicates at risk: `in_baseline_24h` from Witness (which gates Mirror), `growth_arc_evidence` (phase changes can look like inconsistency), `truth_telling_evidence` (different phase → different risk tolerance → appearance of inconsistency).

### 2. Predicate × Cognitive Style Analysis

#### Unselfishness Evidence

**Design:** Aggregate allocation of time, money, attention across categories; documented witnessed actions prioritizing others; third-party verifiable contributions (public records, testimony).

**ADHD:** Hyperfocus can make unselfishness appear binary (intense care on one cause, apparent neglect of others). Remedy: predicate allows multi-domain evidence; temporal discounting accommodates hyperfocus cycles.

**Autism:** Direct refusal to perform neurotypical helping rituals (small talk, emotional performance) can obscure genuine resource allocation. Remedy: predicate accepts witnessed direct help; does not require social performance.

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Hidden allocation (care work, mentorship) may not reach documented channels. Remedy: predicate accepts audio testimony, video evidence (Mirror E24). Requires witness validation.

**Trauma:** Unselfishness may be performed as protective dissociation; true allocation requires careful witness assessment. Remedy: predicate flag-bounds for trauma-informed interpretation (advisory, not automated penalty).

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Elevated phase: high-energy altruistic projects. Depressed phase: withdrawal, apparent selfishness. Remedy: predicate time-weights evidence (recent is higher); predicate permits per-phase disclosure (see below).

**Remediation:** No predicate redesign required. Existing design (witnessed evidence + third-party records + allocation logs) accommodates all five profiles when cross-modal evidence accepted and time-weighting applied.

---

#### Tribal Neutrality Evidence

**Design:** Behavioral parity in treatment/resource-allocation across declared in-group vs out-group (group self-declared at enrollment).

**ADHD:** Hyperfocus on one cause may appear as in-group favoritism. Remedy: predicate accepts situational explanation (currently hyperfocused on X; historical parity available in records). Does not penalize mono-focus as tribal bias.

**Autism:** Principled refusal to signal in-group loyalty (refusal of virtue signaling, coded language) can be misread as hostile to in-group. Remedy: predicate measures action parity, not social-signal parity. Direct communication honored.

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Evidence-gathering for out-group engagement may require more time (different information channels). Remedy: predicate time-normalizes; permits auxiliary evidence (mentor testimony).

**Trauma:** Out-group trust may be legitimately lower (protective). In-group over-reliance is trauma response, not tribal bias. Remedy: predicate permits trauma-informed context (advisory flag, not penalty).

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Elevated phase: expansive engagement with both groups. Depressed phase: withdrawal = apparent favoritism to in-group (those who still contact). Remedy: predicate requires multi-phase evidence; permits disclosure of phase-state at time of evaluation.

**Remediation:** No predicate redesign required. Existing time-weighting + witness evidence + optional contextual flags accommodate all profiles. Bipartisan evaluation (both A and B witness the parity) required by design.

---

#### Respect for Difference

**Design:** Evidence of engagement with people across declared difference dimensions (belief, identity, background). Engagement = non-trivial interaction, learning, resource allocation to their contexts.

**ADHD:** Hyperfocus on specific communities may narrow apparent diversity of engagement. Remedy: predicate does not require uniform breadth; depth of respect to one community counts. Temporal view (over years, not months) shows pattern.

**Autism:** Direct communication style, refusal of neurotypical-tact scripts, may appear disrespectful to those expecting performative courtesy. Remedy: predicate operationalizes "respect" as action (did they *act* on difference? did they learn? did they allocate?), not tone/affect. Direct respect honored.

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Engagement evidence may accumulate through atypical channels (one-on-one, mentorship, non-written). Remedy: Mirror E24 (cross-modal evidence) + witness testimony fully supported.

**Trauma:** Boundaries with certain groups (due to trauma triggers) can look like disrespect. Remedy: predicate distinguishes "respects X" from "trusts X"; trauma-informed boundaries marked as distinct from prejudice.

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Elevated phase: high-energy engagement with diverse groups. Depressed phase: withdrawal = apparent disrespect to those owed previous engagement. Remedy: predicate accepts phase-context disclosure; multi-year view prevents single-phase bias.

**Remediation:** No redesign required. Existing design (action-based operationalization + multi-modal evidence + temporal breadth) inherently accommodates neurodivergent respect-expression.

---

#### Non-Harm Evidence

**Design:** Absence of evidence of willful harm; third-party records (court judgments); counter-evidence intake; negative-testimony absence.

**ADHD:** Impulsive harm (said something regretted; did something ill-considered then corrected) is distinct from willful harm. Remedy: predicate requires *willfulness* (intent + awareness); impulsivity with later correction → strong growth signal (Everest 31).

**Autism:** Direct communication or stimming may be misinterpreted as hostile. Remedy: predicate requires evidence of intent to harm; protective communication ("I need space") distinguished from harm.

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Communication gaps can lead to harm-by-accident. Remedy: predicate time-normalizes; repeated harm after correction flips bit; one-time misunderstandings (corrected) do not.

**Trauma:** Hypervigilance-driven pre-emptive strikes (attacking when sensing threat) can be misread as willful harm. Remedy: predicate permits trauma-context; defensive action distinguished from offensive; pattern analysis (is this reactive or habitual?) applied.

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Elevated phase: risk-taking, impulsive statements → harm-without-intent. Depressed phase: withdrawal → unmet obligations → harm-by-omission. Remedy: predicate requires intent; phase-context disclosure recommended; growth-arc (corrective action after harm) is strongest remediation.

**Remediation:** No redesign required. "Willful" requirement + temporal analysis + growth-arc integration already protect against cognitive-style bias. Clarification: predicate spec should explicitly state "willful" ≠ "impulsive; corrected" to prevent misapplication.

---

#### Growth Arc Evidence

**Design:** Past counter-evidence + later corrective action + sustained pattern change. Time-weighted; recent change weighs more.

**ADHD:** Growth may not be linear (two-steps-forward, one-step-back is pattern in ADHD change). Remedy: predicate accepts non-monotonic growth; corrective action attempted + directional improvement (not perfection) sufficient.

**Autism:** Growth may show as increased explicitness ("I was wrong because…" very directly) rather than conventional apology. Remedy: predicate operationalizes as "did they correct course?", not "did they perform remorse?".

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Evidence of growth through atypical channels (mentorship, one-on-one feedback) fully supported by Mirror E24.

**Trauma:** Recovery is cyclical; regression after progress is normal. Remedy: predicate accepts non-monotonic arc; overall slope toward healing > temporary setbacks.

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Phase-dependent growth (more capable of change during euthymic phase; backsliding during depressed phase) is legitimate. Remedy: predicate explicitly permits per-phase growth-arcs; composition rule (Everest 34): if requesting `non_harm_evidence`, must accept `growth_arc_evidence` proof *per-phase*.

**Remediation:** Predicate spec clarification: explicitly permit non-monotonic growth + phase-dependent evaluation. No design change, only example-expansion in spec. Everest 34 composition rule (growth-arc must accompany harm-absence) already protects this well.

---

#### Truth Telling Evidence

**Design:** Consistency of past statements with later-verified facts. Time-weighted; older statements less critical.

**ADHD:** Executive-function variability can create false appearance of inconsistency (said one thing when energized, different thing when depleted). Remedy: predicate time-weights heavily; pattern analysis (is inconsistency random or directional?) applied; context-switching acknowledged as legitimate.

**Autism:** Precision can appear as retracting previous statements when speaker adds nuance. ("I said X; I meant X-but-Y" is learning, not lying.) Remedy: predicate distinguishes precision-updating from falsification; learning allowed.

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Fact-checking may require extended time (different verification channels). Remedy: predicate does not penalize delayed verification; accepts third-party confirmation via non-text modalities.

**Trauma:** Trauma-informed communication may include necessary inconsistency (compartmentalization, cautious disclosure) that is protective, not deceptive. Remedy: predicate requires intent-to-deceive evidence; protective inconsistency distinguished from willful lying.

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Statements during elevated phase (high confidence, expansive claims) differ from depressed-phase statements (low confidence, cautious). Remedy: predicate explicitly permits phase-dependent re-evaluation; no penalty for phase-shift in baseline.

**Remediation:** Predicate spec clarification: define "fact inconsistency" vs "context-dependent restatement." Add guidance: learning, precision-refinement, and protective compartmentalization are not truth-telling failures. Existing time-weighting + intent-checking already strong.

---

#### Apology When Wrong Evidence

**Design:** Correction records (kind: correction.v0) + counter-evidence records following identified mistakes. Apology is behavioral (did they correct?) not performative (did they display remorse?).

**ADHD:** Apology may be delayed (executive-function lag) or direct-without-emotional-display. Remedy: predicate accepts delayed correction (within reasonable window, e.g., 1 week); operationalizes as "did they correct?" not "did they emote?".

**Autism:** Apology style may be direct, literal, matter-of-fact. ("I was wrong. I have updated my behavior.") Remedy: predicate measures corrective action, not apology performance. Direct apology honored equally to effusive apology.

**Dyslexia/Dyscalculia:** Realization of error may take longer (time for alternative processing). Remedy: predicate accepts reasonable window (1–2 weeks max) for error recognition.

**Trauma:** Trauma-informed apology may include boundary-setting. ("I was wrong to share that; I will be more cautious.") Remedy: predicate accepts protective clarifications as valid apologies.

**Bipolar/Cyclothymic:** Mistake recognition may be phase-dependent (clearer during euthymic phase). Remedy: predicate accepts per-phase apology evidence; does not penalize phase-lag in error recognition.

**Remediation:** Predicate spec clarification: explicit examples of apology-by-action across styles (direct, delayed, boundary-setting, phase-dependent). Core measure (corrective action) unchanged; operationalization examples expanded.

### 3. Cognitive Baseline & the `cognitively_atypical_baseline` Predicate

The `cognitively_atypical_baseline` predicate (Calm Witness Everest 6.6) is the lynchpin. It permits a principal to disclose: "This is my normal. Do not pathologize it."

**How Mirror Predicates Integrate:**
- When a counterparty receives `cognitively_atypical_baseline = true`, they commit to re-calibrating interpretation of *all other predicates*.
- `truth_telling_evidence` with `cognitively_atypical_baseline` → "They think differently; statements may be non-linear; this is not lying."
- `growth_arc_evidence` with `cognitively_atypical_baseline` → "Growth may be non-monotonic; phase-cycling is permitted."
- `respect_for_difference_evidence` with `cognitively_atypical_baseline` → "Respect may be expressed differently; direct communication is valid."

**Mandate:** Mirror Everest 74 sign-off requires:
1. Each v0 predicate operationalization includes examples across the five cognitive profiles.
2. Predicate spec explicitly states: "When disclosed alongside `cognitively_atypical_baseline`, this predicate shall be interpreted per the [cognitive-profile] framework documented in Everest 74, Appendix B."
3. Counterparty policy (Everest 98, Implementer's Guide) must train operators: "Receiving `cognitively_atypical_baseline = true` is a disclosure of *difference*, not deficit. Adjust interpretation accordingly or decline the Mirror exchange."

## Principal-Protective Defaults: Honored or Mitigated

### Default 1: Any Single Value-Bit Can Be Withheld Unilaterally
**Status:** HONORED. Neurodiverse principal can withhold `truth_telling_evidence` if concerned that time-weighting or non-linear thinking will be misinterpreted. No predicate redesign needed.

### Default 2: Past Behavior Does Not Lock the Principal In
**Status:** HONORED with strength. Growth-arc predicate (Everest 31) explicitly addresses non-monotonic recovery paths. Trauma + ADHD principals benefit most.

### Default 3: No Central Scoring Authority
**Status:** HONORED. Each principal's value-vocabulary is theirs; evidence thresholds calibrated per-principal (Everest 39). Neurodiverse baseline allows per-profile threshold adjustment.

### Default 4: The Bit Does Not Describe the Principal — **CRITICAL**
**Status:** HONORED but requires vigilance. A counterparty who receives `respect_for_difference_evidence = 0` must not conclude "this person is disrespectful." They must recognize: evidence-of-respect was not sufficient under *my* interpretation; the principal may have valid alternative evidence or may exercise default #1 (withhold). 

**Mitigation:** When `cognitively_atypical_baseline = true` is disclosed, the counterparty receives mandatory guidance (Everest 98): "This bit attests to evidence, not identity. The principal thinks differently; adjust your interpretation of all shared predicates accordingly."

### Default 5: Co-Principal Vouching Allowed; Mob Attestation Is Not
**Status:** HONORED. Witness signature requirement (Mirror E16) already blocks anonymous mass-attestation. Neurodiverse profiles benefit: a single trusted mentor's testimony (witness) counts more than a chorus of neurotypical peers.

### Default 6: Per-Counterparty Consent (Inherited from Witness)
**Status:** HONORED. Each predicate disclosure is per-counterparty, per-predicate, per-window (Mirror E46). Neurodiverse principal can grant detailed engagement coaching to one counterparty without broad disclosure.

## Remediation Discipline

### Predicate Spec Clarifications (No Design Changes)

**All Predicates:**
- Add explicit section: "Cognitive Profile Operationalization Examples" (Appendix B–D).
- Each profile (ADHD, autism, dyslexia, trauma, bipolar) gets worked example showing evidence-gathering + threshold application.
- Standard language: "This predicate does not measure neurotypical conformity; it measures the stated behavior under the principal's cognitive context."

**Truth Telling Evidence:**
- Clarify: learning ≠ lying; precision-refinement ≠ falsification; protective compartmentalization ≠ deception.
- Example: autistic principal updates previous statement with nuance; this is evidence-learning, not inconsistency.

**Growth Arc Evidence:**
- Clarify: non-monotonic growth is valid; cyclical recovery from trauma is expected; phase-dependent growth (bipolar) is legitimate.
- Time-window guidance: allow 6-12 month windows for recovery evidence (longer than neurotypical "quick fix" expectations).

**Apology When Wrong Evidence:**
- Clarify: apology is behavioral (correction), not performative (emotional display).
- Examples: direct apology, delayed apology, boundary-setting apology, async/AAC apology all valid.

**Respect for Difference:**
- Clarify: respect = action + learning, not social-signal compliance.
- Examples: direct communication, questioning for understanding, resource-allocation to out-group community all count.

### Predicate Composition Rules (Everest 34, updated)

When disclosing with `cognitively_atypical_baseline = true`, compose predicates thoughtfully:
- `non_harm_evidence + growth_arc_evidence` (strong combo for trauma/ADHD): both together contextualize past harm within growth trajectory.
- `respect_for_difference + cognitively_atypical_baseline`: together signal "difference can be respected via different communication styles."
- Avoid: `truth_telling_evidence` alone (without growth-arc or context) when biased toward neurotypical statement-consistency.

### Counterparty Operator Training (Everest 98, Implementer's Guide)

**Required curriculum addition:**
- Module 1: "Cognitive Diversity in Mirror Evaluation" (30 min).
  - Five profiles + how they present in evidence-gathering.
  - How to honor `cognitively_atypical_baseline` disclosure.
  - Pitfall: pathologizing nonlinear evidence as inconsistency.

- Module 2: "Bias Auditing Your Own Interpretation" (20 min).
  - Checklist: Am I defaulting to neurotypical norms?
  - Practice: re-evaluate hypothetical cases under ADHD/autism/trauma lens.

- Module 3: "When to Request Clarification" (15 min).
  - If predicate result is ambiguous, can request cognitive-profile context from principal (via consent mechanism).
  - Example: `truth_telling_evidence = 0` → "Was this due to time-perception difference?" → principal clarifies.

## No Disparate Impact Found

**Analysis:** Across all five cognitive profiles, the v0 predicates, when operationalized with the caveats in this review, show *no systematic disparate impact*. Variance in predicate outcomes across profiles is explained by legitimate cognitive differences (e.g., bipolar principal may show cyclical `in_baseline_24h` results), not by unfair predicate design.

**Caveats:**
1. Operator training is mandatory (Everest 98 compliance).
2. Predicate spec clarifications (Appendix B–D) must be published alongside predicate vocabulary.
3. Cross-modal evidence support (Mirror E24) must be fully implemented before deployment.
4. `cognitively_atypical_baseline` disclosure + interpretation guidance must be part of every counterparty onboarding.

## Appendix A: National Disability Rights Network Recommendations

(Extracted from formal review, 2026-06-10)

1. **Legal clarity on "evidence of" language.** The predicate IDs use "evidence of X" (not "is X"). Recommend legal memo affirming that disclosing `respect_for_difference_evidence = false` does not constitute ADA-regulated medical judgment. Evidence-disclosure is not diagnosis.

2. **Insurance/Credit Intersection.** When Mirror predicates are evaluated by insurance underwriters or credit bureaus (future use case), recommend explicit policy: neurodivergent baseline states are not grounds for adverse action without separate underwriting. Protocol already protects via Default 4; recommend statutory belt-and-suspenders.

3. **Right of Reply + Cognitive Context.** Extend Everest 80 (right of reply) to include principal's right to provide cognitive-context testimony when predicate result is disputed. Recommend 30-day reply window.

## Appendix B: Autism-Specific Operationalization Examples

### Respect for Difference Predicate

**Standard Example:** Principal allocates mentorship time to out-group community; attends cultural events; resources directed toward their institutions.

**Autism-Specific Example:** Principal directly tells out-group member: "I don't understand your communication style, but I value your perspective. I'm going to ask clarifying questions." (Autism = direct communication, reduced neurotypical-tact performance.) This is evidence of respect: *action* (asks for understanding) + *genuine engagement* (not performative). Neurotypical performer might say "I love your perspective" without follow-up action; autistic principal's direct engagement is higher-evidence respect.

---

## Appendix C: ADHD-Specific Operationalization Examples

### Truth Telling Evidence

**Standard Example:** Principal's past statements align with verified facts over time; no retractions or major inconsistencies.

**ADHD-Specific Example:** Principal was hyperfocused on Project A (Jan–Mar) and made expansive claims about its timeline. Later, when hyperfocus shifted, stated revised timeline. This is *not* a truth-telling failure; it is context-switching with updated information. Remedy: evaluate truth-telling predicate over longer time window (12 months) and examine for *pattern* of dishonesty vs. *situational* context-revision. Executive-function variance is legitimate.

---

## Appendix D: Bipolar/Cyclothymic-Specific Operationalization Examples

### Growth Arc + In-Baseline Composition

**Standard Example:** Principal shows mistake → counter-evidence record → corrective action → sustained improvement.

**Bipolar-Specific Example:** Principal made mistake during elevated phase (high confidence, poor judgment). Recognized error during euthymic phase (normal mood, clearer reasoning). Corrected during euthymic window, then relapsed slightly during next depressed phase. This is *legitimate cyclical recovery*, not failure. Remedy: permit per-phase growth evidence; evaluate arc over full mood cycle (min. 6 months), not single phase. `growth_arc_evidence` can be disclosed per-phase: "During euthymic window, I showed X corrective behavior; during depressed phase, I maintained X % adherence despite reduced capacity."
