## I. OVERVIEW

The founding board sets the protocol's normative trajectory. Getting board composition right is the difference between a Foundation that holds the refusal-floor under pressure and one that doesn't.

Calm Witness is not a neutral technology. It is a cryptographic refusal system designed to protect principals from extraction, surveillance, and coercion by counterparties with asymmetric power (employers, insurers, lenders, state actors). The board must be composed of directors whose institutional interest is in **maintaining that refusal**, not weakening it.

The recruiting challenge: find individuals who have a track record of refusing institutional capture, published work in related domains, and the willingness to be the "no" vote when capital, partnerships, or mission creep demand it. The founding board must embody what it governs—cryptographic refusal applied to governance itself.

---

## II. COMPOSITION PRINCIPLES

The board shall consist of:

**Size:** 7 directors (minimum 7, maximum 11 per bylaws; 7 is the founding configuration).

**Independence:** ≥5 directors shall have no material financial interest in commercial Calm Witness deployments or related startups.

**Coverage (mandatory per bylaws Article II §2.2):**
- ≥1 cryptographer with peer-reviewed publications in post-quantum, threshold, or ZK cryptography.
- ≥1 disability-rights advocate or neurodiversity expert with lived experience or professional advocacy background.
- ≥1 non-founder working principal (e.g., open-source maintainer, researcher, community representative).
- ≥1 ethicist with published work in AI ethics, value alignment, or privacy.
- ≥1 cross-cultural reviewer (anthropologist, global-south researcher, or multicultural-competency expert).
- ≥1 jurist or governance expert with experience in standards bodies, open-source law, or nonprofit governance.

**Founder seat:** John Bradley holds one director seat for the duration of his involvement, subject to removal only for cause and with 12 months' written notice (per bylaws Article II §2.3).

---

## III. RECRUITMENT CRITERIA

Each candidate must demonstrate:

**Track record of refusing institutional capture:**
- Has publicly declined lucrative partnerships or funding that conflicted with stated principles.
- Has resigned from organizations or boards when pressured to compromise on core values.
- Has documented a willingness to be a dissenting vote; cited instances where they stood alone.

**Published work in adjacent areas:**
- At least one substantial publication, policy position, open-source project, or court filing that evidences expertise in their coverage area.
- Ideally, work that touches on the friction points Calm Witness addresses (privacy under asymmetric power, cognitive liberty, algorithmic governance, cryptographic standards).

**Willingness to be the "no" vote:**
- Can articulate a threshold for refusal that is *non-negotiable*, not contingent on board sentiment.
- Understands that protecting the refusal floor is the Foundation's primary duty, not a secondary constraint.
- Has experience in peer-review or standards-setting contexts where they have blocked proposals, published dissents, or advocated for higher bars.

**Conflict discipline:**
- Can credibly commit to full disclosure of financial interests, institutional affiliations, and consulting relationships.
- Demonstrates independence from commercial Calm Witness implementations or startups in the protocol space.
- Willing to recuse from voting if a conflict arises.

## IV. TERM DISCIPLINE AND ROTATION

**Initial stagger (3-year cycle):**
- 3 directors serve initial 18-month terms, ending June 30, 2028.
- 3 directors serve initial 3-year terms, ending June 30, 2029.
- Founder seat (1) serves indefinitely until voluntary exit or removal for cause.

**Rotation rule:** By the end of Year 3 (June 30, 2029), ≥2 of the original 3 18-month seats shall be open for re-nomination or rotation.

**No founder-permanent-seat:** The Founder Seat does not exempt John Bradley from the rotation principle if he continues. If John remains beyond 6 years, a Board resolution by 2/3 supermajority is required to extend him past that point.

**Term limits:** No director may serve more than two consecutive 18-month terms (3 years total) without a 12-month sabbatical (except Founder Seat, which is not subject to this limit but is subject to removal for cause).

## V. THE REFUSAL-FLOOR PRESERVATION TEST

Every candidate must publicly commit to the refusal-floor forfeit list:

**Scope-Statement Prohibited Uses (non-negotiable):**
1. Law-enforcement surveillance or governmental intelligence.
2. Employment screening, evaluation, or termination.
3. Insurance underwriting, premium setting, or claims adjudication.
4. Lending, credit-scoring, or creditworthiness assessment.
5. Medical diagnosis, clinical decision-making, or treatment selection.
6. Family-court, custody, or child-welfare proceedings.
7. Immigration adjudication or border-control actions.
8. Behavioral-prediction systems or risk-assessment scores.
9. Marketing, advertising targeting, or consumer profiling.
10. Aggregation across principals (population-level inference).

**Candidate Acceptance Condition:**

Before appointment, each director-nominee must sign a written commitment stating:

*"I affirm that I understand the Calm Witness Scope Statement §2 prohibited-uses list and commit to affirmatively refusing any funding, partnership, operational decision, or predicate proposal that would facilitate use of Calm Witness in a prohibited context. I accept that this duty is non-delegable: I may not abstain from this duty, nor may the Board override it through majority vote. I understand that accepting this commitment is a condition of my Board seat, and that violation of this commitment constitutes grounds for immediate removal with cause."*

This commitment is binding on the initial board and all successor boards per bylaws Article III §3.4 (Refusal Floor).

## VII. ACCEPTANCE CONDITIONS (T-E242.1 THROUGH T-E242.5)

### T-E242.1: Diversity Profile Verification
- At time of seating, verify that the 7-person board satisfies all six mandatory coverage areas (cryptography, disability-rights, working principal, ethicist, cross-cultural, jurist).
- Board roster shall be published on the Foundation website with each director's name, affiliation, coverage area, and term-end date.
- If any coverage area is vacant, the Board has 90 days to nominate and seat a replacement.

### T-E242.2: Refusal-Floor Acceptance Certification
- Each director (including the Founder) signs the refusal-floor commitment (§V above) before or at the first Board meeting.
- The commitment document is stored in the Foundation's permanent records per bylaws Article IV.
- Failure to sign, or public statement contradicting the commitment, constitutes grounds for removal with cause.

### T-E242.3: Conflict-of-Interest Disclosure
- Each director files a Conflict-of-Interest Disclosure (per bylaws Article III §3.1) identifying:
  - Employment, board membership, or equity holdings in organizations deploying or building Calm Witness infrastructure.
  - Consulting relationships or contracts related to the protocol.
  - Family relationships to commercial stakeholders.
  - Any prior funding received from organizations with interest in the protocol.
- Disclosures are maintained confidentially by the Foundation's General Counsel and reviewed annually.
- Material conflicts trigger recusal from specific Board votes (per bylaws Article III §3.2).

### T-E242.4: Background Verification
- Each director consents to a basic background check (criminal conviction, litigation history) conducted via CredexAI or equivalent service.
- The Foundation does not conduct invasive surveillance; the check is limited to public records relevant to fiduciary fitness.
- Results are confidential; only material findings (e.g., felony convictions related to fraud, breach of trust) are grounds for rejection.

### T-E242.5: First Board Meeting & Officer Election
- The initial Board convenes within 30 days of all seven directors being seated and committed.
- At the first meeting, the Board:
  - Elects a Chair (typically a non-founder director for independence).
  - Appoints an Executive Director (may or may not be a Board member).
  - Appoints a Secretary (records management) and Treasurer (financial oversight).
  - Approves the initial annual budget and audit-panel compensation schedule.
  - Publishes the first Quarterly Transparency Report.
  - Schedules the audit-panel seating process (target: within 60 days).
