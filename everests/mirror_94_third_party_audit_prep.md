# Everest 94 — Mirror Third-Party Audit Prep

*Phase XV — Engineering Reliability. Prereq: Everests 73 (Bias Audit), 87 (Rust Production Implementation).*

---

## What this summit ships

An audit-ready packet of artifacts that a reputable third-party cryptographic audit firm (Trail of Bits, NCC Group, Cure53, Doyensec) can pick up cold and execute against. The packet is what makes the audit *startable* without back-channel onboarding — the actual audit run is operational and tracked separately.

This summit's deliverable is the **packet**. The audit itself and its remediation are operational milestones that follow.

## Audit packet contents

The packet is a tarball + an index document. Twelve items:

1. **Protocol spec.** `CALM_MIRROR_EVERESTS_100.md` + all referenced summit deliverables (E1, E5-7, E10, E20-40, E41-55).
2. **Threat model.** Consolidated from Everests 1 (Problem Statement), 9 (Failure-Mode Catalogue), 69 (Adversarial Robustness Study), 75 (Mob-Attestation Defense), 77 (Coercion-Resistance Proof).
3. **Cryptographic spec.** Everests 56 (Pedersen Commitments), 57 (2PC Framework), 58 (Secure Intersection), 59 (ZK Proof of MPC Correctness), 60 (Range Proofs), 61 (Cross-Principal Binding), 63 (Side-Channel Resistance), 65 (Aggregate Alignment-Score Proof).
4. **Code.** `calm-mirror` Rust crate at the audit-frozen commit; SHA-pinned dependency tree; Cargo.lock attached.
5. **SBOM.** Full software bill of materials in SPDX format. Generated by `cargo cyclonedx` + manual review.
6. **Conformance vectors.** All published vector sets across Everests 23, 37, 58, 60, 65, 70 — test coverage for intersection computation, witness binding, MPC output, range proofs, aggregate scores, and cross-implementation compliance.
7. **Fuzzer history.** Last 90 days of fuzzer corpus (E90: adversarial fuzzers on MPC + ZK pipeline); any historical crashes with severity classification + fix commits.
8. **Prior bug history.** Internal-found bugs since Mirror project inception, with severity + fix commit + lessons-learned write-ups.
9. **Ethics review findings.** E8 panel sign-offs on predicates (E5); recommendations from E73 (bias audit) and E74 (disability/neurodiversity review); documented mitigations.
10. **Bias audit findings.** Third-party academic + advocacy panel report on systematic bias in v0 predicate set; gaps across cultural contexts.
11. **Disability + neurodiversity review.** Sign-offs from ≥2 disability advocacy organizations + ≥1 neurodiversity-focused review on fairness across cognitive styles (E74).
12. **Multi-jurisdiction legal memos.** Summaries from US / EU / UK / CA / JP / IN / BR covering intersection of values-disclosure with employment, housing, insurance law (E79).
13. **Designated point of contact.** Calm Foundation engineer available within 24 hours of an auditor question during the audit window; published rotation schedule.

## Audit scope (in scope / out of scope)

**In scope:**
- Cryptographic correctness of Pedersen commitments, two-party MPC, and ZK proofs over value vectors.
- MPC soundness: secure computation of intersection bits without information leakage.
- ZK properties: completeness, soundness, zero-knowledge for alignment proofs.
- Side-channel resistance (timing, memory access patterns, allocator behavior on MPC critical paths).
- Memory safety (Rust's safety guarantees + audit of `unsafe` blocks in crypto primitives).
- Fuzzer coverage assessment (does the adversarial fuzzer corpus exercise one-side-lies, both-sides-collude, coercion attacks?).
- Principal-protective-default enforcement (withhold-any-bit, growth-arc composition rule, per-counterparty consent).
- Cross-principal binding proof (disclosure binds to both principals' chain heads; neither can swap chains post-facto).
- Fairness across populations (predicate evaluation robustness to demographic variation).
- Consent calculus implementation (Everests 46-47, per-counterparty disclosure precision).

**Out of scope:**
- Behavioral-evidence acquisition science (how to reliably measure allocation-evidence, witness-action veracity — separate Everest 14-16 implementation audit).
- Third-party empirical FAR/FRR studies on predicate discrimination (separate Everest 69 engagement with academic partners).
- Governance / ethics-panel composition (separate political-risk engagement).
- Production-deployment operational risk (separate engagement with operational consulting).
- Cross-cultural validation (Everest 71 is research, not subject to security audit).

## Engagement terms (recommended for the contract)

- **Black-box-first.** Auditor begins with the packet only; spec-clarification questions logged.
- **Then white-box.** After 1 week, auditor moves to source-with-context. Foundation engineer joins for source-level walk-throughs.
- **Open report.** The audit report is published openly, with Foundation responses inline. Critical / high findings include the fix commit hash.
- **No NDA on findings.** Auditor's findings are publishable from day one. Foundation may request a 30-day delay only for unmitigated critical issues, with a hard ceiling of 90 days.

These terms are non-standard for security audits; the openness premium is what makes Calm Mirror a credible open standard for principal-protective values-alignment.

## Acceptance test

**T-94.1 (packet exists).** All 13 packet items present as a single tarball + an index doc.

**T-94.2 (auditor cold-start).** A pilot auditor (internal volunteer or vetted external) starts the audit using only the packet; logs questions during the first 48 hours; answers from the index doc resolve ≥ 80% of questions.

**T-94.3 (SBOM correctness).** SBOM matches the actual dependency tree of the audit-frozen commit (verified via `cargo cyclonedx --output-format json` regenerated and compared).

**T-94.4 (live test infra working).** Auditor exercises the dev infrastructure end-to-end within the first 4 hours: pairwise alignment computation → per-counterparty disclosure → verification.

**T-94.5 (publicly-committed remediation policy).** Calm Foundation publishes its commitment to fix all "critical" and "high" findings before v1.0, with a documented remediation timeline.

**T-94.6 (auditor candidate list ready).** At least 3 reputable audit firms identified, scoped, and quoted; one selected for the first engagement.
