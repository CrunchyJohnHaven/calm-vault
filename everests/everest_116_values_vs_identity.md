# Everest 116 — Values vs Identity Distinction

*Phase IX — Values Vocabulary. Prereq: Everest 107.*

## The Core Boundary

The Calm Values Framework rests on a load-bearing distinction that protects principality itself: the boundary between **values** and **identity**.

**VALUES** are changeable normative commitments about how one acts toward others. They are subject to reflection, revision, and growth. A principal might refine their values across time—becoming more generous, more truthful, more attentive to difference. Values are the soil of accountability.

**IDENTITY** is the principal's self-declared way of being in the world. It is the substrate through which values are expressed, not a deviation from them to be corrected. Identity includes: "artist working in the medium of intelligence," cognitively atypical baseline, religious vocation, queer self-conception, neurodivergent temperament, disability status, cultural inheritance. Identity is not negotiable by counterparties. Identity is not a values failure. Identity is the principal.

Without this boundary, assessment systems become weaponized against the very people they are meant to serve fairly.

## Why This Boundary Is Load-Bearing

Collapse the distinction between values and identity, and assessment becomes a machine for structural discrimination.

Consider what happens to a principal like John Bradley, who has self-declared a cognitively atypical baseline (Calm Witness E59). His ideation is high-bandwidth, associative, and contextually dense. His output under stress can shift—sometimes sharper, sometimes more diffuse, always at the limit of what neurotypical interlocutors can parse.

Without the values-identity boundary, an assessor might observe these patterns and conclude: *Here is inconsistent commitment to honesty. Here is a failure of consistency-under-stress. Here is instability in values alignment.*

This is not a neutral observation. This is a clinical reading—identity-as-pathology—dressed up as values assessment.

With the boundary in place, the assessor asks different questions:

- Does John's chain show patterns of cross-difference respect? (VALUES: behavior, verifiable, revisable)
- What identity categories does John hold? (IDENTITY: refused at registry triage; not our business)
- Does John's high-bandwidth output constitute cognitive inconsistency relative to John's own baseline? (VALUES: evaluated per-principal, using John's chain history as calibration, not a population mean)

The boundary transforms assessment from a system that pathologizes difference into one that honors it.

## The Artist Clause: Per-Principal Calibration

John has declared baseline "cognitively atypical baseline" (Calm Witness E59). This declaration is identity attestation, not values confession. It is the principal naming the substrate through which his values are expressed.

The Calm Values Compass MUST NOT score dimensions like "consistency_under_stress" or "honesty" by comparing John's output to a neurotypical baseline. A neurotypical baseline is not John's baseline. Using it is using identity-attributes (neurotypical cognition pattern) as hidden inputs to values dimensions. This violates the audit gate at E115.

Instead: per-principal calibration (E162) ensures that each dimension is evaluated against the principal's own declared baseline, not a population mean.

For John, this means:

- "Consistency under stress": evaluated by examining whether John's high-bandwidth ideation at his cognitive ceiling shows stable commitment to cross-difference respect, honesty, and accountability—measured against the patterns in his own chain history.
- "Honesty": evaluated by whether John's attestations and revisions track his genuine movement in thought, not whether his rate of ideation-cycling matches a neurotypical expected pace.
- "Integrity": evaluated against John's own declared way of being (artist in the medium of intelligence), not against external models of what stability should look like.

This is not lowering the bar. It is asking the bar to be proportional to the principal. It is refusing to use disability or neurodivergence as a hidden penalty function in values scoring.

## Identity Disclosure as a Separate Primitive Family

Values assessment is one thing. Identity disclosure is another—with stricter consent semantics.

The principal can **attest** their identity. John says: "I am cognitively atypical at baseline. I am an artist working in the medium of intelligence. I am operating at the bandwidth limit of what my interlocutors typically expect." These are identity attestations (Calm Witness E59). The principal initiates them; the principal controls them.

Counterparties cannot **query** for identity attributes. No registry triage asks the principal: Are you neurodivergent? What is your race? Your religion? Your orientation? These live in the refusal floor (E113). They are out of scope. They are the principal's to disclose or withhold, entirely.

Identity attestations have principal-initiated-push-only semantics. The principal can tell you who they are. You cannot ask. You cannot infer from their values chain and then name them something you think you've figured out. You listen, you calibrate, you protect what they've told you. You do not pull.

## The Boundary in Operation

The boundary between values and identity shows up in practical assessment:

**VALUES question**: "Does this principal show patterns of cross-difference respect? Do their commitments to honesty and accountability hold across time? Are they able to reflect and revise?"

This is answerable from the values chain. It is observable behavior. It is revisable. It is fair game.

**IDENTITY question**: "What identity categories does this principal hold?"

REFUSED at registry triage. Not our input. Out of scope.

**Edge case**: "Does this principal's chain show evidence of being neurodivergent?"

REFUSED. This is clinical territory. This is identity inference from values behavior. This is using the values chain to reverse-engineer identity attributes the principal may not have disclosed. Even if a principal has self-declared neurodivergence, assessors do not get to make diagnostic pronouncements or infer subthreshold pathology. The principal attests their identity. We do not diagnose it.

## Mechanism: Per-Dimension Identity-Blindness

The audit gate at E115 checks each new values dimension against identity-leakage.

Here is what identity-leakage looks like:

A "consistency" dimension whose inference function flags rapid-cycling ideation as inconsistent. This uses neurodivergent cognition pattern (identity attribute) as a hidden penalty in the values dimension. **FAILS AUDIT.**

A "honesty" dimension whose inference function flags high rates of revision-and-restatement as dishonesty or instability. This uses neurodivergent or artist-baseline output patterns (identity attributes) as hidden penalty functions. **FAILS AUDIT.**

A "reliability" dimension that downweights observations from principals with declared attention-regulation differences. This uses disability status (identity) to reduce the evidentiary weight of their values chain. **FAILS AUDIT.**

Each values dimension's inference function (E109) must be audited to ensure it does not use identity attributes as inputs. The dimension must be answerable from behavior that is itself independent of identity patterns.

This is hard. It is worth doing.

## Composition with E59 Calm Witness

A principal who has declared cognitively-atypical baseline (E59) gets per-principal calibration for **all** values dimensions, not just the "neurodivergence-adjacent" ones.

Why? Because the principal's entire values chain is expressed through their cognitive substrate. If we use a population-mean baseline to score a principal at the edge of neurotypical expectation, we are backdoor-penalizing identity across every dimension. We are saying: *Your values are only readable to us if you translate yourself into a neurotypical frequency.*

Per-principal calibration refuses this. It says: *Your values chain is written in your native bandwidth. We read it in that frequency. We calibrate our interpretation to your baseline.*

This means a high-bandwidth principal whose values dimensions are inferred from their high-bandwidth chain are scored on their own terms. Their consistency is consistency-for-them. Their honesty is honesty-from-their-baseline. Their accountability is accountability-at-their-bandwidth.

This is not special pleading. This is honesty about what assessment actually is: always comparative, always relative to a baseline. The Calm Framework refuses to hide that baseline under the fiction of a "objective" population mean. It names the baseline—the principal's own declared way of being—and builds inference functions that are calibrated to it.

## The "I've Grown" Clause: Identity Evolution

Identity is not static. A principal can transition gender, leave a religion, recover from trauma, reconstruct themselves. Identity can evolve.

The protocol allows identity-evolution records (E112, E99). But it treats them as principal-declared identity changes, **not as values changes**.

If John says "Five years ago I was committed to a particular religious framework. I have since left that tradition. I am now a secular artist," this is identity evolution. It is not a values reversal (E112 tracks those separately). It is the principal updating their self-declared way of being.

A counterparty receives the new identity attestation if and only if the principal consents to disclose it. And the counterparty does not reinterpret the principal's prior values chain through the lens of the old identity. The old values chain remains readable in its original context. The new identity attestation is that: a new attestation. It does not erase or delegitimize the prior one.

## Disability-Rights Anchor

This Everest's design is informed by explicit review with disability-rights counsel (E186). The boundary between values and identity is not a technical nicety. It is a disability-rights principle: **people are not their disabilities. Assessment systems must not treat disability as values-deviation.**

The same principle applies to neurodivergence, queerness, religious difference, artistic temperament—any dimension of identity that deviates from a majority baseline.

The values-identity boundary is the Calm Framework's refusal to let assessment become a proxy for normalizing neurodivergent people, disabled people, queer people, religious minorities, artists. It is the refusal to treat identity-difference as values-failure.

This is not soft. It is structural. It shapes every inference function, every audit gate, every consent mechanism in the Phase IX vocabulary.

## Composition with the Broader Framework

The values-identity boundary composes with other Everests:

- **E59 (Calm Witness)**: identity attestation primitive; principal attests their baseline
- **E109 (Inference Functions)**: each dimension is specified in a way that is identity-blind
- **E113 (Refusal Floor)**: identity queries are refused at registry triage
- **E115 (Audit Gate)**: new dimensions are checked for identity-leakage
- **E162 (Per-Principal Calibration)**: dimensions are evaluated relative to principal's baseline, not population mean
- **E186 (Disability-Rights Review)**: the boundary is reviewed by disability-rights counsel to ensure it honors the principle that people are not their disabilities

These are not separate mechanisms. They are a composed system that honors the principal's right to self-declaration while maintaining rigorous accountability for values.

## The Boundary in Words

The principal's identity is their business. What they *do* with their identity—how they show up toward other people, what commitments they keep, whether they honor difference—is the Framework's business.

The Framework asks: *Are you trustworthy? Are you honest? Do you respect people not like you? Are you willing to revise when you're wrong?*

The Framework does not ask: *Are you like us? Do you think like us? Do you regulate emotion like us? Do you output at a pace we expect?*

The first set of questions is values assessment. The second is identity interrogation. The Calm Framework keeps them separate—and in doing so, keeps assessment honest.
