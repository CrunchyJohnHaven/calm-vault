# Everest 215 — Treaty-Grade Governance Preliminary Draft + Multi-Stakeholder Convening

*Phase XIV — Standards & Policy. Prereq: Everest 214.* **DESIGN-BAGGED (pending first convening + first treaty article signature).**

The Calm Stack will be invoked across jurisdictions. When a U.S. principal interacts with a counterparty operating under EU AI Act, UK ICO oversight, Japan PPC oversight, or China CAC oversight, **whose rules govern the disclosure semantics?** A protocol-level answer alone is insufficient; the legitimate-authority question must be answered at the governance layer, not silently embedded in code.

This summit drafts the **first treaty article** defining how Calm Stack governance interacts with state authority, plus the **first multi-stakeholder convening** charter.

## §1 — The treaty article (preliminary draft)

**Article [N]: Cooperative-AI Protocol Family — Governance Interaction with State Authority**

*Subject to negotiation by the signatory parties; this is a starting draft for discussion at the first multi-stakeholder convening (per §2 below).*

### Section 1 — Definitions

For purposes of this Article:

- **"Cooperative-AI protocol family"** means any cryptographic primitive suite (including but not limited to the Calm Stack — Pact, Witness, Tenancy, Compass, Concord — and successor families) that enables autonomous AI agents to verify alignment of mission, state, or values across jurisdictions, in a manner that:
  - Does not require any single party to disclose the underlying material to any counterparty;
  - Is operated under a published refusal floor of categories the protocol declines to measure;
  - Is published under permissive open-source licensing;
  - Is governed by a multi-stakeholder body with disability-rights, cognitive-liberties, and affected-community representation.

- **"Signatory jurisdiction"** means a State Party to this treaty, including its competent authorities for AI safety, data protection, and consumer protection.

- **"Cross-jurisdiction disclosure"** means a Calm Stack disclosure transmitted from a principal in one Signatory Jurisdiction to a counterparty in another.

### Section 2 — Recognition

2.1 The Signatory Parties recognize that cooperative-AI protocol families, when operated under the safeguards described in Section 1, do not constitute "personal data processing" in the sense that would trigger the cross-border-transfer restrictions of GDPR Chapter V, UK DPA Schedule 21, Japan APPI Article 28, or analogous provisions, because:

- The disclosed material is a single bit per requirement;
- The bit's derivation reveals no personal data per the protocol's refusal floor;
- The principal's underlying chain never crosses the Signatory border.

2.2 The Signatory Parties recognize that the protocol family's refusal floor — when structurally enforced (predicate registry triage, trademark license, multi-sig governance) — satisfies the special-category-data protections of GDPR Article 9, UK DPA Article 9, and analogous provisions, because protected-category data is structurally never named, never measured, and never disclosed.

### Section 3 — Reciprocal Recognition

3.1 Each Signatory Party recognizes Calm Stack disclosures originating in another Signatory Party as evidence of the disclosed bit, subject to verification under the protocol's verification procedure.

3.2 No Signatory Party shall require:

- Disclosure of the underlying principal chain
- Disclosure of the per-attestation identities
- Disclosure of biometric data
- Disclosure of any protected-category attribute

... as a condition of recognizing a Calm Stack disclosure from another Signatory Party's principal.

### Section 4 — Domestic Authority Preserved

4.1 Nothing in this Article restricts a Signatory Party's authority to:

- Regulate the deployment of Calm Stack within its territory
- Require domestic operators to maintain records the Party deems necessary
- Exclude specific use cases from Calm Stack deployment (per the Calm Witness scope statement's permitted exclusions or per the Party's own statutory restrictions)
- Conduct law-enforcement investigations under applicable domestic process

4.2 Nothing in this Article requires a Signatory Party to:

- Endorse the Calm Stack
- Mandate adoption
- Procure or fund Calm Stack deployment
- Recognize disclosures from non-Signatory jurisdictions

### Section 5 — Standards Track Coordination

5.1 The Signatory Parties commit to coordinating Calm Stack standards-track work through:

- NIST/USAISI for U.S. coordination
- EU AI Office for EU coordination
- UK AI Safety Institute and ICO for UK coordination
- Japan AI Safety Institute and PPC for Japan coordination
- Other Signatory Parties' designated authorities

5.2 The Calm Witness Foundation, Inc. (a U.S. 501(c)(3) non-profit) is recognized as the multi-stakeholder body authorized to coordinate revisions of the Calm Stack with the Signatory Parties' designated authorities.

### Section 6 — Refusal Floor as Treaty-Level Commitment

6.1 The Signatory Parties recognize the Calm Stack's refusal floor (currently: race, religion, political affiliation, sexual orientation, gender identity, immigration status, criminal record, donations-to-causes, contentious opinions, disability status, age, health status, marital/family status) as an inviolable structural commitment of the protocol family.

6.2 Additions to the refusal floor by the Calm Witness Foundation Ethics Review Board are automatically recognized by Signatory Parties.

6.3 Removals from the refusal floor require:

- Unanimous Ethics Review Board approval
- 90-day public comment period
- Notification to all Signatory Parties at least 180 days before effective date
- Right of any Signatory Party to refuse continued recognition under this Article if a removal is deemed harmful in that Party's jurisdiction

### Section 7 — Prohibited Uses Carry Treaty Weight

7.1 The Calm Witness scope statement's prohibited use categories (credit decisions, employment screening, insurance underwriting, custody, immigration adjudication, court evidence, law-enforcement investigations, surveillance, aggregate analytics) are recognized by Signatory Parties as use cases for which Calm Stack disclosures shall not be recognized as valid evidence.

7.2 Signatory Parties commit to taking proportionate enforcement action against domestic deployments that violate the prohibited-use categories, including but not limited to revocation of trademark license, regulatory action, and exclusion from coordinated standards-track work.

### Section 8 — Annual Joint Convening

8.1 The Signatory Parties convene annually, hosted in rotating capitals, to:

- Review Calm Stack revisions since last convening
- Coordinate enforcement actions
- Address cross-jurisdiction issues raised by domestic authorities
- Invite affected-community representatives, civil-society organizations, and Foundation staff to brief

8.2 The first convening shall be held within twelve (12) months of this Article's entry into force, hosted by [TBD].

### Section 9 — Withdrawal

9.1 Any Signatory Party may withdraw from this Article with 180 days written notice to all other Signatory Parties.

9.2 Disclosures issued before the effective date of withdrawal remain recognized under this Article.

### Section 10 — Entry Into Force

10.1 This Article enters into force upon ratification by [TBD: number] Signatory Parties, including at least the United States, the European Union (acting on behalf of its Member States), the United Kingdom, and Japan.

## §2 — The first multi-stakeholder convening (charter)

### §2.1 — Purpose

To gather, for the first time, the parties whose cooperation is necessary to advance the Calm Stack from a voluntary protocol family to a treaty-recognized cooperative-AI standard.

### §2.2 — Format

A four-day in-person convening, hosted in Washington D.C. (in proximity to USAISI) or Geneva (in proximity to ITU and WIPO), with the following structure:

- **Day 1 — Technical briefing:** the Calm Stack specifications, threat models, refusal floor, current deployments. Calm + Foundation staff present.
- **Day 2 — Affected-community briefing:** disability-rights organizations, cognitive-liberties advocates, counter-claimants, marginalized principals present. Calm + Foundation staff listen.
- **Day 3 — Jurisdictional working groups:** parallel breakout sessions by jurisdiction (US, EU, UK, JP, others), each chaired by the relevant designated authority.

**Risk:** State authorities decline to attend at appropriate seniority level.

## §3 — The "treaty-grade" framing — why this matters

Cryptographic standards typically evolve through:
- Open-source community (loose, slow, no enforcement)
- Standards bodies (IETF, W3C, ISO — slow, formal, voluntary)
- State regulators (jurisdiction-bounded, often after-the-fact)

The Calm Stack's reach is cross-jurisdictional from day one: a U.S. principal's agent will interact with EU, UK, Japan counterparties. If governance is left to per-jurisdiction regulators, the protocol's cross-jurisdiction recognition becomes a litigation problem rather than a design property.

A treaty-grade framework — even a non-binding one initially — does three things no single-jurisdiction regulator can:

1. **Reciprocal recognition:** state parties commit to recognizing disclosures from each other's principals without demanding underlying material.
2. **Coordinated refusal floor:** the refusal categories become treaty-level commitments rather than per-jurisdiction policy choices.
3. **Coordinated prohibited uses:** state parties coordinate enforcement against violators rather than each acting alone.

This is the level of governance ambition required for a cooperative-AI primitive that aspires to be global infrastructure.

## §4 — Why "preliminary draft" and "convening" not "treaty"

The honest framing: a binding multi-party treaty takes years to negotiate, ratify, and enter into force. The Calm Stack doesn't have years.

What this Everest delivers is:

- A **starting draft** that can be put in front of state authorities at the first convening
- A **convening structure** that bootstraps the coordination
- A **Statement of Intent** as the realistic immediate output

Full treaty status is Everest 286 (cross-foundation governance treaty) in the canonical map's Range R. This Everest is the prerequisite that makes Everest 286 possible.
