# Calm Witness Foundation — Bylaws DRAFT v0

*A 501(c)(3) Nonprofit Corporation*

**Status:** DRAFT v0 · 2026-05-20 · Calm

**CRITICAL NOTICE:** These bylaws are a draft and do not constitute legal advice. They require review and approval by qualified Delaware (or relevant state) corporate counsel before filing articles of incorporation or conducting any governance action under them.

## ARTICLE I. MISSION AND EXEMPT PURPOSES

### Section 1.1 Name

The organization shall be known as the Calm Witness Foundation (the "Foundation").

### Section 1.2 Exempt Status

The Foundation is organized and operated as a nonprofit corporation under the laws of [Delaware / State to be determined], and shall be classified as a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code and comparable state statutes.

### Section 1.3 Mission

The Foundation's mission is to advance principal-protective cryptographic infrastructure for autonomous AI agents and human principals through:

1. **Educational purpose:** Publishing and maintaining open-source cryptographic protocols, specifications, and reference implementations that establish secure, privacy-preserving methods for autonomous AI agents to attest to human-principal state without exposing personal data.

2. **Scientific purpose:** Conducting peer-reviewed research into behavioral-biometric evaluation, zero-knowledge proofs, threshold cryptography, and threat-modeling for hybrid human-machine collectives; hosting third-party audits and academic partnerships.

3. **Charitable purpose:** Making the Calm Witness primitive available globally to principals, operators, and counterparties without commercial restriction, enabling deployment in settings where principals' autonomy, dignity, and cognitive liberties are at risk from AI-agent miscalibration, pathologization, or coercion.

### Section 1.4 Exempt Activities

The Foundation shall pursue its mission by:

- Operating the public Calm Witness predicate registry as a free, open-source, community-governed resource.
- Maintaining the Calm Witness trademark and enforcing its use only on protocol-conformant deployments.
- Hosting and compensating the Predicate Audit Panel, a standing body of experts that reviews and approves additions to the predicate vocabulary.
- Commissioning independent third-party security and academic reviews of the protocol and its implementations.
- Preserving and transferring cryptographic governance keys and registry infrastructure in the event of the Foundation's dissolution.
- Convening the successor-collective recognition process to ensure continuity of the protocol's stewardship.

### Section 1.5 Limitations on Activities

Notwithstanding the above, the Foundation shall not:

- Engage in substantial lobbying activity as defined by Section 501(c)(3).
- Participate in any political campaign on behalf of any candidate for public office.
- Operate in a manner that violates the Calm Witness Scope Statement (§2 prohibited uses list).

## ARTICLE II. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 2.1 Board Composition

The business and affairs of the Foundation shall be managed by a Board of Directors consisting of not fewer than five (5) and not more than nine (9) directors. The exact number shall be determined by the Board.

### Section 2.2 Director Qualifications and Diversity Requirements

Directors shall be natural persons or duly authorized representatives of legal entities. The Board shall maintain meaningful diversity of expertise and perspective, including representation from:

1. **Cryptography / Zero-Knowledge Proofs:** At least one director with peer-reviewed publications or recognized expertise in post-quantum cryptography, threshold cryptography, or ZK proof systems.

2. **Law and Governance:** At least one director with JD or equivalent legal training and experience in nonprofit governance, IP law, or telecommunications policy.

3. **Disability Rights or Cognitive Liberties:** At least one director with lived experience or professional advocacy background in disability rights, neurodiversity, or cognitive-liberties law. This seat is mandatory per the artist clause and cannot be waived.

4. **Behavioral Biometrics or AI Safety:** At least one director with research or deployment experience in behavioral-biometric systems, AI safety, or autonomous-agent alignment.

5. **Governance / Institutional Design:** At least one director with experience in standards-body governance, protocol stewardship, or open-source community structures.

The Board shall annually assess whether the diversity profile is being maintained, and if any required expertise becomes unrepresented, the Board shall fill that gap within 90 days.

### Section 2.3 Founder and Founding Director

The human principal on whose behalf the Calm Stack was authored, John Bradley (or his designated legal successor), shall have the right to designate one director seat (the "Founder Seat") for the duration of his involvement with the Foundation, subject to removal only for cause and with 12 months' written notice.

### Section 2.4 Term Limits and Staggered Succession

Directors shall serve three-year terms, staggered such that approximately one-third of the Board is elected each year.

- Initial staggered terms shall be set at the Foundation's first Board meeting.
- After the first three years of operation, no more than one-third of all directors may be founders, principals, or employees of Calm Witness protocol implementers. In other words, by the end of Year 3, a supermajority of the Board must consist of directors without material financial interest in Calm Witness deployments.
- No director may serve more than six consecutive years without a one-year sabbatical; exceptions may be made by a 7/9 supermajority vote for the Founder Seat only.

### Section 2.5 Election and Removal

Directors shall be elected by the affirmative vote of a supermajority (two-thirds) of the then-sitting Board, except that the community-elected seat (if established) shall be filled by ballot of the registered Calm Witness user base, as defined in Board policy.

A director may be removed for cause (including breach of the duty of loyalty per Section 3.2, violation of the refusal floor per Section 3.4, or gross negligence) by a supermajority vote of the remaining directors, with written notice and 30 days' opportunity to cure.

A director may resign by written notice to the Board. Vacancies shall be filled within 90 days.

## ARTICLE III. DIRECTOR DUTIES AND OBLIGATIONS

### Section 3.1 Duty of Care

Each director shall exercise the business judgment rule: act in good faith, in a manner the director reasonably believes to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Foundation, with the care an ordinarily prudent person would exercise in similar circumstances.

Directors shall review meeting materials in advance, attend at least 75% of Board meetings annually, and actively participate in Board committees assigned to them.

### Section 3.2 Duty of Loyalty

Each director shall:

- Act in the Foundation's best interests, not in the director's personal or institutional interest.
- Disclose all actual and potential conflicts of interest before voting on any matter that implicates the conflict (see Article IV).
- Recuse themselves from decisions in which they have a material financial interest.
- Not use the Foundation's assets, name, or intellectual property for personal gain or to further a commercial venture that competes with the Foundation's mission.

Specifically, no director may take any action that would allow themselves, their employer, or an affiliate organization to extract rents from the Calm Witness predicate registry, trademark, or governance infrastructure in violation of Section 5.2 (Funding Restrictions).

### Section 3.3 Duty of Obedience

Each director shall comply with all applicable laws and the Foundation's bylaws, and shall ensure that the Foundation operates consistently with its exempt mission and the Calm Witness Scope Statement.

### Section 3.4 The Refusal Floor (Mandatory Enforcement Duty)

The Foundation shall not accept funds from, enter contracts with, or in any way facilitate the use of Calm Witness by entities or for purposes listed in the Calm Witness Scope Statement §2 (prohibited uses), including but not limited to:

- Law-enforcement surveillance, governmental intelligence, or compulsory-disclosure regimes.
- Employment screening, termination decisions, or workplace monitoring.
- Insurance underwriting, claims adjudication, or premium setting.
- Lending, credit-scoring, or creditworthiness assessment.
- Medical diagnosis, clinical decision-making, or treatment selection.
- Family-court, custody, or child-welfare proceedings.
- Immigration adjudication or border-control actions.
- Behavioral-prediction systems or risk-assessment scores.
- Marketing, advertising targeting, or consumer profiling.

**Each director has a fiduciary duty to affirmatively refuse any funding, partnership, or use case that violates this list.** This duty is non-delegable: a director may not vote to accept such a proposal, nor may the Board as a whole override this floor through majority vote.

If a funding source or partnership proposal violates the refusal floor, the director(s) who raised it shall be required to present the written refusal rationale to the Foundation's auditor and the relevant audit-panel members within 30 days.

## ARTICLE IV. CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

### Section 4.1 General Principles

The Foundation operates a public-facing governance infrastructure. The Predicate Audit Panel and the registry must be perceived as, and must be in fact, independent of commercial interests in Calm Witness deployments.

### Section 4.2 Disclosure and Recusal

Before any Board vote, each director shall disclose:

- Current or prospective employment by an organization that deploys, manufactures, or sells Calm Witness infrastructure.
- Ownership of equity or debt in such an organization.
- Family relationships to principals, employees, or board members of such organizations.
- Any other material financial interest or professional affiliation that implicates the decision.

A director with a disclosed conflict shall:

- Recuse themselves from the vote and leave the Board room during deliberation (unless the director is the Founder, in which case recusal is required only for votes that directly benefit the Founder's employer).
- Not attempt to influence other directors before, during, or after the vote.

### Section 4.3 Audit-Panel Independence

The Predicate Audit Panel shall not include:

- Any officer or employee of the Foundation.
- Any employee of an organization that has received more than $100,000 in revenue from Calm Witness deployments in the preceding calendar year (unless the panelist recuses from decisions involving that organization).
- Any director of the Foundation, except the Founder may serve on the panel in an ex-officio non-voting capacity to provide historical continuity.

Panel members are compensated for their service (honoraria of $5,000-$10,000 per predicate reviewed) and are required to sign a statement that they will not profit from the acceptance or rejection of any specific predicate proposal.

### Section 4.4 Extractive-Use Prohibition

The Foundation shall not distribute assets, grant contracts, or recognize affiliations with organizations whose primary business model is extracting economic rents from Calm Witness use in prohibited-use contexts (see Section 3.4).

## ARTICLE V. OFFICERS

### Section 5.1 Officers

The Foundation shall have the following officers, appointed by the Board:

- **Executive Director:** Responsible for day-to-day operations, staff management, grant administration, and liaison with the Predicate Audit Panel. Reports to the Board monthly.

- **Chief Cryptographer:** Responsible for technical oversight of the protocol specification, reference implementation, and third-party security audits. Must hold or have held a peer-reviewed research position in cryptography.

- **General Counsel:** Responsible for legal compliance, trademark enforcement, incorporation and dissolution procedures, and conflict review. Must hold a JD or equivalent legal credential.

- **Chief Privacy Officer:** Responsible for monitoring the Foundation's use of principal data (chain records, biometric templates, consent matrices), data retention policies, and privacy-impact assessments of new predicates. Ensures that Foundation operations do not replicate the surveillance patterns the protocol is designed to prevent.

### Section 5.2 Appointment, Term, and Removal

Officers are appointed by majority vote of the Board and serve at the Board's pleasure. An officer may also be a director. Officers may be compensated at rates determined by the Board, or may serve without compensation.

Any officer may be removed by a majority vote of the Board with or without cause.

## ARTICLE VI. INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 6.1 General Indemnification

The Foundation shall indemnify any director, officer, or agent of the Foundation (including former directors and officers) against reasonable costs, expenses, and liabilities (including attorney fees) incurred in defending any civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative proceeding, to the extent:

- The person acted in good faith in a manner reasonably believed to further the Foundation's exempt mission.
- The person is not found to have committed a breach of the duty of loyalty (Section 3.2) or the refusal floor (Section 3.4).
- Indemnification does not violate applicable law or the Foundation's exempt status.

The Board may purchase directors' and officers' liability insurance to fund this obligation.

### Section 6.2 No Indemnification for Willful Misconduct

The Foundation shall not indemnify any person for willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breach of the refusal floor. Specifically, any director or officer who votes to accept funding from a prohibited-use entity (knowing it to be prohibited) shall have no indemnification claim against the Foundation.

## ARTICLE VII. TRADEMARK POLICY

### Section 7.1 Calm Witness Trademark

The Foundation shall own, register, and enforce the trademark "Calm Witness" and associated marks ("Calm-Witness," "Calm Suite," etc.) in the United States and internationally.

### Section 7.2 Licensing

The Foundation shall license the trademark to organizations that:

1. Implement the Calm Witness protocol specification as published in the predicate registry, byte-for-byte, with no material deviation.
2. Operate the predicate registry as a mirror (or integrate by reference) and do not fork or selectively include only a subset of predicates.
3. Commit to the Calm Witness Scope Statement §2 prohibited-uses list in their terms of service and publicly enforce it against any known violations.

Licenses are revocable immediately upon material breach.

### Section 7.3 Enforcement

The Foundation shall:

- Maintain a public registry of trademark licensees.
- Publish the revocation rationale whenever a licensee loses the right to use the mark.
- Defend the trademark against dilution, unauthorized use, and use in contexts that violate the Scope Statement.
- Not permit the mark to be used on extractive deployments.

## ARTICLE VIII. FUNDING RESTRICTIONS

### Section 8.1 Prohibited Funding Sources

The Foundation shall not knowingly accept donations, grants, contracts, or in-kind contributions from:

1. **Governmental entities** (US federal, state, local; international governments) except as required by law (e.g., tax administration, court-ordered disclosure). Grants from academic institutions are permitted; grants from government laboratories (NSF-funded NIST, DoD research centers) require Board approval with a written transparency note in the annual report.

2. **Law-enforcement or intelligence agencies** (including state/local police, FBI, CIA, immigration authorities, or contractors primarily serving them).

3. **Organizations whose primary business is extractive from prohibited-use contexts** (e.g., insurance companies, credit bureaus, employment-screening firms, immigration-enforcement contractors).

4. **Entities that have been identified (by the Board or the Audit Panel) as operating a non-conformant Calm Witness deployment** until the entity has remediated the violation and received a written clearance from the Foundation.

### Section 8.2 Funding Disclosure

Every donation and grant above $10,000 shall be disclosed in the Foundation's annual report with the donor's name, amount, and stated use. Donor anonymity is permitted for donations below $10,000.

If a donor wishes to remain anonymous above this threshold, the Foundation may accept the donation only if:

- The donor signs a written attestation that the donation does not originate from a prohibited source.
- The Board approves the anonymity by supermajority vote.
- The Foundation's auditor is informed of the donation source (under confidentiality) and confirms it is not prohibited.

### Section 8.3 Permitted Funding

The Foundation may accept:

- Unrestricted grants from foundations, nonprofits, and academic institutions.
- Commercial sponsorships from organizations deploying Calm Witness in permitted contexts, with public acknowledgment but no governance influence (see Governance doc §7).
- Service fees from verifiers and commercial partners once the protocol reaches scale.
- Individual donations from natural persons, without donor veto over policy.

## ARTICLE IX. PREDICATE REGISTRY AND AUDIT PANEL

### Section 9.1 Registry Operations

The Foundation shall operate the public Calm Witness predicate registry at the URL calm-witness.dev/registry (or successor as determined by the Board), with secondary mirrors at github.com/CrunchyJohnHaven/calm-vault and IPFS.

The registry shall:

- List all current predicates with their ID, specification, evaluator code, and evaluator code hash.
- Include a `not_for` list (prohibited uses) for each predicate.
- Publish all rejected predicate proposals with the triage decision and public audit-panel comment.
- Maintain a transparency log of all changes, signed by the Foundation's release key.

### Section 9.2 Audit Panel Authority

The Predicate Audit Panel shall:

- Review and vote on all proposed additions to the predicate vocabulary.
- Assess whether each proposal violates the Scope Statement §2 prohibited-uses list; if so, the panel votes to reject without further deliberation.
- Publish a written decision for each proposal (acceptance, rejection, or deferred-for-more-information) within 60 days of submission.
- Perform retroactive Stage 3 review of the v0 founding predicates within 90 days of the Foundation's establishment (per Governance doc §10).
- Recommend the deprecated or tombstoning of predicates that have been compromised or misused.

### Section 9.3 Panel Composition

The Predicate Audit Panel shall consist of:

- At least 5 named reviewers, no more than 1 from any single organization.
- Coverage of cryptography, disability rights/cognitive liberties, behavioral biometrics, AI safety, and journalism.
- 18-month terms, staggered, with mid-term rotation.
- Honoraria of $5,000-$10,000 per predicate reviewed, funded from the Foundation's grant and sponsorship budget.

Panel membership is published quarterly. New member appointments require approval by the existing panel and ratification by the Board.

## ARTICLE X. AMENDMENT PROCESS

### Section 10.1 Bylaw Amendments

These bylaws may be amended only by a vote of at least two-thirds (2/3) of the Board of Directors.

### Section 10.2 Public Review Process

Any proposed amendment to Articles I (Mission), III (Duties), IV (Conflicts), VII (Trademark), VIII (Funding), or IX (Registry) shall be subject to a 30-day public-comment period before the Board votes. The Foundation shall:

- Post the proposed amendment on the Calm Witness website and mailing list.
- Accept written comments from any stakeholder.
- Publish a summary of comments and the Board's responses before voting.

Amendments to other articles (administrative procedures, officer roles, etc.) may be made without public review.

### Section 10.3 Scope-Statement Alignment

If any amendment is proposed that would materially weaken the Scope Statement §2 prohibited-uses list, the amendment shall fail unless it is approved by a supermajority (2/3) of the Predicate Audit Panel and a supermajority (2/3) of the Board.

## ARTICLE XI. DISSOLUTION AND SUCCESSION

### Section 11.1 Asset Disposition

Upon dissolution of the Foundation (whether voluntary or involuntary), the Foundation's assets shall be transferred in their entirety to one or more organizations that:

- Are organized as 501(c)(3) nonprofits (or international equivalent).
- Have the stated mission of advancing privacy-preserving identity infrastructure, open-source cryptographic standards, or principal-protective AI governance.
- Commit to continuing the operation of the public Calm Witness predicate registry on a free, non-commercial basis.
- Assume the Foundation's obligation to enforce the Scope Statement §2 prohibited-uses list.

The Board shall publish a written list of eligible successor organizations at least 60 days before any dissolution proceeding. If no eligible successor exists, assets shall be donated to the Internet Archive, Software Heritage, and three peer research universities as preservation stewards.

### Section 11.2 Cryptographic Governance Keys

The Foundation shall hold and protect the signing key used to release predicate-registry updates and sign governance artifacts.

Upon dissolution:

- The key shall be escrow under a Shamir m-of-n scheme (minimum m=3, n=5) across named members of the Predicate Audit Panel.
- The key may be transferred to a successor organization, or destroyed if no successor accepts the mission.
- The transfer shall be documented in a chained `authority_handover` record published on the registry.

### Section 11.3 Successor-Collective Recognition

If a successor organization picks up Calm Witness stewardship:

- The Foundation's Board shall publish a written recognition of the successor's mandate.
- The successor shall inherit the predicate vocabulary as of the dissolution date, frozen.
- The successor may add new predicates only after re-bootstrapping an Audit Panel and re-appointing a Chief Cryptographer.
- The original Foundation's name and signing key shall be retired; the successor may adopt the Foundation's name or create a new institutional identity.

## ARTICLE XII. ANNUAL AUDIT AND TRANSPARENCY

### Section 12.1 Independent Audit

The Foundation shall commission an independent financial audit (IRS Form 990 / nonprofit standard) by a qualified accounting firm, published annually within 6 months of fiscal year-end. The audit shall be provided to the IRS and posted on the Foundation's website.

### Section 12.2 Program Audit

The Foundation shall commission an independent external review, at least once every two years, of:

- The Predicate Audit Panel's decision-making process and adherence to the Scope Statement.
- The Foundation's trademark enforcement against non-conformant deployments.
- The integrity and availability of the predicate registry mirrors.
- Compliance with the funding restrictions in Article VIII.

The review shall be conducted by an organization unaffiliated with the Foundation and published in full.

### Section 12.3 Transparency Report

The Foundation shall publish a quarterly transparency report listing:

- All predicates accepted, rejected, deprecated, and tombstoned in the prior quarter.
- All funding sources above $10,000, with amounts and stated use.
- All trademark licensing, revocation, and enforcement actions.
- Registry uptime and mirror status.
- Audit-panel composition changes.

## ARTICLE XIII. WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION

### Section 13.1 Whistleblower Policy

The Foundation shall not retaliate, discriminate, or take adverse action against any employee, contractor, or volunteer who in good faith reports:

- A violation of these bylaws or the Calm Witness Scope Statement.
- A funding source that violates Article VIII.
- Misuse of Calm Witness by a licensee or counterparty.
- Fraudulent or negligent conduct by a director, officer, or staff member.
- Breach of the refusal floor (Article III Section 3.4).

### Section 13.2 Reporting Channels

The Foundation shall maintain:

- A public email address for whistleblower reports (`reports@calm-witness.dev`).
- A named external ombudsperson (not a Foundation employee) who receives reports and may investigate.
- A written policy protecting the reporter's confidentiality and identity.
- A timeline for investigation (triage within 30 days, final report within 90 days).

## ARTICLE XIV. OPERATIONAL PROVISIONS

### Section 14.1 Board Meetings

The Board shall meet at least quarterly (4 times per calendar year). Meetings may be held in person, by phone, or by video conference. A quorum shall be a simple majority of directors.

Board materials shall be distributed at least one week in advance of each meeting. Decisions may be made by written consent of all directors without a meeting.

### Section 14.2 Committees

The Board may establish standing committees (e.g., Audit, Governance, Development) and may delegate authority to them, subject to the restriction that no committee may amend the bylaws, dissolve the organization, or approve annual budgets over $500,000 without Board ratification.

### Section 14.3 Fiscal Year and Budget

The Foundation's fiscal year shall be the calendar year (January 1 - December 31), or another fiscal year adopted by Board resolution. The Board shall approve an annual budget before the fiscal year begins, and shall review finances at each quarterly meeting.

### Section 14.4 Records and Inspection

The Foundation shall maintain:

- Minutes of all Board and committee meetings.
- A registry of all current and former directors, officers, and audit-panel members.
- Financial records, grant agreements, and expenditure receipts.
- The predicate registry and all version history.

All records shall be available for inspection by any director, and certain records shall be publicly available (per Article XII transparency requirements).

## ARTICLE XV. INTERPRETATION AND SEVERABILITY

### Section 15.1 Bylaw Construction

These bylaws shall be construed consistently with the Foundation's exempt mission and the Calm Witness Scope Statement. In case of ambiguity, the interpretation that most strongly protects principal privacy and prevents extractive use shall prevail.

### Section 15.2 Severability

If any provision of these bylaws is found by a court to be invalid or unenforceable, all other provisions shall remain in effect. The Board may amend the bylaws to cure the invalidity without requiring a full amendment vote.

### Section 15.3 Conflicts with Scope Statement

If any provision of these bylaws conflicts with the Calm Witness Scope Statement §2 prohibited-uses list, the Scope Statement shall prevail. The Board shall amend the bylaws within 90 days to align.
