## The Ask: AI Safety Architecture Breakthrough Designation (ASABD)

We petition the Administration to establish a new federal designation modeled on the FDA's Breakthrough Device Designation.

### Statutory and administrative basis

The FDA Breakthrough Device Designation exists under Section 515B of the FD&C Act (21 USC §360e-3), enacted in the 21st Century Cures Act (2016). It provides accelerated FDA evaluation for medical devices that (a) effectively treat or diagnose a life-threatening or irreversibly debilitating condition, and (b) represent breakthrough technology, offer significant advantages, or are in patients' best interest.

ASABD parallels this, administered by NIST in coordination with OSTP and AISI, under existing authority:

- The NIST Authorization Act of 1988 (15 USC §271 et seq.)
- The National AI Initiative Act of 2020
- Executive Order 14110 (October 2023) and successor orders

ASABD does not require new legislation. NIST administrative action suffices. Optional Presidential endorsement via Executive Order.

### Criteria (proposed)

An AI architecture, system, or protocol qualifies for ASABD if:

1. It addresses a substantiated AI-safety threat dimension (rogue AI, multi-agent collusion, specification drift, founder capture, capability concentration, or others enumerated by AISI).
2. It represents breakthrough technology — novel in mechanism, or an alternative offering significant advantages, or its availability is in the national interest.
3. It is openly published under permissive licenses (Apache 2.0 or equivalent for code; CC BY 4.0 or equivalent for documentation).
4. It includes a verifiable accountability mechanism (cryptographic attestation or kill switch) allowing independent verification of claimed safety properties.

The AAO Network's architecture meets all four. We submit it as the first applicant.

### Evaluation track

- Cryptographic review by NIST cryptographers (we volunteer our keys and test infrastructure)
- Safety analysis by AISI evaluators (we volunteer our doctrine, attestation log, and operational data)
- Independent academic peer review (we will compose with the IACR ePrint review tradition)
- Public comment period (we welcome and will respond to all substantive comments)

We do not request federal funding, procurement preference, or indemnification. We request only federal evaluation on an accelerated, public, on-the-record track, with a finding within twelve months of acceptance.
