# Calm Witness — Predicate Review Board Operating Rules v0 (S209)

**Scope.** These rules govern the standing body that evaluates proposed additions to the public predicate registry (E53) and approves, conditionally approves, or rejects predicate submissions (E54). They are authoritative from the date of adoption and supersede ad-hoc review practices.

---

## 1. Composition & Term Limits

The board comprises five seated reviewers. Seats are designated by domain: (1) cryptographic attestation, (2) data-schema standards, (3) privacy and consent law, (4) clinical or behavioral science, (5) user-advocate / civil-society. Each reviewer serves a staggered two-year term; no reviewer may serve more than two consecutive terms in the same seat. The Protocol Steward (S132) appoints replacements with 30-day public notice. A permanent non-voting Secretary maintains records and manages procedure.

---

## 2. Quorum

A quorum is four of five seated reviewers. No substantive vote may proceed below quorum. If a recusal reduces seated participation below four, the Protocol Steward shall appoint a temporary substitute from a pre-approved alternate roster within five business days.

---

## 3. Agenda Procedure

The Secretary publishes a provisional agenda no fewer than 14 calendar days before each scheduled session. Any member of the public may request agenda inclusion of a related item by written submission within seven days of publication. The board chair accepts or defers items by simple majority at the session's opening. Emergency items require affirmative vote of four seated reviewers to add mid-session.

---

## 4. Public-Comment Window

Every predicate submission (E54) triggers a public-comment window of not fewer than 30 calendar days from the date the submission appears in the public registry queue. The window must close before the board holds a binding vote on that submission. The Secretary collects written comments, assigns each a docket number, and publishes a full comment log. The board must address, in its written decision, any substantive objection raised during the window; dismissal of an objection requires written rationale.

---

## 5. Voting Procedure

Each seated, non-recused reviewer casts one vote: Approve, Conditionally Approve (with listed conditions), or Reject. Approval or Conditional Approval requires affirmative votes from at least four of the five seated reviewers (three of four if one seat is vacant or recused, subject to quorum). A simple majority suffices only for procedural motions. All votes are recorded individually and published in the session minutes. Proxy voting is not permitted.

---

## 6. COI Recusal

A reviewer must self-recuse from any vote in which they have a material financial interest, employment relationship, or personal stake in the submitting entity or in the predicate's primary beneficiary. Self-recusal must be declared in writing to the Secretary before agenda publication for the relevant session. The board may also vote to compel recusal by a four-fifths majority. Recusal does not strip the reviewer of their seat or affect other agenda items.

---

## 7. Public Minutes

The Secretary publishes draft minutes within seven calendar days of each session. Minutes include: attendance, quorum confirmation, agenda items, a summary of each public comment addressed, individual vote tallies, written rationale for each decision, and any recusals declared. Final minutes are ratified at the following session. All minutes are archived in the public governance repository alongside E53 and E54 records.

---

## 8. Misconduct Sanctions

Misconduct includes: failure to disclose a known COI, deliberate misrepresentation of evidence, ex-parte communication with a submitter during the comment window, and material breach of public-minutes obligations. Upon written complaint, the Protocol Steward (S132) convenes an independent review panel of three persons not seated on the board. Sanctions range from formal censure to immediate removal. Removal requires a written finding and is subject to the appeals path below.

---

## 9. Appeals Path

Any party with standing (submitter, public commenter of record, or seated reviewer) may appeal a board decision to the S217 Appeals Tribunal within 30 calendar days of final-minutes publication. The appeal must specify the grounds: procedural defect, COI failure, or decision unsupported by the evidentiary record. S217 may affirm, remand with instructions, or reverse. Remanded decisions return to the board for re-vote; the board may not re-use the same composition if COI failure was found. S217 decisions are final; further recourse is governed by S220.
