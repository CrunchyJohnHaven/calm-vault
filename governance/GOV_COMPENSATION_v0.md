# Calm Witness — Governance Compensation Disclosure v0 (S220)

## Scope

This specification establishes mandatory transparency requirements for all compensation arrangements involving Calm Foundation board members, officers, and protocol operators. Governance funded in secret is governance compromised in secret. Full disclosure of financial relationships serves three functions: (1) enables stakeholders to identify conflicts of interest, (2) prevents hidden quid pro quo arrangements that undermine protocol integrity, (3) establishes auditability of compensation decisions against self-dealing.

This policy applies to:
- Board members and officers of Calm Foundation
- Protocol operators with discretionary authority over treasury, relay selection, or validator economics
- Any individual holding simultaneous positions at Calm Foundation and affiliated organizations or counterparty protocols

Cross-references: S209 (Foundation Charter), S185 (Operator Registry), S217 (Treasury Controls), S248 (Conflict of Interest Framework).

## Disclosure Fields

Each subject individual must file annual disclosure containing:

**Direct Compensation**: All cash, tokens, equity, or in-kind compensation from Calm Foundation or subsidiary entities. Include amount, currency, vesting schedule (if applicable), and date range.

**Outside Compensation**: Concurrent compensation from positions held at other organizations, including counterparty protocols, staking services, validators, or infrastructure providers. Report title, organization, compensation type, and percentage of time allocation.

**Equity & Investment Holdings**: All equity stakes, token holdings exceeding 0.01% of circulating supply, or investment positions in:
- Entities providing services to Calm Foundation (relay operators, validators, staking pools)
- Competing or adjacent protocols
- Entities with contractual relationships to the Foundation

Include acquisition date, quantity, percentage of issuer capitalization, and market value as of disclosure date.

**Family Relationships**: Disclosure of spousal or immediate family compensation arrangements that create indirect conflicts of interest.

**Prior Compensation**: Historical positions held in preceding 24 months at competing protocols or service providers.

## Update Cadence

Annual disclosure filings required by January 31st of each calendar year, covering the preceding 12-month period. Material changes to compensation arrangements (increases exceeding 20%, new positions, equity acquisition) trigger supplemental disclosure within 14 days of transaction execution.

Disclosure documents are retained in the Calm Witness protocol registry with immutable timestamps. Updates replace prior versions in the public record.

## Verification

Calm Foundation Board maintains independent verification authority over disclosure accuracy. Spot audits of compensation claims against actual payment records occur quarterly. Signers attest under penalty of removal that disclosures are complete and accurate.

Third-party auditors may request supporting documentation (contracts, equity ledgers, tax filings) to validate compensation claims. Refusal to provide documentation on demand constitutes grounds for immediate officer removal.

Protocol participants may challenge disclosure accuracy by submitting evidence of omitted compensation to the Board. Board adjudication occurs within 30 days.

## Penalties for Non-Disclosure

- **First violation**: Written notice and 30-day cure period. Compensation subject to suspension pending remediation.
- **Second violation**: Immediate removal from board or officer position. Return of any unvested compensation to Foundation treasury.
- **Material concealment** (compensation exceeding 10% of total reported compensation, ongoing outside positions not disclosed): Removal, clawback of compensation from preceding 12 months, permanent bar from future positions.

Penalties apply equally to Foundation officers and protocol operators. No discretionary waiver authority. Violations are reported to stakeholder governance body for ratification.
