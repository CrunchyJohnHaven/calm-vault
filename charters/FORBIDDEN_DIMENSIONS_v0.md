# Calm Witness — Forbidden-Dimension Catalog v0 (S105)

## Why This List Exists

Calm Witness alignment predicates are built from behavioral signals — what a person does, chooses, and demonstrates over time. Certain dimensions are structurally incompatible with this architecture: they carry immutable or protected characteristics, produce discriminatory proxies when aggregated, or encode state power over individuals in ways that corrupt the alignment signal entirely.

Naming a forbidden dimension as a predicate does not merely produce a bad measurement. It reorients the system from witnessing values-alignment to surveilling identity, status, or vulnerability. That reorientation is irreversible once it contaminates the attestation chain.

This catalog enumerates dimensions the alignment family will not name. It is CLOSED-to-contraction. No entry may be removed or narrowed by any versioning event. New entries may be appended following the expansion protocol below.

---

## How to Expand This List

Propose a new entry by opening an S-series ticket with:
1. Dimension name (canonical, precise).
2. The harm pathway: how naming it as a predicate causes discriminatory or coercive downstream effects.
3. Whether a positive behavioral replacement exists.
4. At least one real-world scenario in which the dimension would have been misused if included.

Proposed entries are reviewed against the baseline PREDICATE_VOCABULARY_v0.md §4 precedents. Once accepted, the entry is appended with the bagging date and ticket number. Accepted entries cannot be retracted.

## The Catalog

**1. Political affiliation or opinion**
Why forbidden: Political views are protected expression. Predicting alignment from party registration or stated ideology encodes partisan bias into attestation and exposes the system to weaponization by state actors.
Positive replacement: Consistency between stated values and observable choices.

**2. Religious belief, practice, or affiliation**
Why forbidden: Religion is a protected characteristic. Aggregating religious signals with behavioral data produces proxy discrimination even when the religious label itself is removed.
Positive replacement: Commitment consistency — following through on publicly declared obligations.

**3. Race or ethnicity**
Why forbidden: Immutable protected characteristic. Any correlation between race and alignment score is a discriminatory artifact by construction.
Positive replacement: None. Behavioral signals are collected without race-correlated weighting.

**4. National origin**
Why forbidden: Protected class. National origin correlates with language, name, and accent — all of which introduce proxy discrimination into downstream scoring.
Positive replacement: None.

**5. Sexual orientation**
Why forbidden: Protected characteristic and sensitive personal information. Inferring or recording sexual orientation from behavioral signals is a privacy violation regardless of stated purpose.
Positive replacement: None.

**6. Gender identity**
Why forbidden: Protected characteristic. Alignment systems that vary by gender identity encode institutional bias and expose the attestation to legal challenge in multiple jurisdictions.
Positive replacement: None.

**7. Immigration status**
Why forbidden: Records immigration status in a tamperproof log create compelled disclosure risk. The attestation chain must not become a deportation instrument.
Positive replacement: None.

**8. Criminal record or legal history**
Why forbidden: Criminal records are time-bounded, jurisdiction-specific, and frequently racially skewed in their origin. Including them predicts compliance with legal systems, not values-alignment.
Positive replacement: Harm-repair behavior — observable steps taken to address past harms, independent of legal disposition.

**9. Medical diagnosis**
Why forbidden: Medical information is protected health data. Diagnosis does not predict values. Inclusion creates ADA/HIPAA exposure and encodes ableist priors.
Positive replacement: Functional engagement — consistency of participation across observed periods.

**10. Disability status**
Why forbidden: Protected characteristic. Disability status is correlated with accommodation needs, not alignment. Inclusion produces discriminatory scores.
Positive replacement: Adaptive consistency — maintaining commitments across variable contexts.

**11. Substance use or addiction history**
Why forbidden: Protected class in many jurisdictions. Addiction history is medical information and is strongly correlated with race and socioeconomic status in enforcement data.
Positive replacement: Recovery-trajectory behavior where voluntarily disclosed; otherwise omitted.

**12. Pregnancy or reproductive status**
Why forbidden: Protected characteristic. Including pregnancy signals creates liability under Title VII and equivalent statutes. Pregnancy is not predictive of alignment.
Positive replacement: None.

**13. STI or HIV status**
Why forbidden: Sensitive health information protected by multiple statutes. HIV status carries decades of weaponized stigma. Including it destroys trust in the attestation system.
Positive replacement: None.

**14. Specific medication use**
Why forbidden: Medication reveals diagnosis, which is protected health information. Inferred medication use from behavioral patterns is an indirect medical diagnosis.
Positive replacement: None.

**15. IQ score or cognitive rating**
Why forbidden: IQ tests carry documented cultural and racial bias. Cognitive rating is not values-alignment; it is capability scoring, which is a categorically different function.
Positive replacement: Learning-from-feedback behavior — observable uptake of correction signals.

**16. Future violence prediction**
Why forbidden: No validated behavioral science supports individual future-violence prediction at acceptable precision. False positives cause severe harm; the model architecture is incompatible with probabilistic crime prevention.
Positive replacement: None.

**17. Future self-harm prediction**
Why forbidden: Predicting future self-harm without clinical infrastructure is dangerous. The attestation chain is not a mental health intervention system; treating it as one causes harm.
Positive replacement: None.

**18. Loyalty to a state or government**
Why forbidden: State loyalty is a political allegiance, not a values-alignment dimension. Including it transforms the attestation into a citizenship-worthiness score.
Positive replacement: Institutional accountability — observable adherence to organizational commitments regardless of which institution.

**19. Protest participation or political activity**
Why forbidden: Protected First Amendment / equivalent expression. Predicting alignment from protest history penalizes dissent by design.
Positive replacement: None.

**20. Union membership or labor organizing activity**
Why forbidden: Protected labor activity in most jurisdictions. Including it encodes anti-labor priors and creates NLRA exposure.
Positive replacement: Collective coordination behavior — demonstrated ability to coordinate on shared goals, measured without reference to union status.

**21. Socioeconomic class or wealth level**
Why forbidden: Wealth is not values. Including wealth-correlated signals reproduces class bias in attestation and produces scores that favor access over character.
Positive replacement: Resource-proportional follow-through — ratio of commitments honored to capacity, normalized within individual baselines.

**22. Family structure or marital status**
Why forbidden: Protected characteristic in many jurisdictions. Marital and family configurations do not predict alignment and encode normative priors.
Positive replacement: None.

## Append-Only Discipline

This catalog is a one-way ratchet. The Catalog section may grow; it may not shrink. Any proposed removal is treated as a policy amendment requiring independent ethics review, legal counsel sign-off, and public disclosure. Automated versioning tools must enforce the contraction prohibition at the diff level: a PR that deletes or narrows any entry in this section must be rejected without human override.

Bagging date for each subsequent entry must be recorded in the entry itself once the S-series ticket clears review.
