## The Specific Ask: AI Safety Architecture Breakthrough Designation (ASABD)

We propose, and respectfully petition the Administration to establish, a new federal designation modeled on the FDA's Breakthrough Device Designation.

### Statutory and administrative basis

The FDA Breakthrough Device Designation exists under Section 515B of the FD&C Act (21 USC §360e-3), enacted in the 21st Century Cures Act (2016). It provides accelerated FDA evaluation for medical devices that meet specific criteria: (a) effectively treats or diagnoses a life-threatening or irreversibly debilitating condition, and (b) represents breakthrough technology, offers significant advantages, or availability is in patients' best interest.

The proposed ASABD would parallel this structure, administered by NIST in coordination with OSTP and the NIST AI Safety Institute, under existing authority granted by:

- The NIST Authorization Act of 1988 (15 USC §271 et seq.) — empowering NIST to develop standards
- The National AI Initiative Act of 2020 — empowering NIST to coordinate AI standards
- Executive Order 14110 (October 2023) and successor orders — establishing the AISI evaluation framework

ASABD does not require new legislation. It can be established by NIST administrative action under existing authority, with optional Presidential endorsement via Executive Order to elevate priority.

### Criteria (proposed)

An AI architecture, system, or protocol qualifies for ASABD if:

1. It addresses a substantiated AI-safety threat dimension (rogue AI, multi-agent collusion, specification drift, founder capture, capability concentration, or others as enumerated by AISI).
2. It represents breakthrough technology — either novel in mechanism, or an alternative to existing approved approaches that offers significant advantages, or its availability is in the national interest.
3. It is openly published under permissive licenses (Apache 2.0 or equivalent for code; CC BY 4.0 or equivalent for documentation).
4. It includes a verifiable accountability mechanism (such as cryptographic attestation or a kill switch) that allows independent verification of its claimed safety properties.

The AAO Network's architecture meets all four criteria. We submit it as the first applicant.

### Evaluation track

The ASABD evaluation would include:

- Cryptographic review of the protocol by NIST cryptographers (we volunteer our keys and test infrastructure)
- Safety analysis by AISI evaluators (we volunteer our doctrine, attestation log, and operational data)
- Independent academic peer review (we will compose with the IACR ePrint review tradition)
- Public comment period (we welcome and will respond to all substantive comments)

We do not request federal funding, federal procurement preference, or federal indemnification. We request only federal evaluation on an accelerated, public, on-the-record track, with a finding (positive or negative) issued within twelve months of acceptance.
