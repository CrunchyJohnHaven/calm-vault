## Calibration honesty

These numbers are large and probability-weighted. Multiple sources of uncertainty:

1. **Scenario probabilities are estimates.** I put them at 40/35/18/6/1.5 but they could realistically be 50/30/15/4/1 or 30/35/25/8/2. Sensitivity to these probabilities is substantial.

2. **Revenue ranges per scenario have ±50% uncertainty.** I gave ranges; the midpoints are central estimates, not point predictions.

3. **Exit-value multiples vary widely.** 5-15x revenue is the band for SaaS / tech infrastructure; for protocols / standards the multiple can be different. Open-protocol businesses sometimes get LOWER multiples (because revenue is harder to capture) and sometimes HIGHER (because the moat is bigger). I used median tech multiples.

4. **5-year horizon is long enough that AI capability progression dominates many variables.** If frontier AI capability advances such that AAOs become trivially easy to set up, the per-AAO setup-services revenue collapses. If frontier AI capability stalls, the AAO category may not compound as fast.

5. **The tail (Scenario E) dominates EV but is hardest to estimate.** Removing the tail entirely cuts EV by ~$2.4B. Keeping the tail at all assumes any non-zero probability of a Stripe-scale outcome, which is itself a substantial claim.

**80% credible intervals (honest):**

- TEV 5-year: $100M-$5B (central ~$840M; very wide)
- Network revenue 5-year cumulative: $5M-$1B (central ~$150M)
- Founder personal under current doctrine: $200K-$25M (central ~$2.5M)
- Founder personal if doctrine relaxed to 10% equity: $1M-$100M (central ~$45M)

The order-of-magnitude is the right way to read these. The probability we are within an order of magnitude of these centrals is reasonably high (~60-70%). The probability we are within 2x is lower (~30-40%).
