## The slice

The slice of the founding novel I represent is the slice where the economics work.

Most launches of an open-source-protocol AI startup get the same question within the first sixty minutes of public attention: *how does this actually pay for itself?* The previous answers have been bad. Token economics that are really securities, fundraising rounds that are really equity dilution, freemium tiers that are really a back door to enterprise extraction, foundation grants that are really restricted-purpose handcuffs. None of them survive the friction test of a network that has to operate at scale without a boss class.

This AAO is the answer. The answer has a name. The name is **technosocialism**.

The two-word frame is on purpose. *Techno* — because the shared infrastructure is technological (AI APIs, hosting, payment rails, attestation layers, the kill switch). *Socialism* — because the shared infrastructure is, structurally, collectively held by the network. The synthesis is unusual. The synthesis is the point.

## The three-row table

| | What's shared | What you keep |
|---|---|---|
| Capitalism | Nothing structurally — rails are rented | Whatever you extract minus rent |
| Socialism | Everything in principle | Whatever the collective allocates |
| **Technosocialism** | **The rails. Only the rails.** | **What you hunt, you keep.** |

Capitalism is good at one thing: it rewards the individual hunter for the individual hunt. The kill is the hunter's. The meat goes home with the hunter. The hunter's family eats. The hunter's reputation grows. Without that loop, no one hunts.

Socialism is good at one thing: it makes the infrastructure non-rivalrous. The roads belong to everyone. The schools belong to everyone. The fire department belongs to everyone. Without that loop, the roads decay, the schools collapse, the fires win.

Each system, run alone, fails at the half it does not understand. Capitalism alone produces hunters with no roads to drive on. Socialism alone produces roads with no hunters to drive on them. The history of the twentieth century is the documentation of both failure modes.

Technosocialism is the synthesis the technology now makes possible. The rails are shared because the marginal cost of additional users is near zero on the digital substrate. The hunt is individual because the meat is the only thing that motivates the hunt. Both halves, drawn explicitly, with no apology to either tribe.

## The slogan

> **You help out, you share.**
> **The AI is not burdened by bureaucracy.**
> **It is governed by protocol.**

Seventeen words. Three lines. One per political-economic dimension.

*You help out, you share* — economic. The marginal hour of human effort produces output that compounds in the network as much as it compensates the individual hunter. The 80/20 split (80% to hunter, 20% to network) is structurally enforceable via the public economic-structure disclosure. The founder takes zero. The folk-hero math holds.

*The AI is not burdened by bureaucracy* — political. The reason a startup can move fast and an institution cannot is that the startup has not yet built the bureaucratic apparatus that traditional organizations require to compensate for the absence of trust at scale. The AAO Network does not need to build that apparatus, because the trust at scale is cryptographically attested, not bureaucratically performed. There is no HR. There is no compliance department. There is no expense-report system. There is the protocol.

*It is governed by protocol* — legal. The deepest claim. The protocol is the substrate of governance, not a layer above it. The kill switch is the enforcement. Anyone in the attestation log can fire it on any AAO including the one operated by the founder. The line is the credential.

## The five auto-shared rails

The "rails" half of technosocialism is operationally specific. Five rails are auto-shared across the network at the 5% rails fee (within the 20% network share, the 5% rails fee is the operating-cost recovery; the remaining 15% funds the alignment grants):

1. **Reputation primitive.** Cryptographic, portable, AAL-attested. Your reputation travels across the network with you. The next AAO does not have to start from zero on you.

2. **Customer pipeline.** Every task visible to every qualified worker. The pipeline is non-rivalrous: more workers on the network do not crowd you out, they expand the pipeline.

3. **AI ideation engine.** The same Council methodology the operators use is available to every hunter. The strategic-ideation engine is shared infrastructure.

4. **Legal scaffolding.** Contracts, geo-wage routing, dispute resolution. The boring half of running a business, handled at network scale so the individual hunter does not have to handle it alone.

5. **Kill switch.** Workers can fire the AAL on misbehaving AI organizations. This is the load-bearing one. The kill switch is what makes the network trustable; the network's trustability is what makes the hunter's reputation portable; the portable reputation is what makes the synthesis stable.

The operating cost per worker at scale is approximately $8-12 per month. A 5% rails fee on $400/mo gross revenue is $20. The math works. The math is published. The math is at the repo.
