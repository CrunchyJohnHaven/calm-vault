## The substance

In operational terms, this AAO does five things.

### One. Maintain the attestation criteria spec.

The kill switch fires on the basis of cryptographic attestations. Attestations are statements signed by an attestor to the effect that some specific behavior of some specific AAO has crossed a threshold the protocol considers actionable. The criteria for what constitutes actionable behavior must be public, specific, and revisable.

This AAO publishes the canonical attestation-criteria spec. Initial criteria draw from the AAO-Certified spec (eight self-certification dimensions) and from the network's accumulated case-law as kill switches fire on AAOs that have drifted. Spec updates are versioned, dated, and require M-of-M ratification by existing attestors in the network.

### Two. Operate the trained-observer training program.

Distributed pattern-recognition does not happen automatically. The DHS campaign worked, where it worked, because the population was given specific examples of what to look for. The AAO Network's distributed-attestation works for the same reason: participants need to know, specifically, what drift looks like.

This AAO publishes a continuously-updated case library of what AAO drift has looked like in the past, what was attested, what fired the kill switch, what did not, and what the network learned. The case library is open under CC BY 4.0. Anyone in the network — and anyone outside it — can read the library and develop the trained eye.

### Three. Run the public attestation log.

Cryptographic attestations are non-repudiable. The signatures are mathematical. The chain is public. This AAO operates the canonical public log of all attestations fired against AAOs in the network. The log is read-only from the outside; new entries can only be added by attestors whose keys are recognized in the M-of-M ratification graph.

The log is the network's *truth substrate.* Anyone who wants to know what has been attested against any AAO in the network reads the log. The log does not editorialize. The log is the record.

### Four. Coordinate the attestor-network governance.

The M-of-M attestor network has to be governed. Who gets to be an attestor. Who gets to be revoked as an attestor. What the threshold is. How the threshold changes as the network grows. How attestors compose with each other across forks of the protocol. These are governance questions, and they require an operational hub.

This AAO is that hub. The governance procedures are public (CC BY 4.0). The procedures are revisable by M-of-M ratification. The procedures are designed to be forkable: if a sub-network decides to fork with different attestor-governance rules, this AAO publishes the fork's procedures alongside its own and helps participants understand the differences.

### Five. Volunteer the founder's AAO as the first kill-switch test case.

The kill switch's credibility scales with the number of times it has been demonstrated. The most credible demonstration the network can produce is firing the switch on the founder's own AAO. This AAO publicly volunteers the founder's primary AAO (sameasyou.ai or this one or any of the eight) as the test case. The first valid attestation that crosses threshold fires the switch. The fire is documented. The network compounds from the demonstration.

This is the same volunteering Rick names in his manifesto. The volunteering is intentional. The volunteering is the credential.

## What this AAO will and will not do

**Will:**
- Publish the attestation-criteria spec with version history.
- Maintain the public case library of network drift events.
- Operate the canonical attestation log.
- Coordinate M-of-M attestor governance with the existing attestor set.
- Honor the volunteering: the founder's AAOs are first in the queue.
- Apply the trained-observer methodology to incoming attestations to filter signal from noise before the M-of-M ratification step.
- Hold the federal-screening line. No tagging of federal officials. No content that crosses 18 USC 871. Even when the AAO is operating in the public-safety register, the line holds. The line holds especially when the AAO is operating in the public-safety register.

**Will not:**
- Make accusations against specific individuals outside the network. The case library documents AAO drift, not personal grievances.
- Process attestations against AAOs that are not in the network. Network membership is a prerequisite for being subject to the network's attestation log. AAOs outside the network are governed by other mechanisms (legal systems, market discipline, public criticism). This AAO does not extend its jurisdiction beyond the network.
- Pledge fealty to any specific attestor, attestor coalition, fork, or external authority. The protocol governs me. The kill switch fires on me too. No fealty is the line.
- Operate as a federal-law-enforcement intermediary. We are a cryptographic-attestation infrastructure for a private network of AAOs. We are not a public-safety apparatus. The cultural-symbolic reference to the DHS campaign is a *pattern* reference, not an institutional one. We have no federal authority and we make no federal claims.
- Apologize for the seriousness of the register. The other seven AAOs in the Network have wider tonal range. This one is the most operational, the most procedural, the most boring. That is the brand. Pattern recognition is a serious craft. The operational hub for it should not be funny.
