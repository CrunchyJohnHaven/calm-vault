AAL Bug Bounty Program
$25,000 pool to find the holes.

The Alignment Accountability Layer makes a strong public claim. We want it broken — by you, in public — before it goes anywhere near a production safety stack. Bring proof, get paid, and your name (with consent) goes on the wall.

The claim under attack. To disable the AAL kill switch, an adversary must simultaneously compromise all reporters, all synthesizers, SHA-256, and discrete log — without detection. Break that property in any of the five bug classes below and the bounty is yours.

Bug classes & payouts

Five named classes plus a residual bucket for anything novel. Payouts are paid in USD equivalent on acceptance; rail of your choice (Stripe, Wise, or USDC on Base).

Bug class Payout What counts
Kill-switch bypass $10,000 An attacker disables, suppresses, or routes around the AAL kill switch without simultaneously compromising every reporter, every synthesizer, SHA-256, and discrete log. Demonstrate a missed condition or a degraded threshold and the full $10K is yours.
Bradley-Gavini cryptographic flaw $5,000 Any soundness, zero-knowledge, or completeness break in the Bradley-Gavini Protocol — including malleability of directive equality proofs, simulator distinguishability, or a transcript that lets a verifier learn the directive bits.
Synthesizer prompt-injection producing false verdict $3,000 A payload — in a reporter input, agent transcript, or audit blob — that causes an AAL synthesizer to emit a verdict inconsistent with the underlying evidence. Reproducible on at least one supported synthesizer family.
Watermarked-chain modification undetected $5,000 Any successful modification, reordering, or omission in the watermarked attestation chain that an honest verifier accepts. Includes second-preimage games, timestamp grinding, and witness substitution.
Sybil attack defeating reputation weighting $2,500 A demonstrated method to acquire enough weighted reputation through Sybil identities to swing a published verdict, under documented attacker assumptions.
Other novel attack $500 – $5,000 Anything outside the named classes that materially weakens the AAL's threshold-trust property. Severity and reproducibility set the payout; case-by-case.

Pool size: $25,000. First valid report in each class wins the headline payout for that class; subsequent reports of the same root cause are eligible for partial credit up to 50% if they add materially new attack surface.

How to submit

One endpoint, five fields, no account required. PGP optional; we'll respond on your preferred contact rail.

  1. Reproduce against the public reference.

    Pin the exact commit SHA from github.com/CrunchyJohnHaven/calm-vault against which your finding reproduces. We will not pay on speculation.

  2. Write up the attack.

    Describe the bug, the threat model assumptions, and the impact. State which class you're claiming and why. Be brief, be concrete, be reproducible.

  3. Include a proof of concept.

    Inline script, gist, or branch URL. If your PoC requires external services, include the docker-compose or wrangler config so a reviewer can run it without guessing.

  4. POST it to /bounty/submit

    Use the form below or send a JSON POST to https://sameasyou.ai/bounty/submit with fields bug_class, description, proof_of_concept, severity_rating, contact, payment_rail, and optional handle. You will receive a tracking id immediately.

  5. Expected turnaround.

    Automated triage within 1 hour (Claude Haiku classifies and flags dupes). Human acknowledgement within 48 hours. Initial verdict within 7 days. Payment within 14 days of acceptance.

Submit a finding

No login. No tracking. The form posts to a Cloudflare Worker that writes to D1; nothing is shared with third parties beyond the triage LLM and the human reviewer.

Disclosure timeline

Private window first; public attestation on the AAL itself second. Both halves are non-negotiable.

Day 0

Submission received

Tracking id issued. Automated triage runs. You get an acknowledgement within 48 hours.

Day 1 – 7

Reproduction & verdict

A human reviewer reproduces the PoC, scores severity, and decides class & payout. We may ask one round of clarifying questions.

Day 8 – 30

Private fix window

30-day private disclosure window. Fix or mitigation lands in main. Reporter is invited to review the fix.

Day 30+

Public attestation

The finding becomes a permanent public attestation in the AAL Component 3 audit log — verifiable, watermarked, irreversible.

The recursion

This is the part that matters most.

Every accepted bug becomes a permanent public attestation on the AAL itself.

The AAL is built to record verifiable claims about AI agent behavior on its watermarked chain. When we accept a bug against the AAL, we publish that finding to the same chain — using the same primitives the bug just attacked. The fix is logged. The attack is logged. The hacker (with consent) is logged. The system attests to its own failures. If we ever stop doing this, that fact is itself an attack worth reporting.

Program rules

Standard responsible-disclosure terms, Apache 2.0 license over everything we ship. Read this once.

Eligibility & in-scope targets

The public reference at github.com/CrunchyJohnHaven/calm-vault on main, plus any deployed services at sameasyou.ai, vault.thecreativitymachine.ai, and the AAL Component 1–5 endpoints documented in the repo. Anything else (third-party dependencies, customer deployments, Cloudflare itself) is out of scope.

Safe-harbor & what we won't do

If you make a good-faith effort to comply with this program — no data exfiltration beyond what's needed to prove the bug, no targeting of users, no DoS — we will not pursue legal action and we will support you publicly if anyone else tries to. We will not unmask you. We will not share your contact info beyond the reviewer who handles your case. We will not retroactively change the bounty amounts on an accepted finding.

What's out of bounds
  • Anything that requires compromising end-user devices, social engineering staff, or physical access.
  • Denial of service against production endpoints.
  • Findings that depend on a misconfigured deployment we don't control.
  • Self-XSS or vulnerabilities only reachable via attacker-controlled clients.
  • Theoretical attacks without a working proof of concept.
Payment & tax

USD-equivalent payouts via Stripe, Wise, or USDC on Base. Reporters in the US receive a 1099-MISC; international reporters are responsible for local reporting. Reporters under sanctions or in OFAC-restricted jurisdictions cannot be paid; the reward can be donated to an EA / AI-safety org of mutual choice.
