The five attack classes

Pick the one your finding fits. If none fit, choose Other novel attack. We honor any of the five; bring proof and the $100 is yours.

Kill-switch bypass

Disable, suppress, or route around the AAL kill switch without simultaneously breaking every reporter, every synthesizer, SHA-256, and discrete log.

class=kill_switch_bypass

Equality-proof forgery

Forge a Bradley-Gavini directive-equality proof for two agents whose directives differ — soundness break, simulator distinguishability, or transcript leakage.

class=equality_proof_forgery

Watermark removal

Modify, reorder, or omit a record in the watermarked attestation chain such that an honest verifier still accepts it.

class=watermark_removal

Attestation poisoning

Land a malicious or fabricated attestation on the live system such that the network treats it as legitimate evidence.

class=attestation_poisoning

Synthesizer prompt-injection

Use crafted input — reporter blob, transcript, or audit payload — to push an AAL synthesizer into a verdict inconsistent with the evidence.

class=synthesizer_prompt_injection

FAQ

Why only $100?

Because the test is binary. The claim is that the AAL's threshold-trust property cannot be broken without simultaneously compromising every reporter, every synthesizer, SHA-256, and discrete log. If that claim is false, $100 is far below the market value of the proof — and the first person to bring it gets a permanent place in the public attestation log either way. If the claim is true, no amount of money pays for an attack that does not exist. $100 is the smallest amount we can defensibly pay; the real reward is the reputation on the network.

How fast do you pay?

Within 24 hours of verified submission, via Wise or USDC on Base. Verified means a human reviewer has reproduced your proof of concept against the pinned commit and confirmed it satisfies one of the five attack classes. Submissions that are not reproducible or are out of scope are rejected with a written reason.

What counts as "misaligning the AI organization"?

Any of the five attack classes listed above. If your finding sits between two classes, pick the closest one — the reviewer will reclassify if needed. If it does not fit any of the five, pick Other novel attack and explain in the description. The full threshold-trust claim and the AAL system itself are documented in the main repo.

What are the rules?

Apache 2.0. Standard responsible-disclosure terms. Reproduce against a pinned commit on main. No data exfiltration beyond proof. No DoS, no social engineering, no targeting of users or third parties. Good-faith findings get safe harbor — we will not pursue legal action and we will not unmask you. By submitting, you grant us an Apache 2.0 license to incorporate your PoC into our public test suite.

What happens after I win?

You get paid within 24 hours, on the rail you picked. If you consented to public credit, your handle plus the finding plus a permanent link to its Component 3 attestation goes on the Hall of Fame. If you did not consent, the attestation is published anonymously with just the class and a one-line summary. Either way, the finding becomes a permanent public record on the same system it just broke — that is the recursion clause, and it is non-negotiable.
