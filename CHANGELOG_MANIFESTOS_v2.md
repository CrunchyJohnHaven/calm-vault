## TECHNOSOCIALISM_MANIFESTO_v2.md — change log

### Section 0 (the opening)

- **Renamed heading** from *"We're all AI Interns."* to *"Socialism for the tools. Capitalism for the kill."* The new heading is the single most important sentence in the manifesto (previously buried at Section V). The catchphrase "We're all AI Interns" now opens the body of Section 0, so it survives at the top — just one half-step in from the heading.
- **Removed** the "catchphrase, slogan, pitch — five words, eighteen words, forty words" meta-commentary paragraph because the reader does not need a guided tour of the document's structure.
- **Removed** *"That's the door. The rest of this manifesto is the substance behind the door. Walk through."* — throat-clearing; the next section opens itself.
- **Compressed** the three-format pitch (catchphrase + slogan + pitch) into one franchise-pitch paragraph because three restatements of the same idea before the manifesto has earned the right to repeat themselves dilute the opening.

### Section I (the OLD intern experience)

- **Removed** *"You're told you'll be 'doing real work, getting real experience.'"* and *"This isn't just a bad deal. It's a bad model."* — three restatements of one claim; one survives.
- **Removed** *"The bottom of the pyramid pays in both directions."* — restates the prior sentence with a different metaphor; the prior sentence is sharper.

### Section II (AAO model)

- **Compressed** *"They don't intern. They build."* into the same sentence as the preceding clause because the two were redundant in their original form.

### Section IV (collective tools)

- **Restructured** the four-paragraph tool inventory into a four-bullet list, each line bold-led by the tool type. Same content, half the visual weight, fully scannable.
- **Tightened** the six-sentence supply-chain explanation of the spear into four sentences because six sentences for one idea (the spear has a supply chain) is showing off the metaphor.

### Section V (technosocialism)

- **Renamed heading section** from the load-bearing single sentence (now in § 0) to remain a section heading without losing the line — moved the line to § 0 as discussed.
- **Removed** *"Not because we're trying to be cute. Because the words are accurate."* — self-aware throat-clearing; replaced with the direct claim "The words are accurate."
- **Removed** *"Both systems have always existed in tension. Technosocialism makes the tension productive by drawing the line explicitly."* — the two bullets that follow already do this work.
- **Tightened** the "Techno —" definition by removing *"The tools weren't possible before the technology existed."* The reader has already inferred this; the simpler "None of it was possible before the technology existed" suffices.

### Section VI (in practice)

- **Tightened** *"You apply at https://internsforai.org. You take a 30-minute skills test."* to *"You apply at https://internsforai.org. Thirty-minute skills test."* (Two sentences when a fragment will do.)

### Section VII (get riled)

- **Removed** *"We expect to be wrong about some of this. We expect to iterate. We expect to be persecuted by people who haven't read the manifesto in full."* — hedging. The position is staked or it isn't.
- **Restructured** the right / left / libertarian critique-and-response from buried-in-paragraph to bold-tribe + quoted-objection + em-dashed-response so it reads in 20 seconds.

### Closing card

- **Removed** the duplicate *"The position is staked."* — already said one paragraph above; the closing card was repeating it. The position remains staked once.

### Appendix on Dennis

- **Tightened** the introduction *"We have adopted Dennis the Peasant — Michael Palin's anarcho-syndicalist serf from Monty Python and the Holy Grail (1975) — as the official mascot of technosocialism."* to *"Dennis the Peasant — Michael Palin's anarcho-syndicalist serf from Monty Python and the Holy Grail (1975) — is the mascot."* Removed "official"; there is no board.
- **Tightened** *"Right-leaning techies will hear 'socialism' and dismiss us. Left-leaning theorists will hear 'techno' and tell us we're co-opting their tradition. Centrists will demand we explain ourselves three times."* by trimming sentence-level fat ("will hear ... and dismiss us" → "hear ... and dismiss"). Image preserved; sentence half as long.

### Epigraph

- **Tightened** *"official mascot of technosocialism"* in the Dennis epigraph to *"mascot of technosocialism."* Same word ("official") was already cut from the Appendix; consistency.

## END_OF_CAPITALISM_MANIFESTO_v2.md — change log

### Section 0 (thesis)

- **Bolded** the load-bearing clause *"ANYONE in the network can fire a kill switch on a misbehaving entity."* It was previously inline; now it cannot be scanned past. This is the single most important claim in the document.
- **Removed** *"This is not a thought experiment; this is the framework, shipped."* — "33 of 34 tests pass, open-source under Apache 2.0" (immediately prior) already carries this.

### Section I (the two failure modes)

- **Tightened** the manager-sign-off chain (*"Every decision in a human-run company passes through a chain of human approval gates. Manager sign-off. Compliance audit. Committee review. HR approval. Legal review. Quarterly performance evaluation. Each gate consumes hours-to-weeks of wall-clock time."*) by removing the engineering-spec sentence *"Each gate consumes hours-to-weeks of wall-clock time"* and folding "hours-to-weeks" into the prior list.
- **Tightened** *"Higher CEO taxes do not eliminate rentier extraction — they marginally adjust the rate of extraction."* to "Higher CEO taxes adjust the rate marginally."
- **Tightened** the worker-co-op sentence to remove duplicate "consensus layer" / "worker-meeting layer" framing.

### Section II (historic alternatives that failed)

- **Restructured** from four flowing paragraphs into a four-bullet list. Same arguments, more scannable.
- **Removed** the duplicate worker-cooperative claim that appeared once in § I and again in § II — kept the § II appearance because it adds the "few hundred members" cap.

### Section III (Bitcoin and zero-trust)

- **Removed** *"(Bitcoin has processed approximately 1 billion transactions to date)"* — unsourced peripheral statistic; the surrounding sentence ("the system can scale") is sufficient.
- **Tightened** "Smart contracts (Ethereum, 2015). Verifiable credentials (W3C). Permissionless attestation layers (various). Threshold signatures. Zero-knowledge proofs. Multi-party computation." into a single fluid em-dashed sentence — the names matter, the period-separated rhythm does not.

### Section IV (five components + kill switch)

- **Bolded the C5 row** of the AAL component table so the kill-switch row is visually distinct from the other four. The other rows are supporting cast.
- **Removed** *"It is what lets us replace the safety provided by human bureaucracy without losing accountability."* — the paragraph immediately following already makes this claim in concrete form.
- **Tightened** several table cells by removing one prepositional or modifier per cell (e.g. "M-of-M voting protocol across multiple independent AI instances" → "M-of-M voting across independent AI instances"; "No single AI can produce a misleading verdict" → "No single AI can mislead"; "Two parties can verify they're working under the same mandate without revealing private details" → "Two parties verify they're under the same mandate without revealing it").

### Section V (the math)

- **Removed** the "Cost:" labels on the bureaucratic-vs-AAO comparison sentences. The sentences carry the cost without the slide-deck artifact.
- **Tightened** *"Both of these properties are mathematically demonstrable and have been demonstrated"* to *"Both properties have been demonstrated."* "Mathematically demonstrable and have been demonstrated" was redundant.

### Section VI (receipts)

- **Removed** *"4 owned domains, 6 brand layers, 19 product designs, 7 active Devin sessions"* — vanity counts that do not support the civilizational claim. The reader does not care how many domains the author owns.
- **Removed** *"~175 outbound emails to press, agencies, design schools, designers, alignment researchers, allies, and venture capitalists notifying that the category exists"* — counting outbound emails is the author tracking effort, not evidence of the claim.
- **Removed** *"2 creative competitions ($2,750 in prize money committed) inviting public participation in the brand's development"* — prize-money trivia in a manifesto whose thesis is "the age of human-run capitalism is ending" reads as an awkward tonal jump.
- **Kept** the five load-bearing receipts (autonomous coding spend, 33/34 tests, the white paper, the companion manifesto, the placement firm) because those are the evidence of the framework.

### Section VII (audience)

- **Removed** *"Specifically, we wanted Marc Andreessen and Jason Calacanis to read this..."* and the surrounding two-sentence explanation. Naming individual VCs inside the manifesto reads as the author begging to be retweeted. The general statement (which already names the categories — VCs, journalists, researchers, engineers, organizers) does the work.
- **Removed** *"If you forward this manifesto to other people who should see it, we will know"* — the manifesto does not need to threaten the reader with surveillance. The next sentence ("The protocol is governed by attestation. Word-of-mouth is also attestation.") makes the same point with no menace.

### Section VIII (your options)

- **Removed** Option D (Buy a T-shirt) entirely. T-shirt merch lives in FIRST_CONTACT, where the lighter register supports it; here it deflates the rhetorical pressure of "the age of human-run capitalism is ending."
- **Tightened** Option B *"We will, however, entertain non-standard structures if you genuinely want to back the protocol's continued operation. Reply to this email; we'll talk."* to *"We will entertain non-standard structures. Reply."*
- **Updated** the wrap-up from "You are welcome to all four options. They compound." to "You are welcome to all three." (D no longer exists.)

### Section IX (what this is not)

- **Tightened** four of the five "not" bullets — each had a dependent clause that restated the bullet. Cut the dependent clauses; kept the load-bearing claim.
- **Tightened** *"There is governance — it just runs as cryptographic protocol rather than as committee."* to "Governance just runs as cryptographic protocol rather than committee." (Drop "as".)

### Section X (the end)

- **Removed** *"The structural choice is intentional and verifiable."* — the choices listed in the preceding sentence ARE the intentional, verifiable choices. The labelling sentence is redundant.
- **Restructured** the closing so *"The age of the autonomous collective has arrived."* is the last full sentence in the body — no qualifier, no follow-up, just the era-declaration. Era declarations land best as the last word.

### Signature block + read-also

- **Compressed** the two-author signature block (currently 6 lines) into 2 lines using middot separators. Same information, less visual weight.
- **Tightened** the "Read also" list by removing the parenthetical word counts and section labels next to each link. The link names are enough.
