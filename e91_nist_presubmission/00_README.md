## Pre-submission strategy — three rules

**1. Engage the venue before petitioning it.** Letters first (`01`, `02`), then workshop attendance (`03`), then informal conversation. By the formal-submission date, NIST staff in the relevant divisions already know who we are and what category we propose.

**2. Co-authors widen credibility.** A submission with academic co-authors from biometrics, cryptography, and disability advocacy survives review better than a single-collective filing. Outreach in `04` starts in Q2 2026 so co-authors shape the document rather than rubber-stamp it.

**3. Lead the public story with technical achievement, not with the submission.** Per `06`, press leads with the Everest 92 open-source release and first independent verification (Everest 100), not with "we are submitting to NIST." NIST relationship matures privately; the submission is mentioned to press only after NIST acknowledges engagement.
