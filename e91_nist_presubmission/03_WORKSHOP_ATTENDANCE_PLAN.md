# Workshop Attendance Plan — NIST and US AISI Pre-Submission Engagement

**Window:** 2026-06 through 2027-09
**Owner:** Standards-engagement liaison (per Everest 91)
**Source for dates:** publicly announced NIST/US AISI cadences as of 2026-05-20; precise dates confirmed via each event's registration page.

---

## Operating principles

**Listen before speaking.** First attendance is observational — track topics, identify staff and working-group leads, learn the community's vocabulary, then propose presentation content.

**Send the right representative for the room.** Technical workshops get the technical lead; ethics-and-governance workshops get a DERB representative; policy events get the standards-engagement liaison plus, when warranted, the principal.

**Pre-workshop materials are prepared, not improvised.** Every workshop produces, in advance, a one-page brief describing the category and the specific topic. This is the artifact handed to anyone met who asks for follow-up.

## Workshop calendar

### W1 — NIST AI RMF Community of Interest Meeting

- **Expected window:** July–September 2026 (quarterly cadence)
- **Relevance:** Broadest US AI-standards COI venue. *Autonomous-agent attestation* fits inside AI RMF's "Manage" and "Govern" functions. Initial attendance observational; second attendance (Q4 2026 / Q1 2027) is the venue at which we propose discussing the category — if the AISI engagement letter has by then produced a positive enough signal.
- **Representation:** Standards-engagement liaison only at first meeting.
- **Pre-workshop materials:** One-page category brief; one-page mapping of user-state attestation to AI RMF "Manage" and "Govern" functions.
- **Post-workshop deliverable:** Notes on relevant staff, working-group affiliations, adjacent topics.

### W2 — US AI Safety Institute Public Engagement Event

- **Expected window:** October–December 2026
- **Relevance:** Primary venue for the AISI submission to be foreshadowed. By Fall 2026 the engagement letter (`01`) should have been acknowledged; this workshop is the first in-person introduction. If unacknowledged by the registration cutoff, we attend anyway as the public.
- **Representation:** Standards-engagement liaison + DERB chair where feasible. Principal attends if agenda lists topics where principal presence carries weight.
- **Pre-workshop materials:** Full pre-submission packet (this directory); two-page protocol overview; DERB membership and charter; anticipated FAR/FRR result framing.
- **Post-workshop deliverable:** Public note on Calm Witness site, neutral in tone, no over-promising on NIST signal.

### W3 — NIST FIPS Standards-Development Public Meeting

- **Expected window:** Q4 2026 or Q1 2027 (by NIST announcement)
- **Relevance:** Cryptographic-protocol angle. Calm Witness is not itself a FIPS-track artifact (it composes primitives, it is not a primitive), but the FIPS-track audience overlaps with the ITL reviewers who will eventually evaluate the formal submission.
- **Representation:** Cryptographic-security reviewer (an academic cryptographer co-author per `04`) + standards-engagement liaison. The academic cryptographer leads any public discussion.
- **Pre-workshop materials:** Detailed cryptographic-construction document; threat model; PQ migration story (Everest 96 design summary).
- **Post-workshop deliverable:** Notes on relevant standards-development direction; any FIPS-track items affecting Calm Witness's choice of underlying primitives.

### W4 — NIST Privacy Engineering Workshop

- **Expected window:** Spring/Summer 2027
- **Relevance:** Privacy-engineering at NIST is where the *privacy-preserving disclosure* framing resonates most clearly. The Privacy Engineering Program has produced SP 800-53-adjacent guidance and is a natural conversational partner.
- **Representation:** Standards-engagement liaison + DERB chair + disability-advocacy co-author (per `04`); the consent calculus benefits most from disability-advocate presence.
- **Pre-workshop materials:** Consent calculus (Everest 8 summary); per-class refusal-disposition documentation; privacy claims and threat model.
- **Post-workshop deliverable:** Public memo on Calm Witness composition with NIST privacy-engineering guidance; submitted to public comment record for any open Privacy Engineering Program comment period.

### W5 — NIST Cryptographic Standards Public Workshop

- **Expected window:** Summer/Fall 2027 (post-submission)
- **Relevance:** Post-submission venue at which the formal submission can be discussed publicly with the cryptographic standards community. Peer cryptographers outside the co-author group offer public feedback that shapes the iteration cycle.
- **Representation:** Full team: liaison, cryptographic-security reviewer, DERB chair, principal if available.
- **Pre-workshop materials:** Submission packet (then on file); any NIST initial commentary; preliminary FAR/FRR re-characterization update.
- **Post-workshop deliverable:** Iteration plan — comments received, accepted, deferred, rejected (with reasoning); published openly.

### W6 — US AISI Sectoral Workshops (rolling)

- **Expected window:** Announced on the AISI public-engagement calendar
- **Relevance:** Variable. Track for topics where autonomous-agent user-state attestation is relevant (financial-services AI, healthcare AI where principal-state disclosure is in scope). Skip topics where it isn't.
- **Representation:** Standards-engagement liaison only unless topic warrants more. Each individual workshop is a judgement call.
- **Pre-workshop materials:** Targeted brief for the sectoral topic; do not over-pitch in sectoral venues whose primary focus is elsewhere.
- **Post-workshop deliverable:** Note on whether the sectoral context is one where Calm Witness composes well.
