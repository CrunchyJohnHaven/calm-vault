# Press and Public-Engagement Notes — Pre-Submission Phase

**Window:** Active 2026-06 onward. Most press contact held until Everest 92 (open-source release) and Everest 100 (first independent third-party verification) are public.
**Owner:** Standards-engagement liaison; principal (John Bradley) makes the final call on any press conversation.

---

## Lead story sequencing — the rule

**Do not lead the public story with "we are submitting to NIST."**

The public story leads with the technical and ethical maturity of the protocol: Everest 92 open-source release, FAR/FRR empirical characterization (Everest 40), first independent third-party verification (Everest 100), and DERB operation. The NIST relationship is mentioned only after NIST itself has acknowledged engagement, and even then framed factually ("we have been engaging with NIST and US AISI on pre-submission consultation") rather than as a headline.

Reason: press coverage framing Calm Witness as "headed for the NIST seal of approval" creates pressure on NIST reviewers in a direction bad for the standards process and bad for the protocol. The standards process works best when no venue feels negotiated against in public. We protect it by patience.

## What we will say if asked about NIST before submission

Approved response: *"We have begun pre-submission engagement with NIST and the US AI Safety Institute, in the same way any open-source cryptographic-protocol project does when it thinks the category is ready for standardization. A formal submission, if filed, will be public at the time of filing. We are not in a position to predict the standards-body outcome and we do not want to anticipate it. The technical work, the FAR/FRR characterization, the ethics review — those are the story right now."*

On the record. Does not over-claim, does not deny, does not put the standards process under unnecessary public pressure.

## What we will not say

- "NIST is considering Calm Witness for standardization." (NIST has not, and may never, said this.)
- NIST staff or working-group leads by name, even after first contact.
- Anchor the press story to the AISI engagement letter or ITL parallel letter. Those are diplomatic artifacts, not press materials.
- Frame Calm Witness as "America's answer to" any other party's protocol. The story is the protocol, the ethics, and the math, not the geopolitics.

Formal-submission-window inbound gets the "we have engaged" response and a pointer to the public submission once filed.
