# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement — Calm Witness v0 Audit Engagement

**Template version**: 1.0
**Effective on signature by both parties**

---

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into between:

- **Calm**: Creativity Machine LLC, on behalf of the Calm Witness operator and the Calm collective ("Calm").
- **Auditor**: `[Vendor legal entity, registered address]` ("Auditor").

Calm and Auditor are each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

This Agreement governs Confidential Information exchanged in connection with the Parties' evaluation of, and engagement in, the third-party security audit of the Calm Witness protocol described in the accompanying Statement of Work template (the "Purpose"). It applies during the proposal phase, during any audit engagement that follows, and for the survival period defined below.

## 1. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other (the "Receiving Party") in connection with the Purpose, in any form (written, oral, electronic, demonstrative). Without limitation, Confidential Information includes:

- The contents of the audit packet (specifications, source code, test corpora, threat models, cryptographic construction notes, dependency SBOM, known-issue lists, prior-review notes).
- Audit findings, draft reports, preliminary findings, internal communications, status updates.
- Vendor proposals, pricing, team allocations, and methodologies.
- The identities of Calm contributors and DERB members participating in the engagement, where not already public.
- Any information marked or identified as confidential at the time of disclosure, or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature.

Information that is in source-code form and published under Apache 2.0 in the public `calm-witness` repository is not Confidential Information once public, except that pre-publication versions, audit-frozen tags, and any unreleased modifications shared under this Agreement remain Confidential Information.

## 2. Exceptions

Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

(a) is or becomes generally publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
(b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, free of any confidentiality obligation, as evidenced by written records;
(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;
(d) is rightfully received from a third party who has the right to disclose it without confidentiality obligation; or
(e) is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or government regulation, provided that the Receiving Party promptly notifies the Disclosing Party (where lawful) and cooperates with any effort to seek a protective order or limit the disclosure.

## 3. Obligations

Each Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(b) protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance, and in no event less than reasonable care;
(c) disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, contractors, and advisors who (i) have a need to know for the Purpose, (ii) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement, and (iii) have been informed of the confidential nature of the information;
(d) not reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to derive Confidential Information through indirect means, except where reverse engineering is the stated subject of the audit work product;
(e) maintain a list of personnel with access to Confidential Information and provide it to the Disclosing Party on reasonable request;
(f) promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, use, or loss of Confidential Information.

## 4. No License

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any right or license to the other Party's Confidential Information, intellectual property, trademarks, or trade secrets, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose. All ownership rights remain with the Disclosing Party.

The disposition of audit-findings ownership and methodology IP is governed by the Statement of Work, not by this Agreement; in the event of conflict, the SoW controls.

## 5. Term and Survival

This Agreement is effective on the date of last signature below.

- **Active term**: continues for the duration of the proposal phase and any resulting audit engagement, plus 3 years from final engagement deliverable acceptance.
- **Survival for cryptographic and source-code material**: confidentiality obligations on cryptographic construction notes, unpublished source code, threat-model details, and pre-publication findings survive for 7 years from disclosure.
- **Trade secrets**: confidentiality obligations on Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret continue for as long as the information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

Termination of any engagement does not terminate confidentiality obligations under this Agreement.

## 6. Return or Destruction of Materials

Upon (i) the conclusion of the engagement (acceptance of the final deliverable), (ii) written request from the Disclosing Party, or (iii) termination of any engagement, the Receiving Party shall, within 30 calendar days:

(a) return all tangible Confidential Information to the Disclosing Party, OR
(b) securely destroy all Confidential Information in its possession (electronic and physical), AND
(c) provide a written certification, signed by an officer of the Receiving Party, attesting to the return or destruction.

The Receiving Party may retain:

- One archival copy of final delivered work product (signed reports) for the Receiving Party's own legal-compliance, insurance, and professional-records purposes. This copy remains subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.
- Electronic copies in backup systems where deletion is technically impracticable, provided such copies are not actively accessed and remain subject to confidentiality.

## 7. Independence and Conflicts

Auditor represents and warrants:

(a) Auditor has no consulting, development, or financial relationship with any Calm contributor, Creativity Machine LLC, or affiliated organizations that would impair audit independence.
(b) No named individual on the proposed engagement team holds equity in, or receives compensation from, the Calm collective or its affiliates.
(c) Auditor will promptly notify Calm if any such conflict arises during the engagement, and Auditor will work in good faith to mitigate or, if necessary, withdraw.

## 8. No Solicitation

During the active term plus 12 months thereafter, neither Party will directly solicit for employment any individual employed by the other Party who participated substantively in the engagement, without the other Party's prior written consent. General job-board postings and responses to unsolicited inquiries are not "direct solicitation" for this purpose.

## 9. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages are inadequate. The non-breaching Party is entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to any other remedy available at law or equity. The prevailing Party in any action arising from this Agreement is entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.
