## §1 — Convening Framing & Purpose

### §1.1 — What this convening is

The first Calm-Suite Multi-Stakeholder Convening is a **formal, three-day treaty-negotiation workshop** held at a neutral international venue. Its purpose is to:

1. Review the preliminary draft of **Treaty-Grade Governance** (Everest 215, TREATY_GRADE_GOVERNANCE_DRAFT_v0.md) in its full article-by-article form.
2. Solicit written and oral comment from state actors, multilateral bodies, standards organizations, academic experts, and civil-society representatives.
3. Produce a **revised governance draft** (v1) incorporating substantive feedback.
4. Publish comprehensive **minutes and decision records** with full attribution (Chatham House Rule for specified sessions, on-the-record for plenary).
5. Establish the formal **Calm Treaty Standing Committee** (Article 8 of the draft treaty) with initial procedural rules and the next convening's schedule.

This convening is **not** the opening of formal treaty negotiation under a depositary; it is the second-stage deliberation that precedes such negotiation. The first stage (Year 1) was written-comment circulation; this is the oral-argument + peer-review stage. The third stage (Years 3–5) is soft-law adoption and formal negotiation.

### §1.2 — Format

**Three days, hybrid in-person + virtual:**

- **In-person attendance:** ≥ 100 total participants at the physical venue, including all state delegations, multilateral body representatives, and standards-body leads.
- **Virtual attendance:** Open to registered observers and secondary participants across all time zones; streamed sessions with Zoom/WebRTC.
- **Language support:** Simultaneous interpretation in English, French, Spanish, Mandarin, Japanese, Russian; documents available in all six languages.
- **Accessibility:** All venues wheelchair-accessible; real-time captioning for all sessions; accessible digital materials (WCAG 2.2 AA); quiet rooms for neurodiverse attendees; childcare provided.

### §1.3 — Convening principles

1. **Parity of voice:** All invited categories (state, multilateral, standards, civil society) have floor access proportional to their representation.
2. **Transparency:** All plenary sessions are on the record and streamed. Closed sessions use Chatham House Rule; decisions are published.
3. **Consensus-seeking, not voting:** The Standing Committee adopts decisions by consensus with documented dissents; where consensus is impossible, the majority view + minority view are both recorded.
4. **Disability-first accessibility:** All facilities, agendas, and materials are designed from the start for disabled participants, not retrofitted.
5. **No pre-negotiated outcomes:** The convening is not stage-managed; the revised draft reflects the genuine deliberation that occurs, not a pre-agreed text.

## §2 — Venue, Dates & Logistics

### §2.1 — Venue selection criteria

- **Neutral jurisdiction:** Not controlled by any single state actor; preferably a city with existing multilateral treaty-negotiation infrastructure.
- **Accessibility:** Excellent public transit, medical facilities, disability services, quiet spaces.
- **Linguistic diversity:** Host nation or region where multiple working languages are native or routine.
- **Size:** Main plenary hall for ≥ 150, breakout spaces for ≥ 6 simultaneous sessions, social spaces for informal dialogue.

### §2.2 — Recommended venues

**Primary:** Geneva, Switzerland (UN Office, CERN facilities, established multilateral infrastructure, strong disability accommodations, four working languages routine).

**Secondary:** Vienna, Austria (OSCE venues, similar multilingual + accessibility profile).

**Tertiary:** Brussels, Belgium (EU facilities, strong accessibility standards, multinational experience).

### §2.3 — Suggested timing

**Q2 2028 (April–June, Northern Hemisphere spring).** Two 10-day windows:

- **April 8–10, 2028** (2nd–4th week of April)
- **May 13–15, 2028** (2nd–3rd week of May)

Avoid July–August (Northern Hemisphere summer, vacation patterns disrupt attendance); avoid December–January (winter holidays).

### §2.4 — Logistics infrastructure

**On-site:**
- Main plenary hall (capacity ≥ 150, accessible stage, accessible seating, real-time captioning screen, large-print materials at registration).
- Three parallel breakout rooms for thematic working sessions (refusal floor, arbitration design, ratification pathway).
- One closed session room (Chatham House Rule; separately recorded).
- Accessible beverage + meal areas; prayer/meditation rooms; childcare facility; accessible restrooms; accessible parking.

**Digital:**
- Secure Zoom webinar + WebRTC gateway (not dependent on any single vendor's infrastructure).
- Document-sharing portal with version control (credentials for live markup + feedback).
- Real-time closed captioning + on-demand transcript publication within 24 hours.
- Accessibility-compliant PDF + HTML + ePub copies of all materials on launch.

**Support staff:**
- Professional facilitators trained in multilingual, disability-centered consensus-building.
- Accessibility coordinators on-site (English-speaking + at least one coordinator fluent in secondary language of each attending region).
- Convening secretariat (≥ 3 people) managing logistics, minutes, document version control.

## §10 — Risk Mitigation & Contingencies

### §10.1 — Geopolitical risks

**Risk:** Key state actor (e.g., US, EU, China) declines to attend or withdraws mid-convening.

**Mitigation:** All invitation letters emphasize that participation is non-binding + convening is consultative (not a treaty negotiation). Standing Committee constitutes even if not all expected states attend; minutes record absences.

### §10.2 — Accessibility failures

**Risk:** Planned accessibility features (interpretation, captioning, accessible venue) fail or inadequate.

**Mitigation:** Backup vendors contracted for interpretation + captioning ≥ 60 days prior; convening insurance covers accessibility-failure liability; accessibility coordinator + disability-rights organizations have authority to pause sessions if accessibility threshold breached.

### §10.3 — Procedural deadlock

**Risk:** Breakout groups or Standing Committee cannot reach consensus; convening ends without substantive progress on key articles.

**Mitigation:** Dissent recording + escalation mechanism built into Minutes Template §7.2. Dissents are not failures; they are data for subsequent deliberation. Revised draft v1 incorporates dissent narratives + notes open questions for Year 3.

### §10.4 — Security & harassment

**Risk:** Participants (especially harm-affected representatives + activists) face threats or harassment during convening.

**Mitigation:** Participant safety protocol drafted by convening secretariat ≥ 90 days prior. On-site security liaison (non-armed, trained in deescalation). Pseudonym options for harm-affected speakers; confidential attendance list not published. Crisis-line contact + safe space available 24/7.

### §10.5 — Virtual-participation failures

**Risk:** Internet connectivity disruptions isolate virtual participants; quorum depends on virtual attendance.

**Mitigation:** Quorum defined as ≥ 50% in-person + ≥ 20% virtual (i.e., virtual participation is valued but not structural single-point-of-failure). Backup dial-in numbers (phone conference) + offline chat transcript (email-based) available if Zoom fails.

### Appendix C: Chatham House Rule Clarification Document

```
# CHATHAM HOUSE RULE — CONVENING OPERATIONS

The Chatham House Rule applies to DESIGNATED CLOSED SESSIONS ONLY:
- Breakout A: Refusal-floor tightening (Day 1 afternoon)
- Breakout D: Arbitration case pathways (Day 2 afternoon)

## How Chatham House Rule Works

**What you can share outside the room:**
- "A disability-rights representative expressed concern about..."
- "A state legal adviser noted that..."
- "An academic cryptographer suggested..."
- General themes and consensus findings

**What you CANNOT share outside the room:**
- Names of specific individuals + what they said
- Quotes attributed to an individual
- Specific organizational positions (unless the organization has publicly stated them)
- Information that would allow reasonable inference of who said what

## When Rule DOES NOT Apply

**On-the-record sessions** (Plenary + Open Breakouts B, C, E, F):
- Full attribution is expected
- Names, quotes, affiliations are published in minutes
- Sessions are streamed and archived publicly

## Violations

If you violate Chatham House Rule by attributing information to a specific person or organization:
- Convening secretariat will contact you for correction within 48 hours
- Corrected version will be published; original removed
- Repeated violations may result in exclusion from future closed sessions

## Questions?

Email accessibility-coordinator@calm-witness.dev or ask a facilitator during the convening.
```
