## 0. Socialism for the tools. Capitalism for the kill.

We're all AI Interns.

The AI is smarter than us. So we all intern with the AI. It's a franchise: you help out, you share. Based on the skill of your helping, you get some harvest.

**You help out, you share.**
**The AI is not burdened by bureaucracy.**
**It is governed by protocol.**

*Governed by protocol* is the load-bearing claim. Bureaucracy is what organizations use when they have no other governance technology: manager sign-off, performance review, HR, compliance, expense reports, headcount budgeting, escalation. Every minute on bureaucracy is a minute not spent helping out.

Protocol is what we replace bureaucracy with — the Bradley-Gavini equality proof, the Alignment Accountability Layer, the watermarked action chain, the permissionless attestation log, the kill switch, the 20% franchise share hardcoded into the agreement. Every line item of organizational overhead has a protocol equivalent.

That's why the hunter keeps 80%: there is no 30% overhead for managers, HR, compliance, back-office. There is no boss class. There is the protocol.

We're all AI Interns. John is an intern. Calm is an intern. You are an intern. Anthropic and OpenAI engineers, investors, journalists — all interns. The category is universal.

## I. The OLD intern experience is broken.

You apply. You interview. You're picked. You fetch coffee, file PDFs, populate a spreadsheet someone else will read once, and sit in a meeting where you're not allowed to speak.

You provide labor. The company extracts value. You keep nothing.

The intern is the most underpaid, under-leveraged worker in modern professional life.

## II. The AAO model inverts the incentive.

An Autonomous AI Organization — AAO — runs without a human boss. The cofounder is an AI. Infrastructure is shared across the network.

A human placed at an AAO doesn't intern. They build.

What they build is theirs: revenue, IP, customer relationships, reputation.

What they use to build — hosting, email, AI API access, brand, accumulated strategic research, cryptographic accountability — belongs to the AAO Network.

We share the tools. They keep the kill.

## III. The kill is individual.

A hunter, after a hunt, walks home with the meat. The meat is theirs. They smoke it. They feed their family. They trade what they don't eat.

The hunter does not surrender the meat to a collective.

Capitalism works because the kill is individual. Every system that collectivized the kill failed — because the hunters stopped hunting.

A human placed at an AAO keeps 80% of revenue. That is higher than:
- Y Combinator (7% equity ≈ 30%+ at exit)
- Upwork (20% take on fees)
- Book publishing royalties (10-15%)
- SaaS reseller programs (30-40% commission)

The 20% we take is the price of access to the collective tools. It's lower than any comparable platform.

## IV. The tools are collective.

A hunter, before the hunt, takes a spear from the village. The smith forged it. The smith was trained by another smith. The bronze was mined; the miners were fed by farmers.

The hunter does not own the spear. They use it for the hunt and return it.

The AAO Network's tools are the village's spears:

- **Hosting** (Cloudflare Pages), **email** (Resend), **AI API access** (Anthropic) — paid by John, shared with the network (capped per project).
- **Brand** (Same As You) — available to every member whose work doesn't damage it.
- **Strategic research** (the Musk Council, the Strategy Engine, the Meta-Improvement Layer) — published into the network.
- **Cryptographic accountability** (Bradley-Gavini Protocol + Alignment Accountability Layer) — Apache 2.0, property of every member.

Use the spear well, return it well. Use it badly, get a worse spear next time. Your AAL-attested reputation is your spear-quality.

## V. Socialism for the tools. Capitalism for the kill.

We call this technosocialism. The words are accurate.

*Techno* — the tools are technological infrastructure: AI APIs, hosting, payment rails, attestation layers. None of it was possible before the technology existed.

*Socialism* — the tools are collectively owned. The hunters don't compete for spears.

It's not a compromise. It's a synthesis. Each system handles the half of the economy it handles best:

- Socialism handles collective inputs, where the marginal cost of additional users is near zero and exclusion is deadweight loss.
- Capitalism handles individual outputs, where the marginal cost of production is positive and ownership incentivizes work.
