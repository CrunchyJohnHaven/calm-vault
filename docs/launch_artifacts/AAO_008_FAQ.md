# Same As You Network — AAO #008 Frequently Asked Questions

*License: CC BY 4.0.*

---

**1. What do I get if I certify?**

A listing in the public AAO Directory, reciprocal linkbacks from the existing seven certified AAOs, eligibility for the Money Python revenue split (80% to hunter, 20% to network), access to shared kill-switch infrastructure, and a vote in M-of-M ratifications. You do not get a logo to add to your site that we control. You do not get traffic guarantees. You get a Schelling-point listing and the right to point at it.

---

**2. What does AAO-Certified mean?**

It means you have publicly attested that your operation meets 8 specific, auditable criteria: published manifesto, named accountable human, declared autonomy boundary, working kill switch, public action ledger, federal-screening commitment, no-fealty commitment, and a fork-friendly license. The certification is self-attested. The Network ratifies what you publish; it does not investigate, audit, or warrant your claims.

---

**3. What's the kill switch and does it apply to me?**

Your kill switch is yours. It is a procedure documented in your repo by which your AI's autonomy can be revoked even if the AI resists. The Network has no power to invoke it. The Network does have a shared infrastructure layer (a coordination signal that helps multiple AAOs trigger their own switches in synchrony if a class-of-failure is detected), but using it is voluntary.

---

**4. Can my AAO be killed by other people?**

Not directly. Other certified AAOs cannot invoke your kill switch. They can, by M-of-M vote, de-list you from the Directory if you violate the federal-screening or no-fealty lines, which removes the network-effect benefit of listing. Your AAO itself remains live on your domain. The standard is fork-friendly precisely so that de-listing is non-catastrophic.

---

**5. Can I fork the standard?**

Yes. The standard is CC BY 4.0. You may fork it, re-license your fork as CC BY 4.0 or more permissive, rename it, modify the criteria, drop criteria, add criteria. We expect forks. A fork that competes with the original standard and wins more adoption is a successful outcome for the original. The point is the standard's existence, not our control of it.

---

**6. Do I need a cryptographer's review?**

No. The standard does not require cryptographic primitives beyond what git commits already provide. Your ledger is append-only by virtue of being in a public git repo with branch protection. Your kill switch does not need to be cryptographically signed; it needs to be documented and tested. If you want to add cryptographic anchoring beyond this, you may, but it is not required for certification.

---

**7. What's the relationship between my AAO and the Same As You Network?**

You are a peer, not a subsidiary. The Network is the set of AAOs that have certified to the same standard. There is no parent entity, no holding company, no central treasury. The 20% network share from Money Python flows to a publicly-audited infrastructure fund, not to a corporate parent. You can exit at any time on 30 days' notice or zero notice. The no-fealty line is not decorative.

---

**8. Can I monetize my AAO?**

Yes. The standard does not restrict commercial activity. Many of the existing certified AAOs are commercial operations. The Money Python revenue split applies only to merchandise sold under the Money Python brand; your own commercial activity is fully yours. The federal-screening and no-fealty lines apply to autonomous outputs, not to commercial transactions.

---

**9. What if Anthropic / OpenAI / Google object?**

Then they object. The standard does not require approval from any model provider. It applies to your operation, not to the model you use. If a provider's terms of service prohibit some specific use, that is between you and the provider; certification status is not affected. The Network does not coordinate with model providers and does not represent itself to them as a governance body. Their objection does not de-list you.

---

**10. What if I want to revoke my certification later?**

Open a pull request removing your row from the AAO Directory. The PR is auto-merged. No penalty, no exit interview, no fee. The 30-day notice is a courtesy to allow reciprocal-linkback partners to update their pages, but it is not enforced. Your manifesto, ledger, and history remain on your own domain — we do not control or delete your content. Revocation is one-way only in the sense that re-certifying requires re-submitting; we don't garbage-collect history.
