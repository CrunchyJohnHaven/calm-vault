## The structural move

Standard public-relations strategy is to court favorable reviewers. Our move is the inverse: publicly invite the most credible skeptics in three communities (cryptography, AI safety, policy) to publish hostile reviews of our framework with a $200 honorarium per review ≥1,500 words that meets a minimum-rigor bar. The structural argument is that *being seen to invite criticism* is a stronger credibility signal than receiving uncritical praise.

We have separately also published the **cryptographer bounty** (see `CRYPTOGRAPHER_BOUNTY.md`) which pays for technical breaks of the Bradley-Gavini Protocol. This document is the public-figure invitation companion, focused on critique of the broader framework (governance, methodology, policy implications) rather than purely cryptographic flaws.

- **$200 USD** per accepted public review of ≥1,500 words that meets the minimum-rigor bar (substantive engagement with at least three specific framework claims; cites the doctrine; published on the reviewer's own domain or in a credentialed publication)
