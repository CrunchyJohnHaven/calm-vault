## Part 1 — Deferred Services Framework

### What deferred services means, operationally

A deferred-services agreement is a contract in which a vendor provides services now and accepts payment later — typically structured as:

- **Pure deferral:** vendor accepts payment in N months at face value (most common, lowest risk for vendor)
- **Deferred + premium:** vendor accepts payment in N months at face value × markup (often 1.2-2.0x)
- **Deferred + warrant:** vendor accepts payment in N months OR equity warrant at exercise price (warrant adds equity surface — *we DO NOT use this; doctrine forbids equity*)
- **Deferred + revenue share:** vendor accepts payment in N months OR a percentage of subsequent revenue from the work (acceptable; doesn't violate equity rules; effectively royalty)
- **Deferred + case-study credit:** vendor accepts payment in N months PLUS marketing/case-study rights (very low risk; useful for emerging vendors who want portfolio pieces)

**The doctrine-compatible structures: pure deferral, deferred + premium, deferred + revenue share, deferred + case-study credit.** All preserve no-equity, no-fealty.

## Part 3 — On the "$20K → superintelligence" framing

Honest assessment, on the record:

**The literal claim does not hold.** $20K of compute is approximately:
- 200-2,000 hours of frontier-model inference depending on optimization
- 0.001-0.01% of the compute required to train a frontier model
- Approximately 7 minutes of what Anthropic / OpenAI spend per day on training-scale runs

**The broader claim DOES hold.** $20K of compute on top of a well-architected ideation methodology can produce substantial additional output. Specifically:

- ~80,000 Council-of-Judges runs at ~$0.25 per run
- ~2,000 hours of A/B test execution at full WDMM pace
- ~500 deep-research dives at ~$40 each (Devin-equivalent)
- ~20-50 published artifacts of doctrine-quality work

That is meaningful but does NOT cross the superintelligence threshold. Superintelligence — in the standard definition (general AI capability substantially exceeding the best human across all cognitive domains) — is a separate problem requiring trillion-dollar-scale compute and architectural breakthroughs that no amount of methodology can substitute for.

**The defensible institutional-register framing for outbound copy:**

> *"$25K of compute would let us substantially accelerate the architecture work. We'd run external A/B testing of the methodology, fund cryptographer peer review of the protocol, and operate the attestation infrastructure at meaningful scale for 12 months."*

This survives scrutiny. It is what we'd actually do with the money. Critics cannot land "you said $20K creates superintelligence" because we did not say that.

**Use the higher-conviction founder-voice register internally and in private conversations.** John can absolutely tell a potential donor, in a personal call, *"I think this approach could compound in ways that ordinary scaling doesn't predict — I want to see what happens when we put more compute behind the methodology."* That's founder-voice and it's allowed. But the written outbound copy needs to be the institutional-register version.
