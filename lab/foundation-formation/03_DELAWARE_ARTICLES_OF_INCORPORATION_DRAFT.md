## ARTICLE III. PURPOSES

**Section 1. Exempt purposes.** The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code") (or the corresponding provision of any future United States internal revenue law), including, for such purposes:

(a) the **advancement of science** within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(5), through the conduct, sponsorship, and free public dissemination of original cryptographic research relating to the governance of autonomous artificial-intelligence agents, including but not limited to the public-protocol research described in the founder's prior published work;

(b) the **lessening of the burdens of government** within the meaning of Rev. Rul. 85-1, 1985-1 C.B. 177, and related authorities, through the development, maintenance, and free publication of the **AAO-Governed certification standard** — a publicly available, open, neutral standard available to federal, state, and foreign governments for the regulation, attestation, and accountability of autonomous artificial-intelligence agents;

(c) the **operation of a public bounty pool** funding independent third-party verification of, and the identification of failures or vulnerabilities in, AAO-Governed implementations and the protocol's security analysis; and

(d) **educational activities** within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(3), including the publication of papers, books, explainers, technical documentation, and curricular materials relating to the foregoing, and the giving of presentations, lectures, and workshops on those subjects.

**Section 2. Restriction to exempt activities.** Notwithstanding any other provision of this Certificate of Incorporation, the Corporation shall not carry on any other activities not permitted to be carried on (a) by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or (b) by a corporation contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code.

**Section 3. No political campaign intervention.** No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation (except as otherwise permitted by Section 501(h) of the Code if the Corporation makes the election thereunder), and the Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of statements), any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

**Section 4. No private inurement; no private benefit.** No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services actually rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in Section 1 of this Article III.

## ARTICLE IV. NONSTOCK; EXEMPT CORPORATION

**Section 1. Nonstock corporation.** The Corporation is a **nonstock corporation**. The Corporation is **not authorized to issue capital stock**.

**Section 2. Exempt-corporation status.** The Corporation is organized to qualify as an "exempt corporation" under Section 391(j) of Title 8 of the Delaware Code on the basis that the Corporation (i) is not authorized to issue capital stock, (ii) is organized exclusively for charitable purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, and (iii) is intended to be exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, and on the basis that no part of the Corporation's net earnings inures to the benefit of any member or individual.

**Section 3. Members.** The Corporation **shall have no members**. The rights that would otherwise be vested in members are vested in the Corporation's Board of Directors.

## ARTICLE VI. LIMITATION ON DIRECTOR LIABILITY

To the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law as it now exists or may hereafter be amended (including Section 102(b)(7) thereof), a director of the Corporation shall not be liable to the Corporation for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty as a director. Any amendment, repeal, or modification of this Article VI shall not adversely affect any right or protection of a director of the Corporation existing at the time of such amendment, repeal, or modification.

---

## ARTICLE VII. INDEMNIFICATION

The Corporation shall, to the fullest extent permitted by Section 145 of the Delaware General Corporation Law as it now exists or may hereafter be amended, indemnify any person who was or is a party or is threatened to be made a party to any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative (other than an action by or in the right of the Corporation) by reason of the fact that such person is or was a director or officer of the Corporation, or is or was serving at the request of the Corporation as a director, officer, employee, or agent of another corporation, partnership, joint venture, trust, or other enterprise. The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any such person as permitted by Delaware law.

**Indemnification of employees and agents.** The Corporation may, in the discretion of the Board, indemnify any employee or agent of the Corporation on the same or different terms as the indemnification of directors and officers above.

**No indemnification contrary to § 501(c)(3).** No indemnification shall be provided to the extent it would (a) constitute private inurement under Section 501(c)(3), (b) cause the Corporation to lose its tax-exempt status, or (c) constitute an excess-benefit transaction under Section 4958 of the Code.

---

## ARTICLE VIII. DISSOLUTION

**Section 1. Voluntary dissolution.** Upon the dissolution of the Corporation, after paying or making provision for the payment of all liabilities of the Corporation, the Board of Directors shall dispose of all of the assets of the Corporation **exclusively for the purposes of the Corporation** in such manner, or to such organization or organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, religious, or scientific purposes as shall at the time qualify as an exempt organization or organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code (or the corresponding provision of any future United States internal revenue law), as the Board of Directors shall determine.

**Section 2. Federal, state, or local government distribution.** Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by the Court of Chancery of the County in which the principal office of the Corporation is then located, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said Court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes, or to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose.

> *(This Article VIII is the dissolution clause required by Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(4). Delaware is not listed in Appendix B of the Instructions for Form 1023 (Rev. 12/2024), so an express clause is required.)*

## Filing notes

- **Filing fee:** $109 base + $50 certified copy (recommended) + $50 receipt + optional expedite (24-hour: $100; same-day: $200; 2-hour: $500; 1-hour: $1,000). Recommend **standard filing + certified copy** for ~$159–$209. Reference: Delaware Division of Corporations Fee Schedule (Aug 2024).
- **Filing channel:** Filed by the registered agent (Northwest) on engagement, or directly via DE Division of Corporations.
- **Post-filing:** Obtain conformed copy of the filed Certificate; convert to PDF for attachment to Form 1023; deliver to DC for foreign-entity registration.
- **Initial Annual Report:** Delaware exempt corporations file an Annual Report by March 1 each year; the franchise tax is $0 for an exempt corporation under § 391(j), but a $25 annual report fee applies (verify with the current DE fee schedule).
- **EIN:** Apply via IRS Form SS-4 (online for U.S.-residency applicants; mail/fax otherwise) **after** the Certificate is filed and a Delaware file number is assigned.
