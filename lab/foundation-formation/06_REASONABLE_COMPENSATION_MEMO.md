## 1. Why this matters

The founder's role as a compensated **employee of the Foundation** creates the highest private-inurement / private-benefit exposure in the Foundation's exemption application. Properly structured, the founder's salary is **fully permissible** under § 501(c)(3) and supported by Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(c)(2) ("the term 'inurement' . . . does not preclude the payment of reasonable compensation for services rendered"). Improperly structured, the same salary can (a) cost the Foundation its exemption (private inurement), (b) trigger intermediate sanctions on the founder (200% excise tax under § 4958(b)) and on participating board members (10%, capped at $20,000 per transaction, under § 4958(a)(2)), and (c) sink the Foundation's public reputation.

The founder's prompt directs that the founder's compensation be structured as **"employee-of-the-Foundation under § 4958 reasonable-comp, not founder-of-LLC"** — i.e., a fixed W-2 salary set under the rebuttable-presumption procedure, not founder distributions, equity, or a revenue share. This memo describes the procedure.

## 2. Legal framework

### 2.1. § 501(c)(3) private inurement

Under Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(c)(2), no part of an exempt organization's net earnings may inure to the benefit of any private shareholder or individual. **Payment of reasonable compensation for services actually rendered is not inurement.** Excessive compensation is inurement and is grounds for loss of exempt status.

### 2.2. § 4958 excess-benefit transactions

§ 4958 imposes excise taxes on:

- A "disqualified person" (any individual in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the organization at any time in the prior 5 years — clearly including the founder/ED) who receives an excess benefit; and
- Organization managers (directors, officers) who knowingly participate in approving an excess-benefit transaction.

An excess-benefit transaction is one in which the value of the economic benefit provided to a disqualified person exceeds the value of consideration (including services) received in exchange.

### 2.3. Rebuttable presumption — Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6

Compensation paid to a disqualified person is **presumed reasonable** (and the burden of proving excess shifts to the IRS) if:

(a) the compensation is approved in advance by an authorized body of the organization composed entirely of individuals who do not have a conflict of interest with respect to the transaction;

(b) the authorized body obtained and relied upon appropriate **comparability data** before making its determination; and

(c) the authorized body adequately documented the basis for its determination concurrently with making that determination.

## 3. Implementation for the Foundation

### 3.1. Authorized body without conflicts (prong (a))

- The **Compensation Committee** (Bylaws Art. IV § 11(b)) is composed entirely of independent directors with no compensation arrangement with the Foundation and no related-party relationship with the founder.
- The founder shall not be a member of the Compensation Committee, shall not attend the portion of any meeting addressing his own compensation, and shall not vote on any matter relating to his own compensation.
- If the Compensation Committee has fewer than three members, the full Board (excluding the founder and any other compensated officers) shall act.

### 3.2. Comparability data (prong (b))

The Compensation Committee shall obtain and rely on:

1. **Compensation surveys for similar-mission, similar-budget tax-exempt organizations**:
   - GuideStar Compensation Report (or Candid equivalent)
   - Council on Foundations Grantmakers Salary and Benefits Report (for grantmaking peers)
   - Nonprofit Quarterly / The NonProfit Times annual surveys
   - Indeed / Glassdoor / Levels.fyi data for technical-leadership roles at comparable nonprofits and standards bodies
2. **Form 990 Schedule J compensation data** for ~10 peer 501(c)(3) organizations operating in cryptography, AI safety, technology policy, and open-source standards. Suggested peer set:
   - Mozilla Foundation
   - The Linux Foundation (and selected subprojects/standards bodies, e.g., CNCF)
   - OpenAI's nonprofit predecessor (historical 990s)
   - The Open Source Initiative
   - Internet Society / IETF Administration
   - Electronic Frontier Foundation
   - Center for AI Safety (CAIS)
   - The Future Society (FoST)
   - Apache Software Foundation
   - National Center for Women & IT (for compensation comparators)
   - Filter to: Executive Director / CEO compensation; total compensation including base, bonus, and benefits.
3. **Independent compensation-consultant report**, if the Board determines the in-house comparability set is insufficient. Recommended for the first comp-setting and every third year thereafter.
4. **Geographic adjustment** for Washington, DC (high cost of living; ~10–15% premium over national).

### 3.3. Contemporaneous documentation (prong (c))

Minutes of the Compensation Committee meeting setting the founder's compensation must:

(a) be prepared and approved **within 60 days** of the meeting or by the time of the next meeting, whichever is earlier;

(b) identify by name each director who voted, and the result of the vote;

(c) recite the comparability data considered, with copies of the underlying reports or screenshots retained in the Foundation's records;

(d) state the terms approved (base salary, benefits, any bonus, employment-agreement terms);

(e) record any deliberation about alternatives, including lower compensation, and the rationale for the level chosen;

(f) confirm that the founder was not present for the deliberation or vote.

### 3.4. Structure of the founder's compensation

Per the founder's direction and consistent with the rebuttable-presumption procedure, the founder's compensation **shall be**:

- **Fully fixed salary** (W-2 employee); no incentive comp, no bonus tied to fundraising, no revenue share, no royalty on Foundation IP, no equity in any related entity in lieu of cash;
- **Set in advance** for the upcoming fiscal year (not retrospectively);
- **Reviewed annually** by the Compensation Committee under the same procedure;
- **Subject to no automatic escalators** beyond a standard cost-of-living adjustment, which itself must be approved annually;
- **Documented in a written employment agreement** between the Foundation and the founder, signed on behalf of the Foundation by the Chair of the Compensation Committee (not by the founder); and
- **Capped at a level the Compensation Committee determines is at or below the median of comparable positions** in the comparability data, recognizing the founder's commitment in the prompt to keep his compensation "clean of private-inurement risk."

### 3.5. What founder compensation must **not** include

- **No founder distributions, dividends, or equity-equivalent rights.** The Foundation is nonstock; there is nothing to distribute.
- **No royalty or revenue share on Foundation IP.** All IP developed in the course of the founder's employment is the Foundation's, dedicated to the public under open-source licenses.
- **No personal use of Foundation funds, credit, or property** beyond reasonable business-expense reimbursement under a written Expense Reimbursement Policy.
- **No bonus tied to Foundation revenue, fundraising, or asset growth.** § 4958 specifically scrutinizes revenue-based compensation.
- **No loans from the Foundation to the founder.** Prohibited outright by the Bylaws (Art. VI § 3).
- **No deferred compensation tied to revenue triggers.** Any deferred-comp arrangement must be a § 457(b) (eligible) plan or § 457(f) plan reviewed by counsel; both require careful documentation.
- **No founder use of Foundation grant or sponsorship to benefit founder's for-profit AAO Network entities.**

## 4. Public-charity classification considerations

Founder compensation at any level the public could read as excessive will not only invite § 4958 sanctions; it will also threaten the Foundation's **public-support test** under § 509(a)(1)/170(b)(1)(A)(vi), because the resulting public-relations damage will deter the broad-based donor support the Foundation needs to clear 33⅓%.

The Compensation Committee should weigh **optics** as well as legal sufficiency.

## 5. Filings

- **Form 990 Part VII** and (above thresholds) **Schedule J** publicly disclose officer/director compensation.
- **Form 1023 Part V** requires the Foundation to describe its compensation-setting procedure.
- The founder's compensation will be visible to donors, regulators, and the press starting with the first Form 990 filing.
