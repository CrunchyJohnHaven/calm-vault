# BYLAWS OF AAO FOUNDATION, INC.

A Delaware Nonstock Corporation

## ARTICLE I. NAME AND OFFICES

**Section 1. Name.** The name of the corporation is AAO Foundation, Inc. (the "Corporation").

**Section 2. Principal office.** The principal office of the Corporation shall be 1480 Chapin St NW, Washington, DC 20009, or such other location as the Board of Directors may from time to time determine.

**Section 3. Registered office.** The registered office of the Corporation in the State of Delaware is as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation, or as may be changed from time to time pursuant to applicable law.

---

## ARTICLE II. PURPOSES AND TAX-EXEMPT STATUS

**Section 1. Purposes.** The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), as set forth in Article III of the Certificate of Incorporation.

**Section 2. Tax-exempt status.** The Corporation shall conduct its activities so as to qualify as a tax-exempt organization under § 501(c)(3) of the Code and as a publicly supported organization described in § 509(a)(1) and § 170(b)(1)(A)(vi) of the Code, and shall not engage in any activity inconsistent with that status.

**Section 3. No private inurement; no private benefit.** No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation may pay reasonable compensation for services actually rendered and make payments and distributions in furtherance of its exempt purposes. The Corporation shall not be operated for the private benefit of any individual, the founder, or any related entity.

**Section 4. No political campaign intervention; limited lobbying.** The Corporation shall not participate or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office. The Corporation shall not engage in lobbying activities to a substantial degree within the meaning of § 501(c)(3). The Corporation shall make a **§ 501(h) election** by filing Form 5768 in its first taxable year and shall limit lobbying expenditures to the limits of § 4911(c) (or such lower limit as the Board adopts).

---

## ARTICLE III. MEMBERSHIP

The Corporation shall have **no members**. The rights that would otherwise be vested in members are vested in the Board of Directors.

---

## ARTICLE IV. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**Section 1. Powers.** The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by, or under the direction of, the Board of Directors.

**Section 2. Number.** The Board of Directors shall consist of not fewer than three (3) nor more than nine (9) directors. The exact number shall be fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board. The initial number of directors is [three (3) / five (5) — finalize at adoption].

**Section 3. Independent-director majority.** A majority of the directors at all times shall be **independent directors**. A director is independent if the director (i) is not employed by, and does not receive compensation from, the Corporation other than for service as a director (and reimbursement of expenses), and (ii) is not related to the founder or to any compensated officer of the Corporation by blood, marriage, or domestic partnership, or by employment, business, or financial relationship that the Board determines could compromise independence.

**Section 4. Term.** Each director shall serve for a term of three (3) years and until a successor is elected and qualified, except that the initial directors shall serve until the first annual meeting and until their successors are elected and qualified. Directors may be reelected.

**Section 5. Vacancies.** A vacancy on the Board (including by reason of an increase in the authorized number of directors) shall be filled by a vote of a majority of the directors then in office, though less than a quorum, and the director so elected shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired term.

**Section 6. Resignation.** A director may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the Board chair or to the Secretary of the Corporation. The resignation is effective when delivered unless it specifies a later effective date.

**Section 7. Removal.** A director may be removed, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the directors then in office (excluding the director proposed to be removed).

**Section 8. Compensation.** Directors shall not receive compensation for their service as directors during the Corporation's first three (3) full taxable years. Thereafter, the Board may, by resolution of a majority of independent directors not receiving such compensation, authorize reasonable compensation for service as a director, provided the compensation is established under the rebuttable-presumption procedure of Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6.

**Section 9. Meetings.**

(a) *Regular meetings.* Regular meetings of the Board shall be held at least quarterly, on such dates and at such times as the Board may designate.

(b) *Annual meeting.* The annual meeting of the Board shall be held in the first quarter of each calendar year.

(c) *Special meetings.* Special meetings may be called by the Chair, the Executive Director, or any two (2) directors, on not less than 48 hours' written notice to each director (which may be by email).

(d) *Notice and waiver.* Notice of regular meetings shall be given at least seven (7) days in advance by email or other written communication. Notice may be waived in writing before or after the meeting, and attendance at a meeting (other than for the express purpose of objecting to lack of notice) constitutes waiver.

(e) *Quorum.* A majority of the directors then in office shall constitute a quorum. Action by the Board requires the affirmative vote of a majority of the directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present, except as otherwise required by these Bylaws or applicable law.

(f) *Action without a meeting.* Action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting if all members of the Board consent thereto in writing or by electronic transmission, in accordance with 8 Del. C. § 141(f).

(g) *Remote participation.* Directors may participate in a meeting by means of conference telephone or other communication equipment by means of which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other; such participation constitutes presence in person.

**Section 10. Chair and Vice Chair.** The Board shall annually elect a Chair from among the independent directors. The Board may elect a Vice Chair. The Chair shall preside at meetings of the Board.

**Section 11. Committees.** The Board may, by resolution adopted by a majority of the directors then in office, designate one or more committees, each consisting of two (2) or more directors. Standing committees shall include at minimum:

(a) **Audit Committee** — composed entirely of independent directors; reviews the Corporation's financial statements and recommends the independent auditor.

(b) **Compensation Committee** — composed entirely of independent directors; reviews and approves compensation of the Executive Director and other "disqualified persons" within the meaning of § 4958, and oversees the rebuttable-presumption procedure (see Article VII).

(c) **Bounty Pool Review Committee** — reviews proposed bounty-pool payouts; recommends Board action on payouts above thresholds set in the Bounty Pool Policy.

The Board may delegate to a committee any authority of the Board permitted under 8 Del. C. § 141(c).

## ARTICLE V. OFFICERS

**Section 1. Officers.** The officers of the Corporation shall be a President (who may also serve as Executive Director), a Secretary, and a Treasurer. The Board may elect additional officers (e.g., Vice President, Assistant Secretary, Assistant Treasurer). One person may hold more than one office, except that the same person shall not be both President and Secretary.

**Section 2. Election and term.** Officers shall be elected annually by the Board at its annual meeting. Each officer shall serve until the next annual meeting and until a successor is elected and qualified, or until the officer's earlier resignation, removal, or death.

**Section 3. Duties.**

(a) *President / Executive Director.* The President shall serve as chief executive officer and shall have general supervision of the business and affairs of the Corporation, subject to the direction of the Board.

(b) *Treasurer.* The Treasurer shall have custody of the funds and financial records of the Corporation and shall ensure the maintenance of accurate accounting records, the timely filing of all required tax and information returns, and the preparation of financial statements.

(c) *Secretary.* The Secretary shall keep the minutes of meetings of the Board and committees, shall maintain corporate records (including the membership of the Board), shall give notices of meetings as required, and shall serve as custodian of the Corporation's records.

**Section 4. Removal.** Any officer may be removed by the Board with or without cause.

---

## ARTICLE VI. COMPENSATION AND FINANCIAL CONTROLS

**Section 1. Compensation Policy.** The Board shall adopt a written Compensation Policy implementing the rebuttable-presumption procedure of Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6. The policy shall require that compensation of the Executive Director and of any other "disqualified person" within the meaning of IRC § 4958 be:

(a) approved in advance by the Compensation Committee (or the full Board acting through directors who are not themselves disqualified persons with respect to the transaction);

(b) based on appropriate **comparability data** (e.g., compensation surveys for similar-mission, similar-budget tax-exempt organizations, Form 990 Schedule J data for peer organizations, independent compensation consultant reports);

(c) **documented contemporaneously** in minutes prepared and approved within a reasonable time, identifying the directors who voted, the data relied upon, and the terms of approval.

**Section 2. Founder compensation.** The Corporation may compensate the founder, John Bradley, for service as Executive Director / President under an at-will employment agreement on terms approved under the Compensation Policy and Conflict of Interest Policy. **The founder's compensation shall be a fully fixed salary**, without bonus, incentive compensation, deferred compensation tied to revenue or asset growth, royalty, or any other variable element, except that the Board may, with respect to fiscal years after the third full taxable year, approve a fixed-amount bonus under the rebuttable-presumption procedure. The founder shall recuse from all Board and committee deliberations relating to the founder's own compensation.

**Section 3. No loans to insiders.** The Corporation shall not make loans to, guarantee the personal obligations of, or extend credit to any director, officer, or key employee of the Corporation.

**Section 4. No private inurement.** All distributions, transfers, payments, and other transactions involving Corporation assets shall be made exclusively in furtherance of the Corporation's exempt purposes and shall not inure to the benefit of any private individual.

**Section 5. Banking and signing authority.** The Board shall designate by resolution one or more financial institutions, the signatories on Corporation accounts, and the dollar thresholds above which dual-signatory or board-approved authorization is required.

**Section 6. Annual budget.** The Board shall adopt an annual budget at or before its annual meeting and shall review actual financial performance against the budget at least quarterly.

**Section 7. Independent review.** The Audit Committee shall engage an independent CPA to perform an annual financial review (compilation, review, or audit depending on revenue level and applicable state-charity-registration thresholds) and shall report findings to the Board.

The Conflict of Interest Policy is attached to these Bylaws and is incorporated by reference. Each director, officer, and key employee shall annually sign a disclosure form acknowledging the policy and disclosing relevant relationships, and the Secretary shall maintain those disclosures.

## ARTICLE VIII. DOCUMENT RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION

The Board shall adopt and maintain a written Document Retention and Destruction Policy specifying the periods during which categories of records (financial, governance, contracts, personnel, donor records) must be retained and providing for orderly destruction or transfer thereafter. The policy shall require preservation of all records relevant to any pending or threatened litigation, investigation, or audit.

---

## ARTICLE IX. WHISTLEBLOWER POLICY

The Board shall adopt and maintain a written Whistleblower Policy under which any director, officer, employee, contractor, or volunteer may report, in good faith, suspected illegal or unethical conduct, fraud, violation of the Conflict of Interest Policy, or violation of these Bylaws, without retaliation. Reports may be made anonymously to the Chair of the Audit Committee.

---

## ARTICLE X. INDEMNIFICATION AND INSURANCE

The Corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, and other agents to the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law (8 Del. C. § 145) and the Certificate of Incorporation. The Corporation shall maintain Directors and Officers (D&O) liability insurance and general-liability insurance in amounts approved by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE XI. FISCAL YEAR; ANNUAL FILINGS

**Section 1. Fiscal year.** The fiscal year of the Corporation shall end on December 31, unless changed by the Board.

**Section 2. Federal filings.** The Treasurer (with assistance of the Corporation's outside accountant) shall ensure timely filing of Form 990 (or 990-EZ or 990-N as applicable), Form 990-T (if applicable), and Form 5768 (in the first taxable year, to elect § 501(h)).

**Section 3. State filings.** The Treasurer shall ensure timely filing of all state charitable-solicitation registrations and renewals, beginning with the District of Columbia Basic Business License — Charitable Services (see [`02_DC_CRA1_CHARITABLE_REGISTRATION_DRAFT.md`](./02_DC_CRA1_CHARITABLE_REGISTRATION_DRAFT.md)), and including the Delaware Annual Report.

---

## ARTICLE XII. DISSOLUTION

Dissolution of the Corporation is governed by Article VIII of the Certificate of Incorporation, which is incorporated herein by reference. Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed exclusively for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of § 501(c)(3) of the Code, or to the federal government, a state government, or a local government for a public purpose, as provided in Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(4) and Article VIII of the Certificate of Incorporation.

---

## ARTICLE XIII. AMENDMENT

These Bylaws may be amended or repealed, and new Bylaws adopted, by the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the directors then in office at any meeting of the Board, provided that written notice of the proposed amendment is given at least seven (7) days in advance of the meeting. No amendment shall be made that would cause the Corporation to lose its qualification as an organization described in § 501(c)(3) of the Code.
