See Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(1)(i), -1(b)(4).

**Narrative (draft):**

**A. Background and exempt purpose.**

AAO Foundation, Inc. ("the Foundation") is a Delaware nonstock corporation organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes within the meaning of IRC § 501(c)(3). The Foundation's exempt purposes are:

1. **Advancement of science** within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(5), through the conduct, sponsorship, and free public dissemination of original cryptographic research relating to the governance of autonomous artificial-intelligence agents — specifically, the Bradley-Gavini protocol composing Pedersen commitments, Schnorr-group equality proofs, and the Fiat-Shamir transform into a system permitting two autonomous AI agents ("AAOs") to verify their primary directives align without disclosing them. This research is published and maintained on an open-source basis under the MIT or Apache 2.0 license, with no proprietary or commercial restriction on use.

2. **Lessening the burdens of government** within the meaning of *Rev. Rul. 85-1*, 1985-1 C.B. 177, and IRS analyses surveyed in the IRS Exempt Organizations CPE Text "Lessening the Burdens of Government" (Louthian & Henchey), through the development and maintenance of the **AAO-Governed certification standard** — a publicly available open standard that federal, state, and foreign governments may adopt or reference when promulgating regulations addressing autonomous-AI-agent safety, attestation, and accountability. The Foundation will (i) consult with federal agencies (including but not limited to NIST, the AI Safety Institute, the FTC, and Congressional staff) on the standard's content; (ii) make all standard materials, test vectors, and reference implementations freely available; and (iii) operate a **public bounty pool** funding independent third-party verification of AAO-Governed claims, on the model of bug-bounty programs operated by government and industry.

3. **Educational purposes** within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(3), through publication of papers, manuals, explainers, and curricular materials relating to (1) and (2); through speaking engagements, academic-conference presentations, and university teaching; and through the maintenance of free, publicly accessible documentation on the Foundation's website.

## Part VII. Foundation Classification

> The Foundation seeks classification as a **public charity**, not a private foundation. The basis for public-charity classification will be one of:
>
> - **§ 509(a)(1) / § 170(b)(1)(A)(vi)** — "publicly supported" via the 33⅓% public support test, or 10% facts-and-circumstances; OR
> - **§ 509(a)(2)** — publicly supported via gross-receipts test (1/3 from public sources, less than 1/3 from investment + UBI).
>
> Given the Foundation's planned funding mix (broad-based individual contributions + diversified foundation grants, with no fees for services), **§ 509(a)(1) / 170(b)(1)(A)(vi)** is the appropriate primary classification. Counsel must confirm and elect on Form 1023 Part VII at filing.
