## CRITICAL CORRECTIONS to the original ideation prompt

The prompt referenced "TEAS Plus" at "$250/class" and a total of "~$750" in fees. **Both are out of date.**

### 1. TEAS Plus no longer exists

Effective **January 18, 2025**, USPTO eliminated the TEAS Plus / TEAS Standard tiers and replaced them with a **single base application fee** of **$350 per class**. There is now only one application path, filed through **Trademark Center** (the successor to TEAS) at **https://trademarkcenter.uspto.gov/**.

Source: [USPTO — Summary of 2025 trademark fee changes](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/fees-payment-information/summary-2025-trademark-fee-changes).

You will owe an additional **$150/class Statement of Use (SOU) fee** when you eventually convert each ITU filing to actual-use registration (must occur within 36 months of Notice of Allowance, in 6-month increments at $125/extension/class).

If a goods/services description has to deviate from the ID Manual, USPTO will add a **$200/class** "free-form text" surcharge and a **$100/class** "insufficient information" surcharge if examiner deems the application incomplete. All three drafts here use **only approved ID Manual entries** to avoid these surcharges.

### 3. Specimen for sameasyou.ai/certified

The prompt named this as the "specimen." **Specimens are not required at ITU filing.** They are only required when you later file the Statement of Use (or an Amendment to Allege Use). Hold the specimen — keep an archived copy of the live `sameasyou.ai/certified` page and any service-mark usage in commerce — and file it with the SOU after the Notice of Allowance issues.

For an ITU §1(b) application, the filing requires a **bona fide intent to use** in commerce. Keep contemporaneous evidence of that intent (development plans, business documents, the spec doc at `AAO_CERTIFIED_SPEC.md`, etc.) in case of an Office Action challenge.
