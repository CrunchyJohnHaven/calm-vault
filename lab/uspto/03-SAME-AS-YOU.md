- The launch announcement at `LAUNCH_GO_PACKET.md` / `LAUNCH_HN_SUBMISSION.md`

**Note on actual use:** The `sameasyou.ai` site appears to already be live as of the 2026-05-12 launch. If the mark is being used in commerce **now** to advertise software services, you may have a §1(a) **actual use** basis available, not just §1(b) ITU. An actual-use filing requires submitting a specimen at filing time and avoids the SOU process entirely. Confirm with a 5-minute review of the live site:

- Is the mark `SAME AS YOU` displayed on the site as a source-identifier for the services?
- Is the site offering the Class 042 services (software design/development) to the public?
- Has the site been continuously available for a period sufficient to constitute use in commerce?

If yes to all three, switch to §1(a) actual-use basis (saves $150 SOU fee + the 36-month deadline pressure). If unsure, default to §1(b) ITU as drafted.

## Section 11 — What to expect after filing

**Most likely scenarios:**

1. **Approved on first examination (40% probability):** Examiner accepts the mark as suggestive of alignment services, not merely descriptive. Publishes for opposition.
2. **§2(e)(1) descriptiveness refusal (35% probability):** Examiner argues "same as you" describes a feature (alignment with users). Response options:
   - Argue suggestiveness (one-step-removed) per *Abercrombie & Fitch v. Hunting World*
   - Argue mark is unitary and the meaning is non-literal in context
   - Amend to Supplemental Register
3. **§2(d) refusal citing a prior registration (15% probability):** Run TESS first to identify any blocker.
4. **§2(c) refusal — refers to a particular living individual (5% probability):** Unlikely; "Same as you" is a generic referent, not a specific person's name.
5. **Other (5%).**

Estimated probability of registration on Principal Register without Supplemental fallback: **~55%**. With Supplemental fallback: **~85%**.
