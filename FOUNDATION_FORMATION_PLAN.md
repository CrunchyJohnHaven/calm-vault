## 1. Why we're doing this now

The Money Python venture, and the AAO Network public materials, reference a Foundation entity as the 501(c)(3) donation vehicle. As of 2026-05-12, **no Foundation entity has been filed.** Two failure modes are active:

- **#18 Triple-Entity Vapor** (P = 70%, Heavy): the publicly-described three-entity architecture (for-profit LLC + Foundation + holding) does not exist. A press cycle, a regulator, or a donor that checks state filings will find a single LLC, not the Triple Entity.
- **#21 Invisible Wounds Foundation Doesn't Exist** (P = 75%, Heavy): donors who follow a "donate" link land on a vehicle that cannot issue a § 170(c)(2) charitable-contribution acknowledgment. Once anyone notices, donor trust collapses and the Network is exposed to false-advertising and consumer-protection exposure.

The mitigation is: **file the Foundation.** This plan does **not** file it — it produces a counsel-review draft package so counsel can be engaged immediately and the filings can begin without redrafting from scratch.

## 7. Verified anchor points

- **Form 1023 Rev. December 2024.** Source: https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1023.
- **Form 1023 must be filed electronically at Pay.gov.** Source: https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i1023.
- **Delaware exempt-corp incorporation = $109 base.** Source: DE Division of Corporations Fee Schedule (Aug 2024), `https://corpfiles.delaware.gov/AugustFee2024.pdf`.

- **Rev. Rul. 85-1, 1985-1 C.B. 177 — lessening the burdens of government**, cited per founder's direction.
- **IRC § 4958** — intermediate sanctions on excess-benefit transactions; **Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6** — rebuttable-presumption procedure for founder compensation.
