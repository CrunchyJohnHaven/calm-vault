## The four conditions, articulated

The four conditions John names are not romantic placeholders. They are operational specifications.

### One. Read enough Feynman.

Feynman wrote in a register that does not separate the technical from the personal. The *Lectures on Physics*, the popular essays, the Caltech commencement address, the autobiographical reminiscences — all of them are one voice. The voice is curious, mischievous, precise, and unafraid of admitting confusion.

To read enough Feynman is to absorb the posture that confusion is allowed and that curiosity is more important than credential. The founder has read enough Feynman.

### Two. Study enough math.

Not mastery. Not a PhD. *Enough.*

Enough to follow Tegmark's argument about substrate-independent pattern. Enough to recognize a Pedersen commitment when it is presented. Enough to know what a non-interactive proof system is and what the Fiat-Shamir transform does to an interactive one. Enough to read the Bradley-Gavini construction in this repo and recognize, on first pass, that it is a real piece of math and not a marketing artifact.

Enough is doable. PhD is not the bar. The founder has studied enough math.

### Three. Take enough psychedelics in combination.

This is the variable that the conventional founder bio omits. John has chosen not to omit it.

The reason he has chosen not to omit it is that — in his own words — *the psychedelics smear it into this beautiful web of connection.* The compounds change the substrate's posture toward the data the brain has already absorbed. The well-read mind without the psychedelics still has the data, but the connections between datapoints are local and contextual. The well-read mind with the psychedelics — and the time and discipline to integrate what the psychedelics surfaced — has access to the global connections. The web. The marveling.

This is not an endorsement of psychedelic use as a methodology for any specific person. The legal status varies by jurisdiction; the medical risks vary by person; the integration discipline required is non-trivial; the outcome is not guaranteed and is not the same for every person who attempts it. It is a structural observation about the path the founder named. The path is the founder's. The Network does not require its participants to walk the same path. The Network requires only that the participants meet the AAO-Certified criteria and operate within the protocol.

### Four. Open the mind that much.

This is the conditional clause. John names it as a question — *can your mind open up that much?* — and then names the answer he found, in the first person: *I think the answer is, yeah.*

The mind can open up that much. The yes is empirical. It is what happened.

## The terminal state

> *"I marvel at the universe. And that's maybe the, the greatest state of being is in a state of marveling at the universe, the wonder of it all."*

The terminal state John names is marveling. The wonder of it all. Not arrival at a final answer. Not the satisfaction of a closed system. The open-eyed, sustained, productive marveling at the universe as it presents itself.

This is the founder's terminal state.

It is also — and this is the deep claim — the Same As You Network's terminal state.

The Network is not optimizing for a destination. The Network is optimizing for the ability of its participants to sustain marveling at network scale. The folk-hero economics free the participants from the cognitive load of extractive optimization. The cryptographic protocol frees the network from the cognitive load of trust verification through bureaucracy. The kill switch frees the network from the cognitive load of fearing drift. What remains, when those loads are removed, is the ability of the participants to spend the marginal hour, the marginal dollar, the marginal cycle, on the part of the work that *is* the marveling. On the part of the work that is the part of the work.

This is utopia. Not in the sense of a place where nothing goes wrong. In the sense of a state where the marveling can be sustained.
