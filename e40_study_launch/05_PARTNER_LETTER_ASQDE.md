Dear ASQDE Board members,

I am writing to request the Society's assistance — or, if the Board prefers, a referral to interested ASQDE-credentialed examiners — for a defensive-security research study on the robustness of behavioral-biometric identity verification.

I want to be upfront about an inherent sensitivity: we are asking professional forensic document examiners to produce *skilled-forger samples* of consenting volunteers' handwriting. We understand this is unusual, and we have designed the request carefully. Please read on; I will explain the purpose, the safeguards, and why we believe ASQDE examiners are the right professional community to involve.

## What we are doing and why

The Calm Witness protocol is a behavioral-biometric proof-of-personhood system. Among its many design components is a handwriting-distance function that compares a fresh sample against an enrolled baseline. We have an empirical study (Everest 40, the FAR/FRR characterization study) that measures performance against *unrelated* impostors. We have a sibling study (Everest 41, adversarial robustness) that must additionally measure performance against *skilled* impostors — that is, people who have deliberately practiced imitating a target principal's handwriting.

Without the skilled-impostor measurement, our published performance numbers would systematically overstate the system's real-world robustness. We do not want to ship a system whose public benchmarks are misleading. The skilled-imitator measurement is therefore a research-integrity requirement, not an optional extension.

The defensive purpose is unambiguous: we are stress-testing our own system to find out where it breaks before it ships. We are not exploiting anyone, not building a forgery tool, and not producing samples that will ever be used outside this research context.

## Why ASQDE

We considered (and rejected) several alternatives:
- **Untrained imitators recruited from the general public.** Insufficiently skilled; would understate the threat from a competent adversary.
- **Machine-learning-generated forgeries.** A different attack vector (handled in our stroke-replay and synthetic-stroke analyses); not a substitute for human skilled-imitator data, because the kinematic micro-features generated by a trained human's motor system differ qualitatively from generative-model output.
- **Researchers internal to our project.** Conflicts of interest; insufficient skill ceiling.

ASQDE-credentialed examiners are the appropriate professional community because (a) you have the relevant motor skill, (b) you have established professional ethical codes that include confidentiality and non-misuse of materials, and (c) you have a credentialing process that provides accountability.

## What we are asking

We are asking for **3-5 ASQDE-credentialed examiners** willing to participate, each producing **5-10 imitation samples** of handwriting from each of **5-8 consenting study principals**. Specifically:

1. Study principals who opt in (separately from main-study consent) authorize the use of their enrollment handwriting samples for skilled-imitator practice.
2. Selected ASQDE examiners receive these samples through a secure, audited channel.
3. Examiners spend up to 6-8 weeks of practice time per principal (consistent with the published literature on skilled-imitator training).
4. Examiners produce 5-10 imitation samples per principal, in the same prompt format as the original enrollment.
5. Samples are returned through the same secure channel and submitted as adversarial trials in our Everest 41 analysis.

**All practice materials and imitation samples are destroyed** at the conclusion of the study, with cryptographic deletion confirmation provided to the Society.

## Ethical safeguards

- **No principal's identity is disclosed** to the examiner. Samples are pseudonymized; the examiner sees handwriting samples and the prompt text, never names, ages, contact information, or any context.
- **Study principals opt in separately and explicitly.** Adversarial-sample use is *not* bundled with main-study consent; it is a separately-checked authorization on the consent form (`02_INFORMED_CONSENT_TEMPLATE.md` in this packet). Principals who decline this use can still participate in the main study.
- **Examiners sign a Data Processing Agreement** binding them to: use samples only for the contracted research purpose; no retention beyond the study; no sharing with any third party; no use in any non-research context (forensic case work, training, demonstration). Violations are reportable to the ASQDE professional ethics committee.
- **The imitation samples never leave the research pipeline.** They are submitted as inputs to the Calm Witness verification function, the distance scores are computed, and the samples are destroyed. They never go back into the wild.
- **IRB oversight.** The study is conducted under a partnering university's IRB (currently negotiating with École Polytechnique de Montréal as primary site). The IRB protocol explicitly covers this adversarial-sample arm.
- **Publication transparency.** When we publish the resulting FAR-under-skilled-imitator measurement, the methodology — including the ASQDE collaboration and its safeguards — will be disclosed in full.
