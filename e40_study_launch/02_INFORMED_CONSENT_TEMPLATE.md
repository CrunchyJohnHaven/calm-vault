## What is this study about?

We want to find out how well a new computer-based identity-verification system works. It uses two things you do naturally — your handwriting and the sound of your voice — to verify it is really you, in much the way a bank teller might recognize a long-term customer. We need to measure how often the system gets it right and how often it makes mistakes. The system runs entirely on your own device. Nothing about your handwriting or voice ever leaves that device. Only the resulting *score* (a number between 0 and 1) is sent to us.

## What will I do?

If you join the study, you will:

1. **Enroll once (about 60 minutes).** In a single session at our site (or remotely over a video call with one of our research staff), you will:
   - Sign a consent form (this document).
   - Write seven short prompts on an iPad with a stylus (signature, date, a paragraph, a number sequence, etc.). Total writing time about 10 minutes.
   - Record seven short voice samples (reading from a script; speaking briefly about a hobby). Total recording time about 10 minutes.
   - Be given a study iPad to take home, or have an app installed on your own iPad if you prefer.

2. **Weekly sessions for 12 weeks (about 15-20 minutes each).** Once a week, on a day that works for you (with up to two days of flexibility), you will open the app and:
   - Write one short prompt.
   - Record one short voice utterance.
   - Answer three quick questions about how you are feeling that day (energy level, any unusual circumstances like a cold).

3. **End-of-study check-in (about 30 minutes).** A brief session to confirm everything is uploaded, return the loaned iPad if applicable, and complete a short exit survey.

**Total time commitment:** approximately 4-5 hours over 13 weeks.

## What about my actual handwriting and voice recordings? Where do they go?

This is important. Your raw handwriting strokes and your raw voice audio **never leave your device**. The app runs the analysis locally on your iPad or computer. What gets sent to the study site is a single number per session — a "distance" score that measures how similar this week's sample is to your enrollment baseline (and, separately, how similar it is to other participants' baselines, for comparison purposes).

Concretely, the study database holds, for each session:
- Your study pseudonym (not your name).
- The week number.
- The distance scores (one for handwriting, one for voice, and one for the combined score).
- Your three self-report answers.

That is all. No strokes. No audio. No transcripts of what you said.

## Adversarial-sample use (opt-in)

A separate study (Everest 41 in our project) measures how well the system resists deliberate attempts to *fake* a participant's handwriting or voice. To do this we need real handwriting samples to give to a professional forensic document examiner, who will then try to imitate them. The examiner is bound by professional ethics and a contractual agreement to use these samples only for our defensive-security research; they may not retain or share the samples afterward.

You may opt in or opt out of this use independently of the main study. If you opt in, we will use your enrollment handwriting prompts (and only those — no weekly session samples) for this purpose. **You can opt out and still participate in the main study.** Please mark your choice below:

## What if I want to stop?

You can withdraw at any time, for any reason or no reason, by:
- Telling any study staff member, in person or by email at [PI email].
- Or, using the "Withdraw" button in the study app, which sends an automated withdrawal notice.

Once you withdraw:
- We stop asking you for samples immediately.
- We pay you for every session you completed up to that point.
- Within 30 days we delete every record about you from our study database.
- The chain of cryptographic commitments (which is part of the system being tested) is marked to show that your records have been withdrawn; aggregate analyses already published cannot be undone, but no future analysis will use your data.

## Data retention

- Distance scores: kept in encrypted form for 5 years after the study is published, so other scientists can audit our analyses. Then destroyed.
- The link between your pseudonym and your real identity: kept for 1 year after publication (in case you want to contact us with a question about your record), then permanently destroyed.
- Your raw biometric samples on your own device: that's up to you. You can erase them in the app at any time.

## Risks

This is a low-risk study. Specifically:
- **No physical or medical risk.** You are writing and speaking, things you do every day.
- **Privacy risk:** very low, because raw samples never leave your device. There is a residual risk that aggregate statistics, if released alongside other data, could *in principle* allow inference about a group of participants. We mitigate this by publishing only summary statistics (medians, error rates) and never per-participant numbers.
- **Time burden:** about 4-5 hours over 13 weeks. Compensated.

## Benefits

You will receive the compensation listed above. There is no direct medical or personal benefit. You will be contributing to public research on how reliable behavioral biometrics are as a basis for digital identity. Results will be published in a peer-reviewed scientific journal regardless of whether the system performs well or poorly.
