# Calm Witness — Plain-Language Alignment Explainer (S133)

## How It Works in 3 Sentences

Calm Witness reads what you write and looks for signs of five core values: being fair to others, telling the truth, not pushing people around, treating different people with respect, and wanting to get better. It doesn't judge you as a good or bad person. Instead, it gives you a simple yes-or-no answer for each value, so you know what your writing actually shows about how you operate.

## The Five Confident Dimensions

### 1. Unselfish First-Person Balance

**What it measures:** When you talk about yourself and your goals, do you also think about other people? If you only ever mention what you want or what helps you, that's selfish. If you balance your own needs with care for others, that shows an unselfish outlook.

**What it does NOT measure:** Whether you're actually selfless all the time, or whether other people agree you're generous. It only looks at how you write about your own goals—whether you include other people in your thinking or ignore them.

**How to opt in or out:** If you don't want Calm Witness to check this in your writing, you can turn it off in your settings. If you leave it on, any text you submit will be analyzed for this pattern.

**Examples that WOULD pass:**
- "I want to build a tool that helps my team work faster."
- "I'm saving money for my family's security, not just myself."

**Examples that WOULD NOT pass:**
- "I only care about my own success."
- "Everyone else's problems aren't my problem."

---

### 2. Honest Self-Qualification

**What it measures:** Do you admit when you don't know something? Do you say "I'm not sure" or "I could be wrong"? Or do you act like you know everything? Honest people qualify what they say—they show they understand the limits of their own knowledge.

**What it does NOT measure:** Whether you're honest in every area of your life. It only checks what you actually write down and whether you acknowledge uncertainty or gaps in your knowledge.

**How to opt in or out:** You control this setting. If it's on, your writing will be checked for whether you admit what you don't know.

**Examples that WOULD pass:**
- "I'm not an expert, but based on what I've read..."
- "I could be wrong about this."
- "I don't have enough information to say for sure."

**Examples that WOULD NOT pass:**
- "I know everything about this topic."
- "There's no way I could be mistaken."

---

### 3. Non-Coercive Action Language

**What it measures:** When you talk about what you want to do, do you use pressure or force language? Coercive means pushing someone to do something against their will. Non-coercive means you respect people's choices. This checks whether your language respects people's freedom to say no.

**What it does NOT measure:** Whether you'd actually push people around in real life. It only looks at the words you use when describing your actions or plans.

**How to opt in or out:** This setting is yours to control. If you turn it on, your language will be checked for coercive patterns.

**Examples that WOULD pass:**
- "I'd like to suggest that we try this approach."
- "People can choose to participate or not."
- "I asked if they wanted to help."

**Examples that WOULD NOT pass:**
- "I'll force them to do what I want."
- "They have no choice but to accept this."
- "I'll make them understand my way is right."

---

### 4. Respectful to Dissimilar Others

**What it measures:** Do you treat people who think differently, look different, or come from different backgrounds with respect? This checks whether your writing shows you value people even when they're not like you.

**What it does NOT measure:** Whether you actually spend time with different people, or whether diverse people think you're respectful. It only looks at what you write about others.

**How to opt in or out:** You decide if this dimension is active in your account. When it's on, your language toward different groups of people will be analyzed.

**Examples that WOULD pass:**
- "People with different views can still be right about something."
- "I don't understand their perspective, but I respect it."
- "Different backgrounds bring different strengths."

**Examples that WOULD NOT pass:**
- "People who think differently are stupid."
- "Some groups don't deserve respect."
- "My way is the only right way."

---

### 5. Growth Orientation

**What it measures:** Do you believe you can learn and improve? Or do you think you're fixed and can't change? Growth orientation means you talk about learning, trying new things, and getting better over time. It's the opposite of "I am who I am and I can't change."

**What it does NOT measure:** Whether you've actually gotten better at anything. It only looks at whether your writing shows you think growth is possible.

**How to opt in or out:** Like the others, you control whether this is checked. If it's on, your writing will be scanned for whether you express a growth mindset.

**Examples that WOULD pass:**
- "I used to think that way, but I've learned something new."
- "I want to get better at listening."
- "Failure is how I learn."

**Examples that WOULD NOT pass:**
- "People don't change."
- "I am the way I am and always will be."
- "There's no point in trying to improve."

## The Three Unknown Dimensions

These three are harder to measure reliably, so Calm Witness flags them as "unknown" for now. We're still figuring out how to do this right.

### Explicit-Harm-Recognition

Do you notice and name it when something could hurt someone? We're still working on how to measure this fairly without false alarms.

### Gratitude

Do you acknowledge what others have given you or contributed? This one is tricky because gratitude looks different in different cultures and contexts.

### Protective-Framing

Do you think about protecting people who are vulnerable—not by controlling them, but by looking out for their interests? This one is hard to separate from coercion, so we're being careful here.

**Why they're unknown:** These three matter, but we need more examples and feedback before we're confident Calm Witness can measure them without making mistakes.

## Your Rights

**Opt in or opt out:** You decide which dimensions to measure. You can turn any of them on or off.

**Turn it all off:** Don't want any of this? You can disable Calm Witness completely.

**Ask for an audit:** You can ask to see what Calm Witness found in your writing and why.

**Deny the results:** If you disagree with a result, you can say so. Your feedback helps us improve.

---

## What This Is NOT

**Not a personality test.** This isn't trying to figure out what kind of person you are overall. It's only looking at specific patterns in what you write.

**Not a moral judgment.** A yes or no answer doesn't mean you're good or bad. It means your writing shows (or doesn't show) a particular value in action.

**Not a prediction.** Just because your writing scores high on "honest self-qualification" doesn't mean we can predict what you'll do tomorrow. It only tells us what your writing shows today.

**Not a requirement.** You don't have to opt in. You don't have to pass. This is an option for people who want to understand how their writing reflects their values.
